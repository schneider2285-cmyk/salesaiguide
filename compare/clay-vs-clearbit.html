Multi-Source Enrichment vs Native HubSpot Data

Sales and marketing teams rely on accurate contact and company data to run effective outreach, but how that data gets into your systems varies widely between these two tools. Clay and Clearbit represent fundamentally different approaches to the enrichment problem: one is an open platform that aggregates data from dozens of independent providers, and the other is a proprietary data layer woven directly into HubSpot's CRM.

This distinction matters because your choice affects not just data quality, but how your team operates day-to-day. If your tech stack spans multiple CRMs, outreach tools, and databases, you need enrichment that can feed all of them. If your organization has standardized on HubSpot and wants enrichment that works without any configuration, the calculus is different. The core question is: do you need flexible data sourcing that works across your entire stack, or native enrichment built into HubSpot that runs automatically on every new record?

Key Differences That Matter

Data sourcing model. Clay connects to 75+ independent data providers and lets you build custom waterfall sequences that check one source, then fall back to another, then another, until you get a match. This means you can combine firmographic data from one vendor with technographic signals from another and contact details from a third. Clearbit, by contrast, relies on its own proprietary database (now maintained as part of HubSpot's data operations). You get one data source, curated and maintained by HubSpot's team, with no option to supplement from external providers within the same workflow.

CRM integration depth. Clay is CRM-agnostic. It outputs enriched data via webhooks, native integrations, or CSV exports to Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Outreach, and dozens of other tools. You configure where the data goes. Clearbit takes the opposite approach: it is built into HubSpot. Enrichment happens automatically when a contact or company record is created or updated, with data fields mapped directly to HubSpot properties. There is no middleware, no webhook configuration, and no export step. For HubSpot-native teams, this is genuinely frictionless. For teams on other CRMs, Clearbit offers limited value.

Real-time vs workflow-based enrichment. Clearbit's signature feature is instant enrichment on form submission. When a lead fills out a form on your site, Clearbit appends company size, industry, revenue range, and other fields before the record even reaches a sales rep's queue. Clay operates differently: enrichment runs as part of configured workflows, either triggered by events or executed on a schedule. Clay offers more control over what gets enriched and when, but Clearbit's real-time approach eliminates the delay between data capture and data availability inside HubSpot.

When to Choose Clay

✓ Choose Clay if you need:

  • Multi-source data coverage across 75+ providers with custom waterfall logic
  • CRM-agnostic enrichment that feeds Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, or any tool via webhooks
  • Granular control over which data sources are queried and in what order

Key strengths: 75+ data providers, custom waterfall sequencing, output to any CRM or tool, AI-assisted data mapping

Clay is the stronger choice when your team uses multiple systems and needs enrichment that is not locked to a single CRM. Because Clay pulls from independent providers, you can build enrichment workflows that prioritize accuracy for specific data points. For example, you might query one provider for direct-dial phone numbers, a second for technographic data, and a third for funding history, all in a single waterfall. This is particularly valuable for teams whose data needs vary by segment or campaign type.

The tradeoff is complexity. Clay requires you to configure which sources to query, define fallback logic, and set up output destinations. If your team has the technical capacity to build and maintain these workflows, the data quality gains are significant. If you want enrichment that works out of the box with no setup, Clay will feel over-engineered for your use case.

When to Choose Clearbit (HubSpot)

✓ Choose Clearbit (HubSpot) if you need:

  • Native HubSpot enrichment that fills contact and company fields automatically
  • Real-time data append on form submissions and new record creation
  • Zero-configuration setup for teams already running HubSpot as their primary CRM

Key strengths: Real-time form enrichment, native HubSpot field mapping, Reveal (IP-to-company identification), automatic record updates

Clearbit is the right fit when your organization has committed to HubSpot and wants enrichment that requires no integration work. Once activated, Clearbit populates contact and company properties automatically as records enter HubSpot. The real-time form enrichment is especially useful for marketing teams running inbound campaigns: a lead submits a short form, and Clearbit fills in company name, employee count, industry, and estimated revenue before the lead hits a sales rep's view.

The limitation is scope. Clearbit's data comes from a single proprietary database, and enrichment is tightly coupled to HubSpot. If you need to enrich records in Salesforce, or if you want to cross-reference multiple data vendors to improve match rates, Clearbit does not support those workflows. For HubSpot-only teams that value simplicity over flexibility, that tradeoff is often worth it.

Our Verdict

Clay wins when your enrichment needs extend beyond a single CRM or a single data source. Teams that operate across Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, and other tools will get more value from Clay's ability to aggregate data from 75+ providers and route enriched records wherever they need to go. The custom waterfall logic gives you control over data quality in a way that a single-source provider cannot match, especially for niche verticals or international markets where no single database has complete coverage.

Clearbit wins when your team runs on HubSpot and wants enrichment that works instantly with zero configuration. The real-time form enrichment and automatic field population remove friction from inbound workflows, and the tight integration means there are no syncing issues or middleware to maintain. If your CRM is HubSpot and your primary need is keeping contact and company records current, Clearbit delivers that with less operational overhead than Clay requires. The decision comes down to whether you need flexibility across your stack or depth within HubSpot.
