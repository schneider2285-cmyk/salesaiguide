When to Choose HubSpot Sales Hub

✓ Choose HubSpot if:

  • You need marketing + sales alignment in a single platform with shared data
  • You want to start free and scale into paid features as the team grows
  • Reporting and analytics matter with custom dashboards and attribution tracking
  • You need deep integrations with 1,600+ apps in the HubSpot marketplace
  • You're building a revenue platform that includes service, operations, and CMS alongside sales

✗ Skip HubSpot if:

  • You only need pipeline management and don't care about marketing tools
  • You're cost-sensitive and can't absorb per-seat pricing above $100/mo
  • Your team prefers a simpler, sales-only tool without feature bloat

Key strengths: Free CRM, email tracking, meeting scheduler, 1,600+ integrations, unified marketing + sales ecosystem

Migration Notes

Pipedrive → HubSpot

Effort: Medium (1-2 weeks)

Export Pipedrive deals and contacts as CSV, then use HubSpot's import wizard. The main work is remapping custom fields, rebuilding automations in HubSpot's workflow builder, and retraining reps on the new interface. HubSpot's free CRM lets you test the migration before committing to paid plans.

What you gain: Marketing tools, deeper reporting, 1,600+ integrations, scalable platform

What you lose: Pipeline simplicity, lower per-seat costs, fast onboarding UX

HubSpot → Pipedrive

Effort: Medium (1-2 weeks)

Export HubSpot contacts and deals via CSV export. The challenge is replacing HubSpot's marketing tools, reporting dashboards, and workflow automations with simpler Pipedrive equivalents or third-party alternatives. Teams usually migrate to reduce costs or simplify their stack.

What you gain: Simpler UX, lower costs, best-in-class pipeline view, faster rep adoption

What you lose: Marketing automation, advanced reporting, deep integration ecosystem

The Verdict

If you're an individual rep or small team: Start with Pipedrive if you just need deal management. Its Rep score of 74 reflects the best pipeline UX in the category, and at $14/mo it's hard to beat on value. If you also need marketing tools, HubSpot's free CRM is the better starting point.

If you're a sales leader building a revenue team: HubSpot's Org score of 74 reflects its platform advantage. As you add marketing, service, and operations hubs, the unified data model pays dividends. But if your team is purely sales-focused and under 20 people, Pipedrive's simplicity keeps reps selling instead of configuring software.

The growth path: Many teams start with Pipedrive for speed and simplicity, then migrate to HubSpot when they need marketing automation and deeper reporting. This is a well-worn path with good tooling support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is HubSpot or Pipedrive better for small businesses?

Pipedrive is typically better for small sales-focused teams due to its lower price ($14/mo vs $20/mo starter), simpler interface, and best-in-class pipeline visualization. HubSpot is better if you also need marketing tools, a free CRM to start with, or plan to scale into a full revenue platform over time.

Can I migrate from Pipedrive to HubSpot easily?

Yes, HubSpot offers a free migration tool and dedicated import wizards. The process typically takes 1-2 weeks for small teams. The main challenge is remapping custom fields and rebuilding automations. HubSpot's free CRM means you can test the migration before committing to paid plans.

Which CRM has better integrations?

HubSpot has significantly more integrations (1,600+ in its marketplace vs 400+ for Pipedrive). HubSpot also offers deeper native integrations with marketing, service, and operations tools within its own ecosystem. Pipedrive covers the essentials well but has fewer specialized integrations.
