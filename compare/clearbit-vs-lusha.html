Passive Enrichment vs Active Contact Lookup

Clearbit and Lusha both operate in the data enrichment category, but they solve fundamentally different problems in a sales workflow. Clearbit, now rebranded as Breeze Intelligence under HubSpot, specializes in website visitor deanonymization and automated firmographic enrichment. It runs passively in the background, using IP-to-company matching to identify anonymous visitors and appending over 100 data attributes to leads the moment they enter your CRM. Lusha takes the opposite approach: it is an on-demand contact reveal tool built around a browser extension that gives sales reps one-click access to verified emails and direct dial numbers while browsing LinkedIn profiles or company websites.

The distinction matters because these tools serve different stages of the pipeline. Clearbit identifies which companies are showing buying intent on your website before anyone fills out a form, powering account-based marketing workflows and automated lead scoring. Lusha helps individual reps find the specific person to call once they already know which company to target. Many teams use both tools in sequence: Clearbit surfaces the accounts, Lusha surfaces the contacts within those accounts. Understanding this use case difference is essential before comparing features, because choosing between them depends on whether your bottleneck is identifying interested companies or reaching the right people at companies you already know.

Key Differences That Matter

Website visitor deanonymization vs contact-level data. Clearbit Reveal uses IP-to-company matching to identify anonymous website visitors at the organization level, surfacing the company name, industry, employee count, and estimated revenue range of businesses browsing your site. This powers account-based marketing workflows where sales teams receive alerts when target accounts visit high-intent pages. Lusha does not offer website visitor identification at all. Instead, it provides individual-level contact data — verified email addresses and direct dial numbers — that reps access on demand through its browser extension. Clearbit tells you which company is interested; Lusha tells you which person to call. Teams running account-based programs typically need the company-level identification first, then use a contact tool like Lusha to find the specific decision-maker within that account.

HubSpot-native automation vs browser extension workflow. Clearbit operates as a background data layer inside HubSpot, enriching records automatically without any manual action from sales reps. Leads arrive pre-enriched with firmographic data, form shortening reduces field count to boost conversion rates, and lead scoring models consume enriched attributes natively within HubSpot workflows. Lusha requires active participation from the rep: install the browser extension, navigate to a LinkedIn profile, click the icon, and reveal contact details. This credit-per-contact model means reps control exactly which contacts they look up, which is efficient for targeted outreach but lacks the passive automation that marketing operations teams value in Clearbit's approach.

Firmographic enrichment depth vs contact accuracy. Clearbit appends over 100 firmographic, technographic, and demographic data attributes per record, covering company size, revenue range, technology stack, industry classification, and individual role details. This breadth of enrichment powers sophisticated lead scoring and segmentation models. Lusha focuses on a narrower but equally critical data set: verified business email addresses and direct dial phone numbers. According to G2 reviewers (Mar 2026), Lusha consistently surfaces mobile numbers for mid-market decision-makers where other tools return only switchboard numbers. The depth of company data in Clearbit is unmatched by Lusha, but the direct dial accuracy in Lusha is unmatched by Clearbit.

Pricing structure and accessibility. Clearbit is now bundled exclusively with HubSpot Marketing Hub as Breeze Intelligence, meaning new customers must subscribe to HubSpot to access the full product. According to HubSpot's Breeze Intelligence page (Mar 2026), enrichment credits are allocated based on the Marketing Hub tier and additional credits are available as add-on purchases. Lusha offers a free tier with five free credits per month, a Pro plan at $49 per month per user with 480 credits per year, and a Premium plan at $79 per month per user for unlimited lookups. For small teams without an existing HubSpot investment, Lusha provides a dramatically lower barrier to entry, while Clearbit delivers more value per credit for teams already paying for HubSpot.

Implementation and Onboarding

Clearbit setup depends on your HubSpot commitment. For teams already running HubSpot Marketing Hub Professional or Enterprise, activating Breeze Intelligence is straightforward: enable the feature within HubSpot settings, configure which records to auto-enrich, set up Reveal for visitor identification, and adjust form shortening rules. The enrichment starts working immediately on new inbound leads. However, teams not currently on HubSpot face a significant platform commitment before they can access Clearbit's deepest features. The HubSpot onboarding itself — migrating CRM data, configuring workflows, training the team — can take weeks or months depending on organizational complexity. Legacy standalone Clearbit plans are being sunset, so API-only access for non-HubSpot CRMs is available but the product roadmap clearly favors the HubSpot integration path.

Lusha is operational in minutes. A sales rep can install the Chrome browser extension, create a free account with five free credits, and start revealing contact data on LinkedIn profiles within five minutes. There is no CRM prerequisite, no platform migration, and no configuration required. The one-click contact reveal workflow works immediately. For teams that want CRM integration, connecting Lusha to Salesforce or HubSpot takes an additional ten to fifteen minutes of setup. According to Capterra reviewers (Feb 2026), Lusha's low onboarding friction is one of the most frequently cited advantages compared to enterprise data providers that require weeks of implementation support and dedicated CSMs.

Data Quality and Coverage

Clearbit excels at firmographic breadth but operates as a single source. Clearbit's proprietary database covers firmographic, technographic, and demographic attributes with strong accuracy in North American and European markets. The enrichment returns structured data including company size, revenue range, technology stack, funding stage, and individual seniority level. However, unlike multi-source orchestration tools such as Clay that waterfall across dozens of providers, Clearbit relies solely on its own database. When Clearbit lacks data on a specific company or contact, the field returns empty with no fallback mechanism. According to TrustRadius reviewers (2025), match rates for small businesses and startups outside well-known databases are noticeably lower than for mid-market and enterprise accounts.

Lusha prioritizes contact accuracy over enrichment breadth. Lusha's community-sourced data model continuously updates contact records through its user base of over 100 million professionals, according to Lusha's product page (Mar 2026). The platform runs email verification including catch-all detection, deliverability scoring, and bounce prediction on every lookup. During testing, Lusha's direct dial accuracy for mid-market contacts in the US and UK was notably strong. Coverage weakens outside North America and Western Europe, with G2 users (Mar 2026) reporting thinner results in APAC and Latin American markets. Lusha does not attempt to compete on firmographic depth — it provides basic company information alongside its core deliverable of verified emails and phone numbers.

Our Verdict

Clearbit wins for marketing and revenue operations teams that need passive, automated data enrichment embedded directly inside HubSpot. Lusha wins for individual sales reps and small teams that need fast, on-demand access to verified contact data without a platform commitment.

These tools solve different problems at different stages of the pipeline, which is why comparing them head-to-head requires understanding your primary bottleneck. If your challenge is identifying which companies are visiting your website and automatically enriching inbound leads with firmographic data for scoring and routing, Clearbit's Reveal and HubSpot-native enrichment are purpose-built for that workflow. The IP-to-company matching, form shortening, and automated enrichment credits eliminate manual research from the marketing-to-sales handoff entirely. The trade-off is HubSpot lock-in: new Clearbit customers must subscribe to HubSpot Marketing Hub, which is a significant platform commitment for teams running other CRMs.

If your challenge is finding the right person's phone number and email address so reps can make contact today, Lusha's browser extension delivers that data faster than any alternative we have tested. The credit-per-contact model means reps control costs by choosing exactly which contacts to reveal, and the free tier lets teams validate data quality before committing budget. The trade-off is that Lusha provides no website visitor identification, no automated background enrichment, and no form optimization — it is a targeted contact lookup tool, not a data infrastructure layer. For teams that need both capabilities, the most effective approach is running Clearbit for automated enrichment and visitor identification alongside Lusha for on-demand contact discovery, using each tool where its methodology delivers the strongest results.
