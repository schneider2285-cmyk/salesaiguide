Migration Notes

ZoomInfo → Apollo

Effort: Medium (1-2 weeks)

Export ZoomInfo contacts and import into Apollo. The main transition is rebuilding sequences in Apollo's built-in engagement tools and adjusting to Apollo's filter system. Teams typically save 80-90% on costs but lose access to intent data and advanced org charts.

What you gain: Built-in outreach, 80-90% cost savings, monthly billing flexibility

What you lose: Intent data, org chart depth, dedicated account management, advanced governance

Apollo → ZoomInfo

Effort: Medium-High (2-3 weeks)

Export Apollo contacts into ZoomInfo. The challenge is replacing Apollo's built-in sequences with ZoomInfo Engage (separate product) or a third-party tool like Outreach/SalesLoft. Budget approval is the biggest hurdle given the 10x+ cost increase.

What you gain: Intent data, org charts, deeper enterprise intelligence, dedicated support

What you lose: Built-in sequencing, free tier, affordable per-seat pricing, monthly flexibility

The Verdict

If you're an individual rep or small team: Apollo is the clear choice. The free tier gives you 10K leads monthly, built-in sequences let you prospect and outreach from one tool, and paid plans start at $49/mo. ZoomInfo's pricing simply doesn't make sense for teams under 20 people.

If you're a sales leader at mid-market or enterprise: ZoomInfo's Org score of 77 reflects its enterprise dominance. Intent data, org charts, and deep CRM integrations justify the premium for teams running ABM programs. But if budget is under pressure, Apollo at $119/mo per seat (Organization plan) covers 80% of the use cases at 10% of the cost.

The cost-conscious path: Many teams start with Apollo, prove ROI, and only upgrade to ZoomInfo when they need intent data or advanced enterprise features that Apollo genuinely lacks. This staged approach minimizes risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Apollo or ZoomInfo better for startups?

Apollo is significantly better for startups. It offers a free tier with 10K leads per month, paid plans starting at $49/mo, and built-in email sequences. ZoomInfo requires annual contracts typically starting at $15K-50K+/yr with no free tier, making it impractical for most early-stage companies.

Does ZoomInfo have better data than Apollo?

ZoomInfo generally has deeper enterprise data, especially for buying intent signals, org charts, and technographics. Apollo has a larger raw contact count (275M+ vs 200M+) and is often better for SMB prospecting. For international markets, both have accuracy gaps, though ZoomInfo invests more heavily in data verification.

Can I switch from ZoomInfo to Apollo to save money?

Yes, many teams migrate from ZoomInfo to Apollo to reduce costs by 80-90%. The main trade-offs are losing ZoomInfo's intent data, advanced org charts, and dedicated account management. Apollo covers core prospecting and outreach well, but lacks ZoomInfo's depth in enterprise intelligence features.
