JustCall vs Aircall: Which Sales Dialer Fits Your Team?

Quick Verdict

JustCall is the better choice for small sales teams that need calling plus SMS outreach in one affordable platform. Aircall is the better choice for teams that need a mature cloud phone system with advanced call routing for both inbound and outbound workflows.

Two Dialers, Two Workflow Priorities

JustCall and Aircall both serve sales teams that rely on phone outreach, but they approach the problem from different angles. JustCall is a sales-first platform that bundles power dialing with SMS campaigns, targeting small teams that want calling and texting in a single tool at an accessible price point. The platform prioritizes outbound workflows where reps combine dial sessions with text follow-up sequences.

Aircall is a broader cloud phone system designed for both sales and support teams. Its call routing engine, IVR menus, and queue management handle inbound call distribution alongside outbound power dialing. Aircall has been in market longer and carries a larger user base, which shows in its integration ecosystem and the maturity of features like call monitoring and live coaching for managers.

The comparison comes down to scope. JustCall keeps the feature set focused on outbound sales calling and SMS. Aircall builds a more complete phone system that handles inbound routing, customer support workflows, and outbound dialing within a single platform. Teams that only need outbound capabilities pay less for JustCall's focused approach. Teams that also manage inbound call volume or need advanced routing logic benefit from Aircall's wider feature surface.

Key Differences That Matter

SMS as a sales channel. This is JustCall's clearest advantage. Bulk SMS campaigns with templates, scheduling, and campaign-level analytics are built into the platform natively. Reps can alternate between phone calls and text messages within the same contact workflow without switching tools. Aircall supports basic one-to-one texting but does not offer bulk SMS campaign functionality. For teams where text follow-up is part of the outbound cadence, JustCall eliminates the need for a separate SMS tool.

Inbound call management. Aircall's call routing engine is substantially more capable. IVR menus, ring groups, queue management with wait-time announcements, and smart routing based on business hours and agent availability are core features. JustCall handles basic inbound routing but lacks the depth needed by teams managing meaningful inbound call volume alongside outbound dialing. If your team answers customer calls and makes outbound sales calls from the same platform, Aircall handles both workflows without compromise.

Manager coaching tools. Aircall includes live call monitoring with whisper mode, which lets managers coach reps during calls without the prospect hearing. Barge-in capability lets managers join calls directly when needed. JustCall's monitoring features are more limited, offering call recording review but less real-time intervention capability. Growing teams where managers need to coach remotely across multiple reps benefit from Aircall's more mature supervision tooling.

Pricing structure. JustCall's entry point is lower at nineteen dollars per user per month with no seat minimum. Aircall requires a three-seat minimum at thirty dollars per user per month, making the minimum commitment ninety dollars monthly before any usage. For a solo founder or two-person sales team, JustCall's pricing structure is significantly more accessible. At ten seats or more, the per-user price gap narrows and the decision shifts toward which feature set matches the workflow better.

Implementation Reality

Both platforms deploy in under an hour for basic outbound calling. JustCall's setup is straightforward: provision a number, connect your CRM, import contacts, and the power dialer is ready. The SMS campaign builder adds minimal additional configuration. Aircall's setup takes slightly longer if you configure IVR menus, ring groups, and routing rules, but teams using it purely for outbound dialing can skip those features and get started at the same speed. CRM integration quality is comparable across both platforms — Salesforce and HubSpot sync reliably on each. The difference surfaces in ongoing operations: Aircall's admin dashboard provides more granular control over team settings, number management, and call flow configuration, which matters as the team grows past ten users and operational complexity increases.

When to Choose JustCall

✓ JustCall is the better fit when:

  • SMS is part of your outbound cadence and you want calling and text campaigns managed from one platform instead of adding a separate SMS tool to the stack
  • Your team is small with fewer than ten reps and the lower entry price plus no seat minimum keeps monthly costs manageable during early growth
  • Outbound only: Your team focuses on outbound dialing and does not need advanced inbound routing, IVR menus, or call queue management
  • Speed to value matters — you want a dialer that can be operational within thirty minutes with minimal configuration

When to Choose Aircall

✓ Aircall is the better fit when:

  • You handle both inbound and outbound calls from the same team and need IVR menus, ring groups, and queue management that JustCall's routing cannot match
  • Manager coaching is critical: Whisper and barge-in features let managers train reps during live calls, which accelerates onboarding and improves call quality across the team
  • Integration ecosystem matters: Aircall's broader integration library connects to tools beyond the standard CRM set, including helpdesk platforms, e-commerce systems, and workflow automation tools
  • Your team is growing past ten reps and you need administrative controls, team management features, and reporting depth that scale with organizational complexity

Our Verdict

JustCall and Aircall both handle outbound sales calling competently, but they serve different team profiles. JustCall wins on price, SMS integration, and simplicity for small outbound-focused teams. Aircall wins on inbound call management, manager coaching tools, and administrative depth for growing organizations that handle both inbound and outbound phone workflows.

If your team dials out and texts prospects but does not manage inbound call volume, JustCall covers that workflow at a lower cost. If your sales team also fields inbound calls or your managers need to coach reps during live conversations, Aircall's broader platform justifies the higher price point. Start with the workflow — the right tool follows from there.
