Chrome Extension Speed vs Full Prospecting Stack

Lusha and Apollo.io both help sales teams find contact information, but they solve fundamentally different workflow problems. Lusha is built around a single interaction: you land on a LinkedIn profile or company website, click the Chrome extension, and get a direct dial or email within seconds. There is no list to build, no sequence to configure, and no dashboard to learn. For individual reps who measure success in dials per hour, that simplicity matters.

Apollo approaches prospecting as a system rather than a single action. It combines contact discovery with multi-step email sequences, call task queues, and team-level reporting. The question is not which tool has better data — both deliver usable B2B contact info — but whether you need a quick-lookup tool or a structured outbound workflow. A solo SDR booking meetings through cold calls has different requirements than a team manager coordinating sequences across five reps. This comparison breaks down where each tool fits based on how you actually prospect day to day.

Key Differences That Matter

Contact lookup workflow. Lusha's Chrome extension is purpose-built for speed. You visit a LinkedIn profile, click the icon, and see verified phone numbers and emails in a pop-up — no need to leave the page or switch tabs. Apollo also has a Chrome extension, but its primary discovery workflow is list-based: you build filtered searches inside Apollo's web app, export contacts, and enrich them in bulk. For reps who prospect by browsing LinkedIn one profile at a time, Lusha's approach requires fewer clicks. For teams building targeted account lists of hundreds of contacts, Apollo's search-and-filter model is more efficient.

Outbound sequence capabilities. This is the widest gap between the two tools. Lusha is purely a data product — it reveals contact information but does not send emails or manage follow-up tasks. If you use Lusha, you still need a separate tool for outreach sequences (Outreach, Salesloft, or even a basic mail merge). Apollo includes a full sequencing engine with multi-step email cadences, automated follow-ups, A/B testing on subject lines, and a built-in dialer with call recording. For teams that want prospecting and outreach in one interface, Apollo eliminates the need for a second subscription.

Pricing structure. Lusha charges on a credit-per-reveal model — each time you view a contact's details, it costs one credit, and plans are tiered by monthly credit allotment (see Lusha pricing). Apollo's paid plans charge per seat and include substantially more contact reveals per month — its Professional tier, for example, bundles sequence sending, call minutes, and enrichment credits into a single per-user fee (see Apollo pricing). For high-volume prospecting, Apollo's bundled model tends to cost less per contact than Lusha's a-la-carte credits.

When to Choose Lusha

✓ Choose Lusha if you need:

  • Simplicity & direct dials as your top priority
  • B2B contact data platform
  • Individual reps finding direct contact info

Key strengths: Direct dials, Email finder, Chrome extension

Lusha works best when your prospecting motion is phone-first. If your day consists of browsing LinkedIn for decision-makers, grabbing their direct dial, and immediately calling, Lusha removes every unnecessary step from that loop. The Chrome extension surfaces verified mobile numbers directly on LinkedIn and company websites, which means you spend less time toggling between tabs and more time in conversation. Reps who track their connect rate per hour tend to see the most value here.

Lusha is also a strong fit for teams that already have a dedicated outreach tool and just need a data layer on top. If you run sequences in Outreach or Salesloft, adding Lusha as a contact enrichment source keeps your stack specialized — one tool for data, one tool for sending. That separation can be simpler to manage than an all-in-one platform, especially for smaller teams that do not need Apollo's broader feature set.

When to Choose Apollo.io

✓ Choose Apollo.io if you need:

  • Feature breadth as your top priority
  • All-in-one sales intelligence & engagement
  • Teams needing prospecting + outreach in one tool

Key strengths: Multi-step email sequences, Team task management, Built-in dialer with analytics

Apollo is the better choice when you need prospecting and outreach under one roof. Instead of discovering a contact in one tool and then manually importing them into a sequencing platform, Apollo lets you find a prospect, add them to a multi-step email cadence, and schedule follow-up calls — all without leaving the app. For team leads managing multiple SDRs, this consolidation means fewer handoffs and clearer visibility into each rep's pipeline activity through Apollo's built-in reporting dashboards.

Apollo also makes sense when your outbound motion depends on systematic list building rather than one-off lookups. Its advanced search filters let you build targeted prospect lists by title, company size, technology stack, and hiring signals, then push those lists directly into automated sequences. If your team runs structured campaigns — say, targeting VP-level contacts at mid-market SaaS companies in a specific region — Apollo's workflow handles the full cycle from list creation to reply tracking in a single interface.

Our Verdict

Lusha wins when speed-to-dial is the metric that matters most. If you are an individual contributor who prospects by working through LinkedIn profiles and calling direct, Lusha's Chrome extension delivers contact data with fewer clicks and less setup than any all-in-one platform. It also fits cleanly into existing tech stacks — pair it with a dedicated sequencing tool and you have a focused, low-overhead prospecting workflow without paying for features you will not use.

Apollo wins when your team needs a coordinated outbound system. Its combination of filtered list building, multi-step email sequences, task queues, and team-level analytics means one platform handles the entire prospecting cycle. For managers who need visibility into rep activity, or teams scaling from two SDRs to ten, Apollo's workflow tools justify the higher per-seat cost by consolidating what would otherwise require two or three separate subscriptions.
