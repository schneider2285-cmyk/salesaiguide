When to Choose Salesloft

✓ Choose Salesloft if:

  • You need conversation analytics with built-in call recording and AI coaching
  • Your team is 50+ reps and needs enterprise-grade reporting and forecasting
  • You want a unified platform combining cadences, dialer, and deal intelligence
  • CRM integration depth matters with Salesforce and MS Dynamics native sync
  • Your org requires SOC 2 Type II and full RBAC for compliance

✗ Skip Salesloft if:

  • You need published, predictable pricing for budget planning
  • Your team is under 20 reps and doesn't need enterprise analytics
  • You want a parallel dialer for high-velocity outbound calling

Migration Notes

Salesloft → Klenty

Effort: Medium (1-2 weeks)

Export cadence templates and contact lists from Salesloft as CSV. Rebuild cadences in Klenty's interface and reconnect CRM integrations. Analytics history and conversation recordings will not transfer.

What you gain: Transparent pricing, parallel dialer, lower per-seat costs

What you lose: Conversation intelligence, enterprise analytics, SOC 2 compliance

Klenty → Salesloft

Effort: Medium (1-2 weeks)

Export contact lists and cadence data from Klenty. Import into Salesloft and rebuild sequences using its cadence builder. Salesloft's onboarding team typically assists with enterprise migrations.

What you gain: Conversation analytics, enterprise compliance, deeper reporting

What you lose: Parallel dialer, transparent pricing, lower cost per seat

The Verdict

If you're an individual rep or small team: Start with Klenty. Its transparent pricing, fast onboarding, and parallel dialer give you strong engagement capabilities without the enterprise overhead. The Startup plan at $50/user/mo covers core cadence needs.

If you're a sales leader building an enterprise org: Salesloft's Org score of 70 reflects its strength in enterprise analytics, conversation intelligence, and compliance. If your team is 50+ reps and you need call recording insights, deal intelligence, and SOC 2 certification, the premium is justified.

The deciding factor: Do you need conversation analytics? If yes, Salesloft is the only choice here. If not, Klenty delivers comparable cadence functionality at 40-60% lower cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Salesloft or Klenty better for a mid-market team?

Klenty is typically better for mid-market teams. Its transparent pricing starting at $50/user/mo, parallel dialer, and solid cadence features deliver strong value without requiring enterprise-level budgets. Salesloft is purpose-built for enterprise with conversation analytics and deeper reporting, but its custom pricing often starts at $75-125/user/mo.

Does Salesloft include conversation intelligence?

Yes. After acquiring Chorus.ai (now part of the platform post-Drift merger), Salesloft includes built-in conversation analytics with call recording, AI-powered insights, and deal intelligence. This is a significant differentiator over Klenty, which offers only basic call analytics.

Can I switch from Salesloft to Klenty easily?

Migration is moderate effort (1-2 weeks). You can export cadence templates and contact lists from Salesloft, but sequence logic and analytics history do not transfer. The main work is rebuilding cadences in Klenty and retraining reps on the new interface. Teams that relied heavily on conversation analytics will lose that capability.
