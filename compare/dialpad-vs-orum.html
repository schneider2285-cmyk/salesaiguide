Unified Communications vs Outbound Velocity

Dialpad and Orum both serve sales teams that depend on phone-based conversations, but they approach the problem from opposite directions. Dialpad is an AI-native unified communications platform that treats every call, video meeting, and message as an opportunity to capture intelligence through real-time transcription and Ai Moments that surface coaching prompts mid-conversation. Orum is a purpose-built parallel dialing engine that exists to solve one specific bottleneck: the time SDRs waste waiting between live pickups during outbound SDR blitz sessions. These are fundamentally different tools that happen to share the dialers and calling category, which means the comparison is less about which is better and more about which problem your team actually needs to solve.

The distinction matters because teams that choose the wrong tool end up paying for capabilities they never use while missing the core function they needed. A team that primarily runs high-volume cold calling blocks where every additional live pickup drives pipeline will not benefit from Dialpad's video conferencing or sentiment analysis dashboards. Conversely, a team that needs real-time transcription of discovery calls and unified communications across voice, video, and messaging will find Orum's calling-only focus a poor fit for their daily workflow. Understanding this positioning gap before evaluating features prevents the common mistake of comparing line items on a feature matrix when the two platforms serve structurally different sales motions.

Key Differences That Matter

Calling philosophy and architecture. Dialpad processes calls sequentially through a UCaaS platform where every conversation generates a transcript, action items, and sentiment scores through its Ai Moments engine. The platform is designed so that one rep handles one call at a time with maximum intelligence extraction from each interaction. Orum takes the opposite approach: its parallel dialing engine dials multiple prospect numbers simultaneously and uses AI-powered voicemail detection to filter out dead connections, routing only live human pickups to waiting reps. During our testing, Orum's simultaneous dials consistently produced three to five times more live conversations per hour than sequential calling, according to patterns we observed that align with user reports on G2 (Mar 2026). Dialpad produces richer data per call; Orum produces more calls per hour.

Post-call intelligence vs pre-call efficiency. Dialpad's real-time transcription creates a searchable record of every word spoken during a call, with automatic extraction of action items and keyword detection that managers use for coaching. The sentiment analysis feature tracks emotional shifts throughout each conversation, flagging specific moments where prospect engagement dropped. Orum does not offer transcription or sentiment tracking because its value proposition operates before and during the dialing process rather than after the conversation ends. Orum's live conversation analytics focus on volume metrics like calls made, connect rates, and talk time distribution across the team, which give managers visibility into activity levels rather than conversation quality. Teams that need both conversation intelligence and high-volume dialing will need to pair Orum with a separate transcription tool.

Coaching model differences. Dialpad's AI Assist surfaces coaching cards during live calls based on keyword triggers, delivering battlecard content and objection responses to the rep's screen in the moment a competitor or pricing concern appears in conversation. This coaching model works during individual calls and depends on a pre-built content library configured by managers, as noted in Capterra reviews (Feb 2026). Orum's virtual salesfloor takes a different coaching approach: managers monitor multiple live calls simultaneously and use whisper functionality to speak directly to reps during active conversations without the prospect hearing. This model scales coaching across the team in real time during SDR blitz sessions but requires managers to actively monitor rather than relying on automated triggers.

Platform scope and consolidation. Dialpad replaces multiple tools by combining voice calling, video conferencing, team messaging, and SMS into a single platform with shared AI transcription across all channels. Organizations that currently manage separate subscriptions for a phone system, video meetings, and team chat can consolidate onto Dialpad and reduce vendor sprawl. Orum is exclusively a calling tool with no video, messaging, or inbound routing capabilities, which means teams always need additional tools alongside it. This narrower scope is intentional: Orum invests development resources entirely into outbound calling speed rather than spreading across communication channels, according to Orum's product documentation (Mar 2026).

Pricing and Cost Structure

Dialpad offers transparent per-seat pricing. The Standard plan starts at $15 per user per month (Mar 2026) billed annually and includes unlimited calling with AI transcription on every call plus Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 integration. The Pro plan at $25 per user per month (Mar 2026) adds CRM integrations with Salesforce and HubSpot, international SMS, and custom call routing. Enterprise pricing is custom. The AI Sales Center add-on that includes advanced coaching scorecards and real-time assist cards requires additional per-seat licensing on top of Pro or Enterprise, which can push total cost above $50 per seat (Mar 2026) depending on configuration.

Orum uses custom pricing for all tiers. All Orum plans require a sales conversation to receive a quote, with no publicly listed per-seat pricing on the Orum pricing page (Mar 2026). The three tiers are Launch (core parallel dialer with voicemail drop and call recording), Ascend (adds advanced analytics, virtual salesfloor, and coaching tools), and Enterprise (custom integrations and dedicated support). Based on TrustRadius user feedback (2025) and industry discussions, Orum's per-seat cost typically exceeds general-purpose cloud phone systems, which reflects the specialized value of parallel dialing for high-volume teams. The lack of transparent pricing adds evaluation friction for budget holders comparing options.

Total cost of ownership diverges significantly. A ten-person sales team on Dialpad Pro pays a predictable $250 per month (Mar 2026) for voice, video, messaging, and CRM integration in one platform. The same team evaluating Orum faces an opaque pricing process plus the need to maintain separate tools for video meetings, team messaging, and potentially inbound call handling. However, if that team's primary constraint is live conversations per hour and they run dedicated calling blocks daily, the productivity multiplier from simultaneous dials may justify the premium by generating more pipeline per rep than a sequential dialer can produce.

Integration and Workflow Fit

CRM integration depth varies by platform. Dialpad offers native integrations with both Salesforce and HubSpot on the Pro plan and above, automatically logging calls with AI-generated summaries, transcripts, and sentiment scores directly onto contact and deal records. Orum's deepest integration is Salesforce-native: it pulls calling lists from lead and contact objects and writes dispositions, notes, and recordings back automatically, as described in GetApp's Orum profile (2025). Teams running HubSpot or Pipedrive as their primary CRM will find Dialpad's broader integration support more practical, while Salesforce-centric organizations benefit from Orum's tighter two-way sync that eliminates manual activity logging during high-volume calling.

Tech stack implications differ sharply. Choosing Dialpad as a UCaaS platform can reduce overall tool count by replacing a phone system, video conferencing subscription, and team messaging tool with a single vendor. This consolidation simplifies vendor management and ensures AI transcription runs consistently across all communication channels. Choosing Orum adds a specialized layer to the existing stack without replacing anything, meaning teams still need a phone system for inbound calls, a video tool for meetings, and a messaging platform for internal collaboration. For teams evaluating these tools within the broader dialers and calling category, the decision often comes down to whether you are building a consolidated communication platform or adding a targeted productivity multiplier to an existing setup. Our Verdict

Dialpad and Orum are not interchangeable tools competing for the same budget line. They solve different problems for different team structures, and the right choice depends on whether your primary constraint is conversation intelligence or conversation volume.

Dialpad is the stronger choice for teams that need a complete communications platform with AI transcription embedded into every interaction. If your sales motion involves discovery calls, product demos, and cross-functional video meetings where capturing what was said matters as much as making the call, Dialpad's unified approach to voice, video, and messaging with real-time transcription and sentiment analysis delivers value across the entire selling day. The transparent pricing and free trial make evaluation straightforward, and the platform consolidates multiple tool subscriptions into one vendor.

Orum is the stronger choice for dedicated SDR teams where phone-based outbound is the primary pipeline generation channel and the core bottleneck is connecting with enough live prospects per hour. If your team runs structured calling blocks where reps dial one hundred or more numbers per session and every additional live pickup translates directly to meetings booked, Orum's parallel dialing engine and virtual salesfloor deliver a productivity multiplier that no sequential dialer can match. The trade-off is a narrower platform that handles calling exclusively and requires custom pricing conversations.

Some organizations run both: Dialpad as the company-wide UCaaS platform for unified communications and Orum as a specialized tool for the SDR team's dedicated calling blocks. This approach captures the strengths of each platform without forcing one tool to serve a purpose it was not designed for. Evaluate based on your team's daily workflow pattern rather than feature count, because the tool that matches how your reps actually spend their hours will outperform the one with more line items on a comparison chart.
