When to Choose HubSpot Sales Hub

✓ Choose HubSpot if:

  • Marketing-sales alignment is critical and you want CRM, marketing, and sales in one platform
  • You need built-in sales engagement with email sequences, templates, and meeting scheduling
  • Ecosystem matters and you want access to 1,500+ marketplace integrations
  • You're scaling fast and need a platform that grows from startup to enterprise
  • Enterprise compliance is required with SOC 2, SSO, RBAC, and audit logs

✗ Skip HubSpot if:

  • Your budget can't absorb the Starter-to-Professional price jump
  • You need deep no-code customization beyond HubSpot's structured templates
  • You only need basic pipeline management and don't use marketing features

Key strengths: Unified marketing-sales platform, 1,500+ integrations, Breeze AI, enterprise compliance, free CRM tier

Migration Notes

HubSpot → monday CRM

Effort: Medium (1-3 weeks)

Export contacts, deals, and companies from HubSpot as CSV and import into monday boards. The data migration is straightforward, but rebuilding automations, email sequences, and reporting is the main effort. monday's no-code builder helps, but complex HubSpot workflows may need to be simplified.

What you gain: Lower cost, visual flexibility, no-code customization

What you lose: Marketing Hub integration, sales sequences, ecosystem depth, advanced reporting

monday CRM → HubSpot

Effort: Low-Medium (1-2 weeks)

Export monday boards as CSV and import into HubSpot CRM. HubSpot's import wizard handles most field mapping. The transition usually adds capabilities (sequences, marketing tools) rather than requiring simplification, making it a smoother path.

What you gain: Marketing-sales alignment, sequences, 1,500+ integrations, Breeze AI

What you lose: Visual board flexibility, no-code customization depth, lower per-seat costs

The Verdict

If you're an individual rep: Both tools are easy to adopt (tied at 9/10). HubSpot's free CRM gives you unlimited basic CRM at no cost. monday CRM's visual interface is more intuitive for reps who think visually. The Rep score gap (HubSpot 72 vs monday 70) is narrow, so personal preference matters here.

If you're a sales leader: HubSpot's Org score of 74 vs monday's 58 tells the story. HubSpot is the more complete platform for growing sales organizations. It offers marketing alignment, built-in engagement tools, and a deeper enterprise feature set. monday CRM works well for teams with simpler needs and tighter budgets.

The budget question: If your team has outgrown spreadsheets but HubSpot Professional ($100/seat) is out of reach, monday CRM ($17/seat Standard) delivers solid pipeline management at a fraction of the cost. But if you can afford HubSpot, you'll get more long-term value from the ecosystem.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is monday CRM a real alternative to HubSpot?

For small to mid-market teams with straightforward sales processes, yes. Monday CRM offers visual pipeline management, no-code customization, and lower pricing. However, it lacks HubSpot's depth in marketing automation, sales engagement, and ecosystem breadth. If you need a full revenue platform, HubSpot is more capable. If you need a flexible, visual CRM, monday competes well.

Which CRM is cheaper for a 10-person sales team?

Monday CRM is significantly cheaper at comparable tiers. Monday Standard costs $170/mo (10 x $17) vs HubSpot Professional at $1,000/mo (10 x $100). Even HubSpot Starter at $200/mo is more than monday Standard. However, HubSpot's free CRM (unlimited users, limited features) can be the cheapest option if you only need basic contact management.

Can I migrate from HubSpot to monday CRM easily?

The data migration is straightforward via CSV export/import. The harder part is rebuilding automations, email sequences, and reporting. Monday doesn't match HubSpot's automation depth, so complex workflows may need to be simplified. Expect 1-3 weeks for a full migration depending on your HubSpot complexity.
