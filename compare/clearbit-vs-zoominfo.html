Quick Verdict

Clearbit wins for teams that need real-time data enrichment baked into their CRM and marketing workflows -- API-first architecture, HubSpot-native integration, and form-fill reduction make it the stronger choice for inbound-led organizations. ZoomInfo wins for outbound-first sales teams that need a searchable prospecting database, intent signals, org charts, and technographic filters to build targeted account lists at scale.

Clearbit and ZoomInfo occupy neighboring but distinct positions in the B2B data market. Clearbit, now part of HubSpot following its 2023 acquisition, operates as a real-time data enrichment engine built around an API-first architecture. Its core function is appending firmographic, technographic, and contact-level data to records that already exist in your CRM or marketing automation platform. ZoomInfo, by contrast, is a full-scale B2B intelligence platform designed for outbound prospecting teams. It provides a searchable database of over 260 million business contacts, layered with intent data, org chart mapping, and territory planning tools. The comparison between these two products is less about which is objectively superior and more about which workflow your revenue team depends on: enriching inbound leads and existing records, or building outbound prospect lists from scratch.

Core Differences at a Glance

The fundamental difference between Clearbit and ZoomInfo comes down to the direction of data flow. Clearbit is enrichment-first: you feed it an email address, domain, or IP address, and it returns structured company and contact data in real time. This makes it particularly useful for shortening forms on landing pages, scoring inbound leads instantly, and keeping CRM records current without manual research. The product was built for developers and marketing operations teams who want to embed data enrichment directly into their existing tech stack through API calls and native integrations.

ZoomInfo is prospecting-first: you start with filters like industry, company size, technology stack, and geographic region, and the platform returns lists of contacts and companies that match your ideal customer profile. Its strength is in enabling outbound sales motions where reps need to identify, research, and reach decision-makers they have no prior relationship with. ZoomInfo also layers in buyer intent signals, website visitor identification, and org chart visualization -- capabilities that Clearbit either does not offer or handles in a more limited fashion. Where Clearbit excels at making existing data richer, ZoomInfo excels at generating net-new pipeline through targeted prospecting.

Clearbit -- Full Breakdown

Clearbit was founded in 2014 with a simple premise: give developers an API that returns structured company and contact data from minimal inputs. An email address yields job title, seniority, company name, employee count, revenue range, industry classification, and technology stack. A domain yields the full firmographic profile. An IP address yields the visiting company. This API-first model made Clearbit a standard tool in marketing operations and growth engineering teams that embed enrichment logic directly into lead routing, scoring, and segmentation workflows.

Following HubSpot's acquisition in late 2023, Clearbit's enrichment capabilities have been progressively integrated into HubSpot's Smart CRM. HubSpot customers on Professional and Enterprise tiers now receive a baseline of Clearbit-powered enrichment included in their subscription. For non-HubSpot users, the standalone Clearbit product remains available with its own pricing tiers. The Clearbit Reveal product -- which identifies the company behind anonymous website visits -- continues to serve both HubSpot and non-HubSpot customers and remains one of the more accurate IP-to-company resolution tools on the market.

Where Clearbit falls short is in outbound prospecting. The platform does not provide a searchable contact database in the way ZoomInfo, Apollo, or Lusha do. You cannot open Clearbit, filter by industry and company size, and export a list of VP-level contacts. If your sales motion depends on building cold outbound lists, Clearbit is the wrong tool for that job. Its value is concentrated in making the data you already have more complete, more accurate, and more actionable. For a detailed evaluation, see our Clearbit review.

ZoomInfo -- Full Breakdown

ZoomInfo is the incumbent in B2B sales intelligence, serving over 35,000 customers as of 2025 according to its public filings. The platform centers on a proprietary database of business contacts and companies, built and maintained through a combination of machine learning models, a contributor network (users who share data in exchange for access), web crawling, and manual verification teams. The result is a searchable database of 260 million-plus contact profiles and 100 million-plus company profiles, with direct dial phone numbers, verified email addresses, and organizational hierarchy data.

Beyond the core database, ZoomInfo has expanded into a broader go-to-market platform. Its intent data product tracks buying signals across thousands of topics, surfacing accounts that are actively researching solutions in your category. The org chart feature maps reporting structures within target accounts, helping enterprise reps identify the full buying committee rather than relying on a single point of contact. Technographic filters let users find companies that use specific software products -- useful for competitive displacement campaigns or integration-based selling.

The trade-offs with ZoomInfo are well documented. Pricing starts at $14,995 per year for the SalesOS package, and most mid-market deployments run between $25,000 and $50,000 annually once additional seats and features are added. Contracts are annual with auto-renewal clauses that require 60 to 90 days advance notice to cancel. Data freshness can be inconsistent, particularly for smaller companies and contacts outside North America. And the platform's breadth -- while a strength for enterprise teams -- can feel overwhelming for smaller organizations that only need a fraction of its capabilities. Pricing Comparison

Clearbit and ZoomInfo sit at opposite ends of the pricing spectrum, which reflects their different target markets and product scopes. Clearbit's enrichment plans start at approximately $99 per month for lower-volume API usage, with pricing scaling based on the number of enrichment calls and Reveal page views per month. HubSpot customers on paid CRM tiers receive a bundled allocation of Clearbit enrichment credits at no additional cost, which can reduce or eliminate the need for a separate Clearbit subscription depending on volume requirements.

ZoomInfo's SalesOS platform starts at $14,995 per year for a single-seat package with limited credits. In practice, most teams investing in ZoomInfo spend between $25,000 and $50,000 per year once they add seats for multiple reps, upgrade to higher credit tiers, and enable add-on modules like intent data or website visitor tracking. All ZoomInfo contracts are annual, and the platform does not offer a monthly billing option. This pricing model makes ZoomInfo a significant budget commitment that generally requires VP-level or C-level approval, while Clearbit can often be adopted at the individual contributor or team lead level without enterprise procurement involvement.

Which Should You Choose?

The decision between Clearbit and ZoomInfo is less about comparing two equivalent products and more about identifying which data workflow drives your revenue. If your primary challenge is enriching inbound leads, reducing form friction, maintaining CRM data quality, or identifying anonymous website visitors at the company level, Clearbit is the more focused and cost-effective solution. Its API-first architecture integrates cleanly into marketing automation platforms and custom internal tools. HubSpot customers in particular benefit from the native integration that comes included with their CRM subscription.

If your team runs an outbound-first sales motion -- building target account lists, researching buying committees, running account-based marketing campaigns, or using intent signals to prioritize outreach -- ZoomInfo provides capabilities that Clearbit simply does not offer. The searchable prospecting database, direct dial phone numbers, org chart mapping, and intent data tracking are purpose-built for outbound pipeline generation at scale. The cost is substantially higher, but for enterprise sales organizations where a single closed deal can exceed the annual platform cost, the return on investment math often works.

For teams that operate both inbound and outbound motions, it is worth noting that some organizations do run both tools in parallel. ZoomInfo handles the prospecting and intent data layer, while Clearbit handles real-time enrichment of inbound leads and CRM hygiene. This dual-tool approach makes the most sense for mid-market and enterprise revenue teams with the budget to support it and the operational complexity that justifies two separate data providers. Smaller teams should pick one based on their dominant go-to-market motion and add the other only when they outgrow the first. Frequently Asked Questions

Below are the most common questions we receive from sales and marketing teams evaluating Clearbit and ZoomInfo. Each answer draws on our hands-on testing, verified pricing research from official product pages and third-party sources, and conversations with current users of both platforms to help you make the right data infrastructure decision for your go-to-market workflow.

Is Clearbit the same product now that HubSpot acquired it?

HubSpot completed its acquisition of Clearbit in 2023 and has since integrated Clearbit's enrichment engine into HubSpot's Smart CRM. Clearbit's API and standalone enrichment plans remain available, but HubSpot customers on paid tiers now receive certain enrichment features natively. The standalone Clearbit product continues to serve non-HubSpot users through its API-first platform.

Can Clearbit replace ZoomInfo for outbound prospecting?

Clearbit is not designed as a direct replacement for ZoomInfo's prospecting capabilities. Clearbit focuses on enriching records you already have and identifying anonymous website visitors, while ZoomInfo provides a searchable database of over 260 million contacts for outbound list building. Teams that rely heavily on outbound prospecting will find ZoomInfo's database and filtering tools more suited to that workflow.

Which tool has more accurate company data?

Both platforms maintain high accuracy rates, but their strengths differ. Clearbit refreshes firmographic and technographic data in real time via its API, which tends to keep records current for companies with active web presences. ZoomInfo relies on a combination of machine learning models, contributor networks, and manual verification across its larger database. Accuracy can vary by region and company size on both platforms.

Do I need both Clearbit and ZoomInfo?

Some mid-market and enterprise teams do run both tools simultaneously. In that setup, ZoomInfo handles outbound list building and intent-based account identification, while Clearbit enriches inbound leads and CRM records in real time. However, the combined cost can exceed $20,000 per year, so most teams under 50 reps choose one based on whether their primary motion is inbound enrichment or outbound prospecting.

Clearbit is the right choice for inbound-led teams, marketing operations professionals, and HubSpot users who need real-time data enrichment embedded directly into their CRM and lead management workflows. ZoomInfo is the right choice for outbound-first sales organizations that need a large-scale prospecting database, buyer intent signals, and org chart data to build pipeline proactively. The two tools address different problems, and the right answer depends entirely on how your revenue team generates and processes its pipeline.
