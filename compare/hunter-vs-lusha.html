Domain Search Specialist vs LinkedIn Contact Revealer

Hunter.io and Lusha both serve the lead prospecting category — helping sales teams find verified contact information for the people they want to reach — but they approach the problem from fundamentally different starting points. Hunter.io is a deliverability-first email discovery engine built around domain search: you enter a company domain and Hunter returns every email pattern and verified address it can find associated with that domain, along with confidence scores and sourcing transparency. Lusha is a contact reveal platform designed around individual lookups: you browse a LinkedIn profile or company page, click the Lusha extension, and it surfaces the person's direct email and phone number from its proprietary database. The tools overlap on basic email finding, but the divergence in architecture shapes how they fit into prospecting workflows, what data they return, and how teams scale their outreach.

The practical distinction matters for daily prospecting. If your team builds lead lists by identifying target companies and then finding every reachable contact at those companies in bulk, Hunter.io's domain search and bulk enrichment via CSV upload are purpose-built for that workflow. If your reps prospect one contact at a time through LinkedIn browsing, sending connection requests and then revealing phone numbers and emails as they go, Lusha's browser extension fits that motion with less friction. Neither tool replaces a full sales engagement platform, but each excels at the specific discovery step that feeds the rest of your outreach pipeline.

Key Differences That Matter

Domain search and email pattern detection. Hunter.io's domain search is the foundation of the product and remains one of the most transparent email discovery tools available. Enter any company domain and Hunter returns a list of email addresses associated with that domain, along with the email pattern the company uses (such as first.last@ or f.last@), confidence scores for each address, and the public sources where each email was found. This transparency lets reps assess data quality before sending a single email. Lusha takes a different approach: rather than mapping an entire domain, it reveals individual contact cards one at a time when you visit a LinkedIn profile or company page. Lusha does not expose email patterns or source attribution in the same way. For teams that build prospecting lists by targeting entire companies and need to find every reachable contact at a domain, Hunter's domain search workflow is significantly faster than revealing contacts individually through Lusha's extension.

Phone numbers and direct dials. This is Lusha's defining advantage. Lusha's database includes direct dial phone numbers and mobile numbers alongside email addresses, and phone data is available across all paid plans starting at $36 per user per month. For sales development teams that rely on cold calling as a primary outreach channel, having verified phone numbers attached to every contact reveal eliminates the separate step of sourcing phone data through another vendor. Hunter.io does not provide phone numbers at all — the platform is focused exclusively on email discovery and verification. Teams that prospect primarily through email and do not cold call will not miss this feature, but teams with a multichannel outreach strategy that includes phone touches will find Lusha's phone data a meaningful addition to their workflow.

Email verification and deliverability focus. Hunter.io includes a dedicated email verification engine that checks addresses against SMTP servers, detects catch-all domains, identifies disposable emails, and returns a deliverability confidence score. Verification is available as a standalone feature on all plans, including bulk verification via CSV upload on the Starter plan at $34 per month with 500 verifications included. Users on G2 (Mar 2026) consistently highlight Hunter's verification accuracy as a reason they chose the platform over alternatives. Lusha validates that email addresses are formatted correctly and exist, but does not offer the same depth of SMTP-level verification or deliverability scoring. For teams where bounce rates directly impact sender reputation and domain health, Hunter's verification engine provides an extra layer of protection that Lusha does not match.

API-first design vs browser-extension-first design. Hunter.io was built as an API-first platform, which means developers and revenue operations teams can integrate email finding and verification directly into CRM workflows, lead routing systems, and custom prospecting tools using the REST API available on all paid plans. The API supports domain search, email finder, email verification, and account management endpoints with clear documentation and transparent rate limits. Lusha's API is available on the Pro plan at $59 per user per month and above, and Lusha's primary interface is the browser extension that overlays on LinkedIn and company websites. Teams with technical resources who want to automate enrichment at scale will find Hunter's API-first approach more flexible and cost-effective. Teams whose reps prospect manually through LinkedIn will find Lusha's extension-first design more natural for their daily workflow.

Strengths and Weaknesses at a Glance

After testing both prospecting tools across domain search, LinkedIn contact reveal, bulk enrichment, email verification, and API integration workflows, here is where each platform delivers genuine value and where it falls short. These assessments reflect hands-on usage with real prospecting data across multiple industries, not feature-list comparisons from vendor marketing pages.

✓ Hunter.io Strengths

  • Domain search with source transparency: Every discovered email includes the public source URL where it was found and a confidence score, so reps can assess data quality before adding contacts to outreach sequences
  • Built-in email verification engine: SMTP-level verification with bulk CSV support catches invalid addresses, disposable emails, and catch-all domains before they damage your sender reputation
  • Transparent credit system with generous free tier: The free plan includes 25 monthly searches and 50 verifications at no cost, and paid plans offer predictable per-search pricing without per-user fees
  • API-first architecture for automation: Full REST API on all paid plans enables custom integrations with CRMs, enrichment pipelines, and lead routing systems without relying on the browser extension

✗ Hunter.io Weaknesses

  • No phone numbers in the dataset: Hunter focuses exclusively on email, which means teams with multichannel outreach strategies need a second tool for phone data sourcing
  • Domain search misses newer or smaller companies: Coverage depends on public web indexing, so startups and small businesses with limited online presence may return few or no results
  • LinkedIn integration is limited: The browser extension works on any website but does not offer the deep LinkedIn profile overlay that competitors like Lusha provide for one-click contact reveal
  • Credit consumption can be unpredictable: Domain searches against large companies consume one credit but may return dozens of contacts, while searches against small domains return one or two results for the same credit cost

Lusha's strengths center on the completeness of its contact data and the simplicity of its LinkedIn-first prospecting experience, but the platform carries tradeoffs around bulk operations, verification depth, and pricing structure that teams should weigh against their specific workflow requirements and outreach channel mix.

✓ Lusha Strengths

  • Phone numbers and direct dials included: Every contact reveal can include mobile numbers and direct lines alongside email, making Lusha a single source for multichannel prospecting data
  • LinkedIn extension is best-in-class: One-click contact reveal from LinkedIn profiles and company pages fits the daily workflow of reps who prospect through social selling with minimal friction
  • Compliance-focused data sourcing: Lusha emphasizes GDPR and CCPA compliance with opt-out mechanisms and data sourcing transparency, as noted in reviews on Capterra (Feb 2026)
  • CRM auto-enrichment on higher plans: The Premium and Scale plans push contact data directly into Salesforce and HubSpot records, reducing manual data entry for reps working high-volume pipelines

✗ Lusha Weaknesses

  • Per-user pricing escalates quickly: At $36 per user per month on the Pro plan, a 10-person SDR team pays $360 monthly before factoring in credit overages for high-volume prospecting
  • No dedicated email verification engine: Lusha validates email format and existence but does not offer the SMTP-level deliverability scoring or bulk verification workflow that Hunter provides
  • Bulk operations are credit-intensive: One-at-a-time enrichment through the browser extension works well for individual prospecting but burns through credits rapidly when building large target lists
  • API access gated behind higher tiers: The API requires the Pro plan at $59 per user per month, which prices out smaller teams that want to automate enrichment in their tech stack

Our Verdict

Hunter.io is the better choice for teams that prospect by domain, need bulk email enrichment, and prioritize deliverability through built-in verification. Lusha is the better choice for teams that prospect through LinkedIn, need phone numbers alongside emails, and want a compliance-focused data source for multichannel outreach.

The decision comes down to how your team finds and reaches prospects. If your workflow starts with a list of target companies and your reps need to find every reachable email address at those companies, Hunter.io's domain search returns results in seconds with source attribution and confidence scores that let you assess quality before sending. The built-in verification engine protects your sender domain by catching bad addresses before they trigger bounces. The transparent credit model with no per-user fees means your cost scales with usage, not headcount, which favors larger teams that want to centralize email discovery in a single tool.

If your workflow starts on LinkedIn and your reps build pipeline by identifying individual decision-makers, sending connection requests, and then following up by phone and email, Lusha's browser extension surfaces the contact data they need without leaving the platform. The inclusion of direct dial phone numbers is a genuine differentiator for teams that cold call — no other prospecting tool in this price range delivers phone and email data in a single reveal with comparable accuracy, as noted by reviewers on TrustRadius (2025). The compliance posture matters for organizations selling into EU markets or regulated industries where data provenance is a procurement requirement.

For teams that are genuinely torn, consider starting with both free tiers. Hunter.io's free plan includes 25 domain searches and 50 email verifications per month, while Lusha's free plan includes 50 email credits and 5 phone credits. Run both against your actual target account list for two weeks, then compare the hit rate, data accuracy, and workflow fit before committing to a paid plan.
