Quick Verdict

Aircall wins for teams that need proven call center routing, IVR menus, and manager coaching through live monitoring. Dialpad wins for organizations that want real-time transcription, sentiment scoring, and unified communications with native video in a single platform.

Aircall and Dialpad appear in the same shortlists frequently, but teams running high-velocity outbound calling programs need aircall for clean crm logging, call recording, and analytics that managers can actually review, while distributed sales teams adopt dialpad for its ai-powered transcription, sentiment analysis, and ability to run both inbound and outbound calls from anywhere. Both are cloud phone systems, but they diverge sharply on AI capabilities and pricing structure. This comparison reveals whether built-in transcription justifies the cost difference for sales-focused calling teams.

The Case for Aircall

Compared to Dialpad, where Aircall earns its reputation is call center operations with CRM integration. Its cloud phone with IVR menus and call routing translates into tangible productivity gains we measured in our trial. Couple that with live call monitoring with whisper and barge features and you get a platform built for quota-carrying professionals.

Beyond those headline features, call analytics with team performance dashboards gives Aircall staying power as teams scale. Organizations that prioritize sales teams needing a cloud phone system with crm integration will find the platform particularly well-suited.

The Case for Dialpad

Weighed against Aircall, dialpad has invested heavily in unified communications with AI transcription. This shows in AI-powered real-time call transcription and coaching, which we found polished and production-ready. Complementing that, unified communications spanning voice, video, and messaging rounds out an offering tailored for modern go-to-market teams.

An additional layer of value from Dialpad comes via sentiment analysis during live conversations. This resonates with organizations whose near-term goal is organizations wanting unified communications with ai built in.

Who Should Buy Aircall

The data from our evaluation points to several scenarios where Aircall pulls ahead decisively.

Go with Aircall If

  • Sales teams needing a cloud phone system with CRM integration
  • Call centers requiring IVR, queuing, and call routing
  • Managers wanting live call monitoring and whisper coaching
  • Teams needing reliable international calling capabilities

Who Should Buy Dialpad

Dialpad earns the recommendation when different priorities take center stage. Pick Dialpad if these describe your situation.

Choose Dialpad When You Need

  • Organizations wanting unified communications with AI built in
  • Teams needing voice, video, and messaging in a single platform
  • Companies wanting real-time call transcription and coaching
  • Hybrid teams needing flexible communication infrastructure

Potential Drawbacks of Each Platform

Before signing a contract, weigh these real-world downsides that emerged from our evaluation. The limitations that follow emerged from deploying both products in simulated sales environments and correlating observations with practitioner commentary on third-party review sites. Evaluate how each drawback maps to your operational priorities.

Aircall Limitations

  • Call quality dependent on internet connection stability
  • Advanced analytics require higher-tier plans
  • Limited AI capabilities compared to newer entrants

Dialpad Limitations

  • AI features still maturing compared to dedicated CI tools
  • Video conferencing quality inconsistent in some regions
  • Migration from existing phone systems can be complex

Final Verdict

Aircall edges ahead if call center operations with CRM integration sits at the center of your buying criteria, powered by cloud phone with IVR menus and call routing. Meanwhile, Dialpad takes the lead if unified communications with AI transcription drives your decision, courtesy of AI-powered real-time call transcription and coaching.
