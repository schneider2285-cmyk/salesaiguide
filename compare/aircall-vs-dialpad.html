Two Different Philosophies for Sales Calling

Aircall and Dialpad both serve sales teams that depend on phone conversations, but they approach the problem from fundamentally different directions. Aircall is a traditional cloud call center platform built around carrier-grade voice infrastructure, call center routing, and manager oversight tools like live monitoring with whisper and barge capabilities. Its strength is handling high-volume inbound and outbound calling with reliable queue management and deep CRM screen pop integrations that display caller context the moment a connection is made. Teams that run structured phone operations with IVR menus, ring groups, and warm transfer workflows between reps will find Aircall's architecture purpose-built for that motion.

Dialpad takes an AI-native approach where every conversation produces structured intelligence by default. Real-time transcription runs on every call without additional configuration, Ai Moments detect topics and action items as the conversation unfolds, and sentiment scoring tracks the emotional arc of each interaction so managers can intervene or coach based on data rather than guesswork. Dialpad also bundles unified communications with native video conferencing and team messaging under one license, which appeals to organizations trying to reduce vendor count across their communication stack. The core question for buyers is whether they need a proven call center backbone with international numbers in over one hundred countries, or an AI-first platform where every conversation generates searchable, actionable intelligence within seconds of hanging up. Key Differences That Matter

Call center routing depth versus AI intelligence breadth. Aircall's call center routing capabilities are meaningfully deeper than what Dialpad offers on its base plans. Aircall provides configurable multi-level IVR menus, skills-based distribution that sends high-value prospects to senior closers, ring groups organized by team or geography, and queue management with overflow rules that prevent missed calls during peak volume. These features matter for organizations that handle significant inbound call traffic alongside outbound prospecting. Dialpad includes basic IVR and routing through its contact center module, but teams that need advanced queue management and warm transfer workflows between specialized agent groups will find Aircall's routing engine more capable without requiring an enterprise-tier upgrade. According to G2 reviewer feedback (Mar 2026), Aircall's call routing flexibility is consistently cited as a top strength by mid-market support and sales teams.

Real-time transcription and Ai Moments as a default layer. Dialpad's most significant differentiator is that AI transcription runs on every call from the fifteen-dollar-per-user Standard plan onward without requiring activation or additional licensing. Every conversation produces a searchable transcript with speaker labels, timestamps, and automatically extracted action items within seconds of the call ending. Ai Moments detect specific topics, competitor mentions, and pricing discussions in real time, triggering coaching content to appear on the rep's screen while the prospect is still talking. Aircall does not include native transcription or AI call analysis and relies on third-party integrations with tools like Gong or Fireflies.ai to provide post-call intelligence. For teams that value conversation data as a strategic asset, this difference is substantial rather than incremental.

Unified communications versus voice-only focus. Dialpad bundles native video conferencing, team messaging, and business SMS alongside voice calling under a single license. Sales reps can escalate a phone call to a video meeting with the same prospect without switching platforms, and the AI transcription carries across both channels. Aircall is a voice-only platform by design. It does not include video conferencing, team messaging, or native SMS capabilities, which means teams using Aircall still need Zoom or Google Meet for video, Slack for internal messaging, and a separate SMS tool if text outreach is part of their workflow. Organizations trying to consolidate their communication stack into fewer vendors will find Dialpad's unified approach reduces both licensing costs and the friction of switching between disconnected tools throughout the selling day.

Sentiment scoring and AI coaching versus human-driven monitoring. Dialpad tracks sentiment scoring across every call in real time, flagging moments where prospect engagement shifts negative so managers can review the exact transcript segment that caused the drop. AI coaching cards surface battlecards and objection responses during live conversations based on natural language processing of what the prospect is saying. Aircall takes a human-driven approach to coaching: managers join active calls through live monitoring, deliver whisper coaching advice that only the rep hears, or barge into conversations when senior involvement is needed. Both models work, but they serve different management styles. Aircall's approach requires managers to be actively listening during calls. Dialpad's approach scales coaching through AI so managers review aggregated data and intervene selectively based on sentiment trends across the full team.

International numbers and global presence dialing. Aircall provisions local and toll-free numbers in over one hundred countries, which gives it a clear advantage for sales organizations that sell into multiple geographies and depend on local presence dialing to improve answer rates. Number management happens from a centralized admin dashboard where team leads assign and port numbers without contacting carriers. Dialpad offers international numbers in approximately seventy countries according to their pricing page (Mar 2026), which covers major markets but leaves gaps for teams selling into regions across Africa, the Middle East, or parts of Southeast Asia where Aircall has broader carrier relationships. Teams with significant international dialing requirements should verify country-specific availability on both platforms before committing.

Implementation and Onboarding

Aircall setup for structured call operations. Aircall deploys in under a day for basic outbound dialing, but teams that need full inbound call center routing with IVR menus, ring groups, and skills-based distribution should budget two to three days for initial configuration. The visual call flow editor lets admins build multi-level routing trees without engineering support, and CRM screen pop connections with Salesforce or HubSpot require the fifty-dollar-per-user Professional plan. The three-user minimum on all plans means the entry cost is at least ninety dollars per month before placing a single call, which makes Aircall a poor fit for solo reps or two-person teams. According to Capterra reviewers (Feb 2026), most teams complete full onboarding including CRM integration and call flow configuration within the first week.

Dialpad setup for AI-first workflows. Dialpad's initial deployment is faster for teams that primarily need voice calling with transcription because the AI features activate by default on every call without additional configuration. A team of five reps can be making transcribed, sentiment-tracked calls within fifteen minutes of account creation on the Standard plan at fifteen dollars per user per month. The complexity increases when teams want to activate the AI Sales Center add-on for real-time coaching cards, because the coaching system requires building out a battlecard library, configuring keyword triggers, and populating objection response databases before it delivers useful content during live calls. Teams that skip this setup investment will find the AI coaching feature underwhelming. CRM integration with Salesforce or HubSpot requires the twenty-five-dollar Pro tier, so teams on Standard need to account for the upgrade cost if CRM sync is essential to their workflow.

Pricing Breakdown

Aircall pricing tiers and minimums. Aircall's Essentials plan starts at thirty dollars per user per month with a three-user minimum and includes unlimited inbound calls, basic IVR, call recording, and integrations with HubSpot and Pipedrive. The Professional plan at fifty dollars per user per month unlocks the power dialer, Salesforce integration, advanced analytics, live call monitoring with whisper and barge, and queue management callbacks. A Custom enterprise tier adds SLA guarantees, unlimited international calling to select destinations, and dedicated account management at negotiated pricing. Outbound minutes are metered on the Essentials tier, which adds variable cost for teams running high-volume outbound campaigns from the entry-level plan.

Dialpad pricing and add-on structure. Dialpad's Standard plan starts at fifteen dollars per user per month billed annually and includes unlimited domestic calling, AI transcription on every call, native video meetings, team messaging, and Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 integration. The Pro plan at twenty-five dollars per user per month adds CRM integrations with Salesforce and HubSpot, international SMS, custom call routing rules, and additional admin controls. Enterprise pricing is custom and includes a one-hundred-percent uptime SLA with dedicated support. The AI Sales Center, which includes real-time coaching cards, AI scorecards, and advanced call analytics, is a separate add-on that costs additional per-seat licensing on top of the Pro or Enterprise base plan. According to G2 reviewers (Mar 2026), teams should budget for the AI Sales Center add-on from the start if real-time coaching is a primary buying criteria, since activating it later requires both budget approval and configuration time.

Cost comparison at team scale. For a ten-person sales team on the plans most relevant to sales workflows, Aircall Professional costs five hundred dollars per month while Dialpad Pro costs two hundred fifty dollars per month, a meaningful difference that compounds as headcount grows. However, Dialpad's AI Sales Center add-on narrows this gap for teams that need the full coaching feature set. Teams should also factor in that Aircall does not include video conferencing or team messaging, so the true cost comparison requires adding Zoom or Google Meet licenses to the Aircall column. The full review of each platform covers pricing details in more depth: see our Aircall review and Dialpad review for complete breakdowns.

Integration and Workflow

CRM integration depth and tier gating. Both platforms integrate natively with Salesforce and HubSpot, but the integration tiers differ. Aircall locks Salesforce integration behind the fifty-dollar Professional plan, while Dialpad gates CRM access at the twenty-five-dollar Pro tier. Aircall's Salesforce connector is notably deep according to TrustRadius feedback (2025), supporting custom object mapping, bi-directional field sync, and advanced workflow triggers that enterprise teams depend on for accurate pipeline reporting. Dialpad's CRM integration pushes AI-generated call summaries and sentiment scores directly to contact records, which adds a layer of intelligence to CRM data that Aircall's basic call logging does not provide. For Salesforce-centric organizations that rely on custom object workflows, Aircall's integration depth may justify the higher per-seat cost. For teams that value automated AI summaries in their CRM records, Dialpad provides that without requiring a separate conversation intelligence tool.

Ecosystem and automation layer. Both tools connect to Zapier and Make for workflow automation, and both offer API access on higher tiers. Aircall's integration marketplace includes over one hundred pre-built connectors according to their integrations page (Mar 2026), covering helpdesk platforms, CRMs, and productivity tools commonly used by sales and support teams. Dialpad's integration count is smaller but includes the unique advantage that AI transcripts and sentiment data flow through integrations automatically, meaning downstream tools receive enriched call data rather than basic call logs. Teams running complex multi-tool workflows where call intelligence feeds into sequencing platforms, deal intelligence dashboards, or custom reporting systems will benefit from evaluating which platform's API exposes the data fields most relevant to their automation requirements.

  • Deep Salesforce integration with custom object mapping and bi-directional sync for enterprise CRM workflows
  • A proven voice infrastructure that scales from ten to two hundred reps without platform migration

When to Choose Dialpad

✓ Choose Dialpad if you need:

  • AI coaching cards that surface battlecards, objection handling, and Ai Moments during live conversations based on what the prospect is saying
  • Unified communications with native video conferencing, team messaging, and SMS under one license to reduce vendor sprawl
  • A lower per-seat entry price that includes AI transcription from the fifteen-dollar Standard plan onward
  • Sentiment trend reporting and AI coaching scorecards that scale manager visibility across the entire team's call activity

Key strengths: Real-time transcription, AI coaching, sentiment scoring, unified communications, native video

Our Verdict

Aircall wins for sales organizations that run structured call center operations where call center routing depth, IVR menus, queue management, and manager-led coaching through live monitoring define the daily workflow. Dialpad wins for teams that prioritize AI-generated call intelligence where real-time transcription, sentiment scoring, and AI coaching cards deliver value on every conversation without requiring managers to listen in person.

The decision often comes down to team size, budget, and communication stack strategy. Aircall's three-user minimum and thirty-dollar entry point make it better suited for teams of ten or more reps that have outgrown budget dialers and need a dedicated voice platform with deep CRM screen pop integration and international numbers across one hundred or more countries. Dialpad's fifteen-dollar starting price with AI transcription included from day one makes it the stronger value for teams of five to fifty reps that want conversation intelligence without layering on a separate tool like Gong, and the unified communications bundle eliminates the need for separate video and messaging licenses. Teams that sell internationally into emerging markets should verify Aircall's broader country coverage meets their dialing needs, while teams that value post-call intelligence and AI-driven coaching at scale will find Dialpad's approach more aligned with how modern sales organizations train and manage reps.

One practical path for growing teams: start with Dialpad if AI transcription and unified communications are higher priorities than advanced call center routing, then evaluate whether the AI Sales Center add-on delivers enough coaching value to justify its additional cost. If your operation depends heavily on structured inbound call flows with warm transfer between specialized groups and you need international numbers in regions Dialpad does not cover, Aircall's infrastructure focus will serve that workflow more reliably from day one.
