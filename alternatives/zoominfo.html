ZoomInfo Alternatives: 5 Tested Options for Sales Prospecting

Quick Summary

ZoomInfo remains a strong enterprise data platform, but its pricing and contract structure push many teams to look elsewhere. After testing five alternatives across real prospecting workflows, Apollo.io stands out as the strongest overall replacement for most teams — it combines a 275M+ contact database with built-in outreach tools starting at $49/user/mo. For teams focused narrowly on email discovery, Hunter.io offers the lowest barrier to entry with a free tier and plans from $34/mo.

Why Teams Switch from ZoomInfo

ZoomInfo built its reputation as the go-to B2B intelligence platform for enterprise sales organizations. But as the market has matured, several friction points drive teams to evaluate alternatives:

  • High cost floor. Annual contracts typically start at $15,000+ with rigid multi-year commitments, putting it out of reach for growing teams.
  • Regional data gaps. Coverage is strongest in North America. Teams prospecting into EMEA, APAC, or Latin America often find thinner contact records and outdated company data.
  • Onboarding complexity. The platform packs intent data, org charts, workflows, and enrichment into a single UI. New reps frequently report a steep learning curve before reaching productivity.
  • Unpredictable credit consumption. Depending on your plan tier, export and reveal credits can burn faster than expected, making monthly budgeting difficult.
  • Feature excess for SMBs. If your team has fewer than 20 reps and runs straightforward outbound sequences, much of ZoomInfo's enterprise feature set goes unused.

The alternatives below address different subsets of these pain points. None replicate ZoomInfo's full feature depth, but each offers a targeted value proposition at a fraction of the cost.

1. Apollo.io — Best All-in-One Platform

Apollo is the closest thing to a full ZoomInfo replacement at a fraction of the price. Its database holds 275M+ contacts with email addresses and phone numbers, and it layers a complete outreach suite on top: email sequences, a built-in dialer, LinkedIn task queues, and workflow automation. That combination means your team can prospect, contact, and track engagement without toggling between tools.

Where Apollo diverges from ZoomInfo is depth vs. breadth. ZoomInfo's org charts and buying committee mapping are more refined for complex enterprise deals. Apollo's intent signals exist but are less granular. However, for teams running outbound at the mid-market level, Apollo's data coverage and built-in sequencing close the gap fast.

Strengths

  • Largest accessible database among ZoomInfo alternatives at 275M+ contacts
  • Built-in email sequencing and dialer eliminates need for separate outreach tools
  • Free tier lets teams evaluate data quality before committing budget
  • Paid plans start at $49/user/mo — roughly 90% less than a typical ZoomInfo contract

Limitations

  • Phone number accuracy trails ZoomInfo's verified direct dials in some verticals
  • Intent data exists but lacks the depth of ZoomInfo's Streaming Intent
  • UI density can overwhelm new users during the first week

2. Seamless.AI — Best for Real-Time Contact Discovery

Seamless.AI takes a different approach than static database providers. Instead of pre-crawled records, it uses AI to find and verify contact information in real time as you search. The result is often fresher data, particularly for contacts who have recently changed roles or companies. Its Chrome extension works directly on LinkedIn, letting reps pull verified emails and phone numbers without leaving their prospecting workflow.

The trade-off is the credit-based model. Each search consumes credits, and heavy prospectors can burn through allocations quickly. The free tier includes 50 credits (Seamless.AI pricing page, Mar 2026), which gives enough room to test data quality but not enough for sustained outbound campaigns.

Strengths

  • Real-time search model surfaces recently updated contact details
  • Strong LinkedIn integration for prospecting inside existing workflows
  • AI-powered search returns results that static databases may miss

Limitations

  • Credit consumption can be hard to predict for high-volume teams
  • No built-in outreach sequencing — requires a separate tool for email campaigns
  • Data quality is inconsistent outside the US market

3. Lusha — Best for Direct Dials and Verified Emails

Lusha focuses on what many sales reps actually need day-to-day: accurate phone numbers and verified email addresses, delivered fast. Its Chrome extension surfaces contact data on LinkedIn and company websites in a single click, and the data accuracy on direct dials has consistently tested well against more expensive platforms in our evaluations.

The pricing model is straightforward: credit-based plans starting at $36/user/mo. That simplicity extends to the product itself. Lusha does not try to be an all-in-one platform. There are no built-in sequences, limited intent data, and no workflow automation. For teams that already have outreach tooling and just need better contact data, that focus is an advantage rather than a gap.

Strengths

  • Direct dial accuracy is consistently high, particularly for North American contacts
  • Simple credit model makes budgeting predictable
  • Lightweight UI means minimal onboarding time for new reps
  • GDPR-compliant data sourcing for teams selling into Europe

Limitations

  • Database size is smaller than Apollo or ZoomInfo
  • No built-in outreach or sequencing capabilities
  • Intent data is basic compared to ZoomInfo or Clearbit

4. Clearbit (Breeze Intelligence by HubSpot) — Best for Enrichment and Intent

Clearbit was acquired by HubSpot in late 2023 and rebranded as Breeze Intelligence, but the core product remains one of the strongest enrichment engines available. Rather than functioning as a prospecting database you search through, Clearbit enriches records already in your CRM — filling in firmographic data, technographic details, and intent signals on accounts you are already tracking.

The HubSpot integration is where Clearbit shines brightest. If your team runs on HubSpot, enrichment happens natively inside your CRM with no CSV exports or manual syncing. Intent data flags accounts visiting your site or researching relevant topics, giving reps prioritization signals they can act on immediately. Pricing is usage-based (Clearbit pricing page, Mar 2026), which works well for teams with defined target account lists but less so for high-volume top-of-funnel prospecting.

Strengths

  • Best-in-class data enrichment for existing CRM records
  • Native HubSpot integration eliminates manual data workflows
  • Intent signals surface in-market accounts automatically
  • Strong firmographic and technographic data quality

Limitations

  • Not a standalone prospecting database — works best as an enrichment layer
  • Strongest value is locked to HubSpot users
  • Usage-based pricing can scale up quickly for large databases
  • No Chrome extension for on-the-fly contact discovery

5. Hunter.io — Best for Email Finding on a Budget

Hunter.io does one thing well: finding and verifying email addresses associated with specific domains. Its domain search returns all known email patterns and verified addresses for a company, and the email verifier checks deliverability before you hit send. For sales teams, freelancers, or agencies that primarily need email addresses rather than full prospect profiles, Hunter offers the fastest path from company name to verified inbox.

The free tier includes 25 searches and 50 verifications per month (Hunter pricing page, Mar 2026), which is enough for light prospecting. Paid plans start at $34/mo for 500 searches. The pricing is per-account rather than per-user, making it economical for small teams. However, Hunter does not provide phone numbers, intent data, or company intelligence — it is strictly an email discovery tool.

Strengths

  • Domain search surfaces all known email patterns for a company instantly
  • Built-in email verification reduces bounce rates before campaigns launch
  • Lowest cost entry point among all alternatives tested
  • Per-account pricing (not per-user) benefits small teams

Limitations

  • Email-only — no phone numbers, direct dials, or company intelligence
  • No intent data or buyer signals
  • Not a full ZoomInfo replacement for teams needing comprehensive prospect profiles

How to Choose the Right ZoomInfo Alternative

The best replacement depends on what drove you away from ZoomInfo in the first place:

  • Switching because of price: Start with Apollo (full platform at $49/user/mo) or Hunter (email-only at $34/mo).
  • Switching because of data quality: Test Lusha for direct dials or Seamless.AI for real-time verified contacts.
  • Switching because you only need enrichment: Clearbit fills CRM gaps without the overhead of a full prospecting platform, especially if you run HubSpot.
  • Switching because of contract rigidity: Apollo and Hunter both offer monthly billing with no annual lock-in on starter plans.

Most teams evaluating ZoomInfo alternatives should trial at least two options with the same target account list. Export 50 contacts from each platform, verify the data against LinkedIn profiles, and measure which tool delivers the highest rate of accurate, current information for your specific market segment.
