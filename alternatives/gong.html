Why Teams Switch from Gong

Gong built the conversation intelligence category and remains the most comprehensive platform for analyzing sales calls, tracking deal momentum, and coaching reps based on real conversation data. But that breadth comes with trade-offs that hit smaller teams hardest.

Per-seat pricing is the most common trigger for teams exploring alternatives. Gong typically costs $100 or more per user per month on annual contracts, with minimum seat requirements that push entry costs into five-figure territory (Gong pricing page, Mar 2026). For a 20-person sales team, that is $24,000 or more per year before implementation and onboarding costs.

Implementation complexity is another friction point. Getting full value from Gong requires CRM integration, call routing configuration, deal board setup, and manager training on the coaching dashboards. Teams report that reaching steady-state usage takes four to eight weeks, during which adoption gaps can undermine the ROI case.

Feature density also works against Gong for teams with narrower needs. If you only need meeting transcription and action items, paying for deal intelligence, forecast tracking, and competitive mention alerts feels like buying the full Adobe Creative Suite when you only need a photo editor. Annual contract lock-in compounds the risk: if the team does not fully adopt the platform within the first quarter, you are still on the hook for eleven more months of payments.

1. Chorus.ai (by ZoomInfo) — Best for ZoomInfo Customers

Chorus.ai is the conversation intelligence platform inside the ZoomInfo ecosystem, and that tight integration is both its greatest strength and its defining constraint. If your team already uses ZoomInfo for prospecting data, adding Chorus means your call recordings, contact enrichment, and deal intelligence all live in one connected system. Prospect data from ZoomInfo flows directly into call context, so reps see firmographic details, org charts, and intent signals before they pick up the phone.

On the conversation intelligence side, Chorus handles the fundamentals well. Call recording, automatic transcription, topic detection, and talk-ratio analysis work reliably across Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet. The coaching scorecards let managers rate calls against custom criteria and track rep improvement over time. Deal intelligence pulls conversation signals into a pipeline view that highlights deals at risk based on engagement patterns.

Pricing is bundled with ZoomInfo subscriptions, typically falling in the $100–150/user/month range (ZoomInfo pricing page, Mar 2026) depending on which ZoomInfo package you choose. This makes Chorus cost-competitive with Gong for teams already paying for ZoomInfo, but expensive as a standalone conversation intelligence purchase since you cannot buy Chorus without ZoomInfo.

Limitations: Chorus requires an active ZoomInfo subscription, which means it is not a viable option for teams that do not need ZoomInfo's contact database. Standalone flexibility is limited compared to Gong. Teams that use competing data providers like Apollo or Lusha will find the forced bundling restrictive rather than convenient.

2. Clari — Best for Revenue Forecasting

Clari approaches the conversation intelligence space from a different angle than Gong. Rather than starting with call recording and building outward, Clari built a revenue operations platform first and added conversation intelligence as one input into its forecasting engine. The result is a tool that is stronger on pipeline inspection and forecast accuracy than on coaching individual reps.

Where Clari stands out is revenue predictability. The platform aggregates signals from CRM activity, email engagement, meeting cadence, and conversation data to generate forecast projections that account for deal slippage, pipeline coverage gaps, and seasonal patterns. Sales leaders who spend hours each week reconciling forecast submissions in spreadsheets find that Clari replaces that manual process with data-driven projections that update in real time.

Pricing follows a custom enterprise model, typically ranging from $50–80/user/month (Clari pricing page, Mar 2026) depending on modules selected and team size. The conversation intelligence module is an add-on rather than the base product, so teams that only want call recording and coaching may find the overall cost hard to justify without adopting the full revenue platform.

Limitations: Conversation intelligence in Clari is a complement to the revenue platform, not the primary product. Teams whose top priority is call coaching, competitive mention tracking, or detailed conversation analytics will find Clari's capabilities thinner than what Gong or Chorus offer in those areas. The enterprise sales process and custom pricing also make Clari harder to evaluate quickly compared to self-serve alternatives.

3. Fireflies.ai — Best for Budget-Conscious Teams

Fireflies.ai occupies the opposite end of the pricing spectrum from Gong, offering meeting transcription, AI summaries, and conversation search starting at $10/user/month (Fireflies pricing page, Mar 2026) on the Pro plan. A free tier with limited transcription credits lets teams test the platform before committing, which stands in stark contrast to Gong's enterprise sales cycle that can take weeks to complete.

The core product handles transcription and summarization well. Fireflies joins video calls automatically, generates transcripts with speaker identification, and produces AI summaries that capture action items, key topics, and follow-up tasks. The search functionality lets you query across all recorded meetings using natural language, so finding that specific pricing discussion from three weeks ago takes seconds rather than scrubbing through recordings manually.

Integration breadth is a genuine advantage. Fireflies connects with over 50 tools including Slack, Notion, Asana, HubSpot, and Salesforce, pushing meeting summaries and action items directly into the systems where your team already works. The Business plan at $19/user/month adds conversation intelligence features like sentiment analysis, talk time metrics, and custom topic tracking.

Limitations: Fireflies is primarily a transcription and meeting intelligence tool, not a full conversation intelligence platform. Deep coaching features like call scorecards, competitive mention tracking, and deal risk alerts are either basic or absent compared to Gong. Teams that need manager-level coaching workflows and deal board analytics will find Fireflies covers the transcription layer but not the strategic sales intelligence layer.

How to Choose the Right Gong Alternative

Picking the right alternative depends on what you actually use Gong for today, or what you would use it for if the price were not a barrier. Start by identifying which layer of conversation intelligence drives the most value for your team, then match that need to the platform built around it.

  • You already use ZoomInfo for prospecting: Chorus.ai adds conversation intelligence directly into your existing ZoomInfo workflow. The bundled pricing means you are not paying for two separate platforms, and the contact-data-to-call-recording pipeline eliminates manual data entry.
  • Forecasting accuracy matters more than call coaching: Clari treats conversation data as one signal feeding a broader revenue intelligence engine. If your VP of Sales spends more time on forecast accuracy than reviewing call recordings, Clari solves the higher-priority problem.
  • You need affordable transcription and summaries: Fireflies.ai delivers reliable meeting transcription, AI summaries, and cross-meeting search at a fraction of Gong's cost. Best for teams that need the recording and summarization layer without the full coaching and deal intelligence stack.

Before committing, record at least two weeks of real sales calls in the new platform alongside your current workflow. Compare transcript accuracy, summary quality, and how quickly reps and managers adopt the new tool. The right platform should reduce post-call admin work, not create a parallel system that people forget to check.
