What Is Close CRM?

Close is a CRM and sales engagement platform designed from the ground up for inside sales teams that spend most of their day calling, emailing, and texting leads. Founded in 2013 by Steli Efti and the Elastic team, Close took the position that a CRM should not just store contact data but should be the actual workspace where outreach happens. That design philosophy means calling, SMS, and email sequences are built directly into the platform rather than requiring separate subscriptions to tools like Aircall, RingCentral, or Salesloft. For SMB sales teams and startups, this consolidation removes both the cost overhead of multiple tool subscriptions and the workflow friction of switching between applications throughout the day. According to Close's own positioning, the platform is built for teams that want speed, clarity, and results without the bloat of enterprise CRMs like Salesforce.

The core value proposition is communication-first CRM design. Where HubSpot and Pipedrive treat calling as an add-on or integration, Close treats it as a primary feature. Reps can make calls using the built-in Power Dialer, send email sequences, and text leads from the same interface where they manage their pipeline and track deal stages. Every interaction is automatically logged to the lead record without manual data entry. This auto-logging means managers can audit rep activity from the CRM directly, seeing call counts, email open rates, and response metrics alongside pipeline data. The platform also includes Smart Views, which are saved filtered lists of leads based on any combination of fields and activity data, giving reps always-current calling lists without running manual reports. For teams that previously managed their CRM in one tab and their dialer or email tool in another, Close merges those workflows into a single environment that reduces context switching and keeps reps focused on selling activities rather than administrative tasks.

Key Features We Tested

✓ Built-In Calling and Power Dialer

Close includes native VoIP calling on every plan with no per-minute charges for domestic calls. The Power Dialer on the Growth plan ($99/mo) automatically queues the next lead when a call ends, keeping reps moving through lists without manual dialing. Call recording captures every conversation for coaching and compliance review. One-click voicemail drops let reps leave pre-recorded messages instantly instead of waiting through voicemail greetings. AI-generated call summaries on higher tiers transcribe and summarize conversations automatically, eliminating post-call note-taking that eats into selling time.

✓ Predictive Dialer

Available on the Scale plan ($139/mo), the Predictive Dialer calls multiple leads simultaneously and connects the rep only when someone answers a live call. This eliminates time spent listening to rings, busy signals, and voicemail greetings, which is significant for teams making over fifty calls per rep per day where idle dialing time compounds quickly into hours of lost productivity. The system detects answering machines and skips them automatically, routing only live human connections to the rep for maximum talk time per session.

✓ Email Sequences and Two-Way Sync

Reps can build multi-step email sequences with customizable timing, delay intervals, and conditional logic directly inside Close without needing a separate email automation tool. Two-way email sync with Gmail and Outlook ensures every sent and received email appears on the lead record automatically, so managers see the complete conversation history without relying on reps to log messages. Templates with merge fields let teams standardize outreach messaging while preserving personalization, and sequence performance metrics show open and reply rates per step.

✓ Native SMS Messaging

Close supports sending and receiving SMS messages from within the CRM, logged directly to the lead timeline alongside calls and emails for a complete multichannel conversation record. For inside sales teams that use text as a follow-up channel after missed calls or unopened emails, this eliminates the need for a separate messaging tool or personal phone usage. SMS messages can be sent individually or incorporated into automated workflows, and incoming replies trigger notifications so reps respond quickly without monitoring a separate messaging platform.

✓ Smart Views and Pipeline Management

Smart Views are saved dynamic filters that create live lead lists based on any combination of lead fields, activity data, and custom attributes. Reps use these as always-updated calling queues that refresh automatically as lead data changes throughout the day. Pipeline management supports multiple visual pipelines with customizable stages, letting teams track different products or sales motions separately. Deals link directly to lead records for full context on every opportunity, and drag-and-drop stage movement keeps pipeline updates fast without form-heavy data entry.

✓ Custom Reporting and Activity Dashboards

Close provides activity-based reporting that tracks calls made, emails sent, SMS messages, response rates, and pipeline movement across the team in real time. Managers can view individual rep performance and overall team metrics from a single dashboard without assembling data from multiple tools. Custom reports combine activity data with deal outcomes for revenue attribution analysis, and leaderboard views create visibility into who is hitting targets. Reports can be filtered by date range, rep, and lead source for targeted performance reviews.

What Close Gets Right (and Wrong)

After testing Close across several weeks of active inside sales workflows including cold calling campaigns, email sequence deployment, pipeline management, and team reporting, here is an honest assessment of where Close delivers genuine value and where it falls short. These findings come from hands-on use with real prospect lists and live calling sessions, not from marketing demos or sandbox environments. We evaluated Close specifically against the needs of SMB inside sales teams making high-volume outbound calls alongside multichannel email and SMS follow-up. The comparison baseline includes HubSpot Sales Hub, Pipedrive, and standalone calling tools that teams commonly pair with less communication-focused CRMs.

✓ What Works Well

  • Calling is genuinely built in, not bolted on: Unlike HubSpot or Pipedrive where calling requires third-party integrations or add-on fees, Close includes VoIP calling on every plan. The Power Dialer keeps reps moving through call lists with minimal dead time, and call data logs automatically without reps needing to create manual activity records after each conversation
  • Multichannel outreach from one workspace: Having calling, email sequences, and SMS in the same interface where pipeline data lives eliminates the context switching that slows reps down. According to user reviews on TrustRadius, teams consistently report that Close replaced multiple tools and reduced total cost even though individual seat prices are higher than basic CRMs
  • Fast setup and minimal admin overhead: Close is functional within hours of signing up. Import a CSV, configure a pipeline, and reps are calling from the platform the same day. There is no weeks-long implementation project or administrator certification required, which matters for startups and lean teams without dedicated sales operations staff
  • Smart Views create dynamic prospecting lists: Instead of running manual reports or building static lists, Smart Views use saved filters that update automatically as lead data changes. Reps always work from current lists without needing a manager to refresh their calling queues each morning

✗ Key Limitations

  • Marketing features are nearly nonexistent: Close is a sales tool, not a marketing-and-sales platform. There are no landing pages, marketing email tools, content management, or lead scoring based on website behavior. Teams that need marketing automation alongside CRM will still need HubSpot Marketing Hub or a separate platform, which limits Close to organizations where marketing and sales tooling are already separated
  • Reporting is functional but not deep: Activity dashboards cover calls, emails, and pipeline movement adequately for SMB teams. However, advanced analytics like revenue forecasting, deal velocity trending, and custom report builders with visualization options are limited compared to what Salesforce or HubSpot Enterprise offer. Teams that rely on complex sales analytics will find the ceiling quickly
  • Integration ecosystem is narrower than competitors: Close offers around one hundred native integrations plus Zapier connectivity, which covers common tools. But the integration library is substantially smaller than HubSpot or Salesforce marketplaces. Niche tools or industry-specific platforms may require custom API work. User reviews on TrustRadius confirm that limited integrations are a recurring friction point
  • Per-seat pricing adds up fast for growing teams: At $99 per user per month for the Growth plan that includes the Power Dialer, a fifteen-person team pays nearly eighteen thousand dollars annually. While this often replaces separate CRM plus dialer costs, teams evaluating Close against free-tier HubSpot CRM with a basic dialer add-on will see sticker shock on the headline price

Gotchas and Limitations to Watch For

Power Dialer and Predictive Dialer are gated behind higher tiers. The built-in calling available on Essentials ($35/mo) is manual click-to-call only. To get the Power Dialer that automatically queues calls, teams need the Growth plan at $99 per user per month. The Predictive Dialer that calls multiple leads simultaneously requires the Scale plan at $139 per user per month. Teams that sign up expecting automated dialing on the base plan will be disappointed, as the feature that most differentiates Close from competitors like Pipedrive is locked behind a significant price jump.

The Solo plan caps leads at ten thousand. Close offers a $9 per month Solo plan that looks attractive for individual users, but the ten-thousand-lead cap makes it unsuitable for anyone doing high-volume prospecting. Once you outgrow it, you jump directly to $35 per month for Essentials, which is a nearly four-times price increase. This is not a meaningful free-tier alternative in the way that HubSpot's free CRM operates for early-stage companies.

No free plan exists for team evaluation. Close offers a fourteen-day free trial but does not have a permanently free tier. HubSpot provides free CRM with unlimited users, and Pipedrive has been aggressive with trial extensions. For teams that want to evaluate a CRM with real data over several months before committing, the two-week trial window is tight, especially for organizations with longer sales cycles where the value of a CRM becomes apparent over months rather than days.

International calling costs are additional. While domestic calling is included, international calls incur per-minute charges that vary by country. Teams selling globally need to factor these costs into their budget calculations. The pricing page lists domestic calling as included but routes international rate details to a separate support document, which can catch teams off guard during month-end billing.

Custom object support is limited. Close operates on a lead-centric data model with leads, contacts, opportunities, and activities. It does not support fully custom objects the way Salesforce or HubSpot Enterprise do. Teams with complex data structures that need to track entities beyond standard sales objects, such as product inventory, project records, or custom operational data, will find the schema restrictive for their use case.

When to Choose Close: Scenarios and Use Cases

✓ Right Fit For:

  • Inside sales teams of 3-30 reps whose daily workflow centers on phone calls and email follow-ups, and who want calling built into the CRM rather than paying for and managing a separate dialer tool alongside their pipeline software
  • Startups and SMBs that need a production-ready CRM with multichannel outreach capability on day one without a lengthy setup process or requiring a dedicated administrator to configure the system before reps can start using it
  • Teams currently using a basic CRM like a spreadsheet or lightweight tool alongside a separate phone system who want to consolidate into a single platform that handles calling, email sequences, SMS, and pipeline tracking together
  • Sales organizations where managers need to audit rep activity through call logs, email metrics, and pipeline movement from one dashboard without assembling reports from multiple disconnected tools

✗ Not the Right Tool If:

  • You need marketing automation, lead scoring based on website behavior, landing pages, or content management alongside your CRM. Close is a sales-only tool and teams requiring an integrated marketing-and-sales platform should evaluate HubSpot instead
  • Your sales team exceeds fifty reps and requires enterprise features like advanced revenue forecasting, territory management, CPQ integration, or a large ecosystem of third-party app integrations from a marketplace
  • Your deal cycle is heavily field-sales or relationship-driven with minimal phone outreach, where calling features provide little value and a CRM focused on deal stage tracking and relationship mapping like Pipedrive would serve better
  • You need a free CRM to start with and plan to expand over time. Close does not have a free tier, and its lowest plan caps leads at ten thousand, making it less suitable for teams that want to grow into a paid plan gradually

How Close Compares to Alternatives

Close vs HubSpot Sales Hub is the most common comparison for SMB teams evaluating CRM options. HubSpot offers a free CRM tier with broad functionality and scales into marketing, service, and operations hubs for all-in-one business management. Close wins on communication depth: its built-in calling with Power and Predictive Dialers, native SMS, and email sequences in a single workspace give inside sales teams a more streamlined daily workflow than HubSpot, which requires add-ons or integrations for comparable calling capability. HubSpot wins on breadth, ecosystem size, and scalability beyond sales. For a full breakdown, see our Close vs HubSpot comparison.

Close vs Pipedrive comes down to communication versus visual pipeline focus. Pipedrive emphasizes deal-centric visual pipeline management and is popular with field sales and relationship-driven teams. Close emphasizes outbound activity volume with its calling and email infrastructure. Teams that make more than twenty calls per rep per day will get more value from Close. Teams that manage complex deal stages with multiple stakeholders and fewer but higher-touch interactions may prefer Pipedrive's pipeline visualization and deal management interface. See our Close vs Pipedrive comparison for more detail.

Close vs Salesloft represents different approaches to sales engagement. Salesloft is a dedicated sales engagement platform designed to layer on top of an existing CRM like Salesforce, providing cadences, call recording, and analytics for enterprise revenue teams. Close combines the CRM and engagement layer into one product. For SMB teams that do not already have Salesforce, Close eliminates the need for a CRM plus engagement platform stack. For enterprise teams already running Salesforce, Salesloft integrates more deeply with existing infrastructure. The decision hinges on whether you want one combined tool or best-of-breed components that integrate through APIs and native connectors.

Final Verdict

★★★★☆
4.3/5 — SAG editorial score

Close is the best CRM for inside sales teams that want calling, email, and SMS built directly into their pipeline management tool without assembling a multi-vendor tech stack.

Its Power Dialer and Predictive Dialer give high-volume calling teams genuine productivity advantages that no other CRM at this price range matches natively. The combination of built-in VoIP calling with no per-minute domestic charges, email sequences with two-way sync, native SMS, and Smart Views for dynamic lead list management creates a unified sales workspace that eliminates the tool switching and data fragmentation that plagues teams running separate CRM and dialer applications. The trade-off is clear and intentional: Close does not try to be a marketing platform, an enterprise analytics suite, or a customer service hub. It stays focused on the daily workflow of inside sales reps who need to make calls, send follow-ups, and move deals through a pipeline. For SMB teams of three to thirty reps where outbound calling is a core activity, Close delivers that workflow better than any other CRM we have tested. Teams that need marketing automation should pair Close with a dedicated marketing tool or evaluate HubSpot for an all-in-one approach. Teams that outgrow Close's reporting and need enterprise analytics will naturally move toward Salesforce. But for the inside sales use case where communication volume and speed matter most, Close earns its position as the top-performing CRM for the teams it was built to serve.
