What Is Close?

Close is a CRM built specifically for inside sales teams where calling is the primary outreach channel. Unlike HubSpot or Pipedrive that treat calling as an add-on, Close was designed from day one around making and tracking calls — with a built-in VoIP dialer, power dialer, predictive dialer, and call recording natively in the CRM. The result is a tool where reps can call, email, and SMS from one interface without juggling multiple tools.

Key Features

Built-in Calling (VoIP)

Full-featured VoIP dialer integrated directly in the CRM. Click-to-call from any contact record, call recording, voicemail drop, and local presence numbers included.

Power Dialer

Automatically dials through a list of contacts without rep manually clicking call each time. Dramatically increases call volume for SDRs working high-volume lists.

SMS & Email

Two-way SMS and email directly in the platform. Sequence templates for automated follow-up. All communication history in one contact timeline.

Pipeline Automation

Automated pipeline stage transitions, task creation, and follow-up sequences triggered by rep actions or time-based rules.

Smart Views

Saved filtered views that surface the right leads at the right time. Hot leads, overdue follow-ups, and custom segments all accessible with one click.

Reporting

Activity leaderboards, call analytics, response rates, and pipeline performance dashboards. Built for sales managers who need visibility into rep activity.

Pros & Cons

What's Great

  • Best built-in dialer of any CRM — no separate dialer tool needed
  • Power dialer and predictive dialer dramatically increase call volume
  • All communication (call, email, SMS) in one contact timeline
  • Excellent for teams where calling is the primary outreach activity
  • Transparent pricing — easy to calculate total cost upfront

What Could Be Better

  • Less ecosystem breadth than HubSpot for marketing integration
  • Primarily optimized for calling — less strong if email is your primary channel
  • Reporting less sophisticated than Salesforce for large orgs
  • Power/predictive dialer locked to Professional plan at $99/seat

Who It's For

Perfect For

  • Inside sales teams where calling is the primary daily activity
  • SDR teams doing high-volume cold calling who need a power dialer
  • Teams tired of paying for a separate CRM + separate dialer
  • Small-to-mid revenue teams (5-100 reps) wanting simple, call-centric CRM

Skip If You

  • Primarily email-based outbound teams (Instantly + HubSpot is better value)
  • Enterprise teams needing advanced marketing automation and analytics
  • Teams on the HubSpot ecosystem who would lose integration benefits switching

Limitations & Watch-Outs

No tool is perfect. Here are the downsides we found during testing.

  • Limited third-party app marketplace compared to larger CRMs
  • Reporting capabilities less advanced than enterprise platforms
  • Email and calling features good but not best-in-class individually
  • Scaling beyond 50+ reps may require migrating to larger CRM

When to Choose Close: Use Cases

Close works best in specific scenarios where its strengths align with your team's needs.

  • SMB sales teams wanting built-in calling and email in their CRM
  • Startups needing a fast-to-deploy, all-in-one sales platform
  • Inside sales teams wanting power dialing integrated with pipeline
  • Small teams preferring simplicity over enterprise complexity
