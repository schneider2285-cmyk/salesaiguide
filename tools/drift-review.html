Drift is a conversational sales platform built around AI chatbots, live chat, and automated meeting booking. Acquired by Salesloft in 2023, Drift now operates as Salesloft's conversational layer. It remains a strong standalone option for enterprise teams that want to convert website visitors into pipeline without forms.

What Is Drift?

Drift pioneered the "conversational marketing" category, replacing static web forms with AI-powered chatbots that qualify leads, book meetings, and route conversations to the right sales rep in real time. After its acquisition by Salesloft in late 2023, Drift's chat and bot technology became part of Salesloft's broader revenue platform. However, Drift still operates as a distinct product within the Salesloft ecosystem, and many enterprise teams purchase it as their primary inbound engagement tool.

Drift competes most directly with Intercom (which leans more toward customer support) and Qualified (which focuses on enterprise ABM). Drift's advantage is its deep integration with Salesloft's sales engagement workflows, making it particularly strong for teams already using Salesloft for outbound.

Key Features

AI Chatbots

Custom conversational bots that qualify visitors based on firmographic and behavioral data. Bots ask qualifying questions, check against target account lists, and route high-value leads to reps instantly. No coding required for bot builder setup.

Live Chat

Real-time chat with visitor intelligence overlay showing company name, industry, pages viewed, and previous engagement history. Reps can see exactly who they're talking to and tailor their response accordingly.

Meeting Booking

Automated calendar integration that lets qualified visitors book meetings directly from the chat widget. Syncs with Google Calendar and Outlook, respects rep availability rules, and distributes meetings across the team with round-robin logic.

Conversational Landing Pages

Replace traditional landing pages with chat-first experiences. Instead of filling out a form and waiting for a follow-up, visitors engage with a bot immediately on the page. Drift reports this approach increases conversion rates by reducing friction for high-intent buyers.

Account-Based Targeting

Target specific accounts with personalized chat experiences based on firmographic data, ABM lists, or CRM segments. VIP visitors see custom welcome messages and get routed directly to their assigned account executive.

Revenue Orchestration

Post-acquisition integration with Salesloft cadences. Chat conversations can trigger outbound sequences, and engagement data flows into Salesloft's deal intelligence for a unified view of buyer interactions across channels.

Drift does not publish pricing. All plans require a demo and custom quote. Since the Salesloft acquisition, some pricing structures have shifted toward bundled Salesloft + Drift packages.

Pros & Cons

What's Great

  • AI chatbots are genuinely useful for qualifying and routing high-intent visitors
  • Meeting booking directly from chat eliminates form friction and speeds up pipeline
  • Account-based targeting lets you personalize experiences for key accounts
  • Salesloft integration creates a unified inbound + outbound workflow
  • Conversational landing pages are a differentiator no competitor matches well

What Could Be Better

  • Expensive for smaller teams — no self-serve or affordable starter plan
  • Post-acquisition roadmap uncertainty — some features being merged into Salesloft
  • Bot builder has a learning curve for complex conversation flows
  • Reporting could be deeper for attribution and ROI measurement

Who It's For

Perfect For

  • Enterprise sales teams wanting to convert high-intent website traffic without forms
  • Teams already using Salesloft that want a unified inbound + outbound stack
  • ABM-driven organizations targeting named accounts with personalized chat
  • B2B companies with enough website traffic to justify the investment ($2,500+/month)

Skip If You

  • Small teams or startups with limited website traffic (use Intercom or free HubSpot chat)
  • Teams needing primarily customer support chat (Intercom or Zendesk is better)
  • Organizations without budget for enterprise conversational tools
  • Companies that get most leads through outbound, not inbound website visits

Limitations & Watch-Outs

No tool is perfect. Here are the downsides we found during testing.

  • Enterprise pricing puts Drift out of reach for most small and mid-sized teams
  • The Salesloft acquisition adds uncertainty about long-term standalone product roadmap
  • Complex bot flows require significant upfront configuration and testing
  • Attribution reporting does not always clearly show Drift's contribution to closed-won deals

When to Choose Drift: Use Cases

Drift works best in specific scenarios where its strengths align with your team's needs.

  • Enterprise B2B teams with high-traffic websites generating inbound demand
  • ABM programs that need personalized chat experiences for target accounts
  • Sales teams wanting to eliminate form-fill friction and book meetings instantly
  • Salesloft customers looking to add a conversational inbound layer to their outbound stack

Final Verdict

4.4 / 5
Recommended

Drift remains the leading conversational sales platform for enterprise teams that want to convert website visitors into pipeline without traditional forms.

The AI chatbots are genuinely useful (not just gimmicky), the meeting booking integration eliminates real friction, and the Salesloft acquisition adds outbound integration that competitors lack. The main drawback is price: Drift is built for enterprise budgets, and smaller teams should look elsewhere. If you have the traffic and the budget, Drift delivers measurable pipeline impact.
