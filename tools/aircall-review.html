What Is Aircall?

Aircall is a cloud-based business phone system designed for sales and support teams that prioritize call quality, reliability, and deep CRM integration over raw feature breadth. Founded in 2014 in Paris and now operating globally, Aircall has built its reputation on delivering a phone platform that mid-market teams can deploy in a day and scale to hundreds of users without the telephony infrastructure headaches that plague legacy PBX systems or the reliability concerns that follow budget VoIP providers. Unlike tools that bolt calling onto an existing workflow platform, Aircall treats the phone call itself as the core product — investing in carrier-grade infrastructure, HD voice quality, and uptime guarantees that matter when your revenue team depends on the phone for thirty or more dials per rep per day.

The platform serves two distinct workflows under one roof: outbound sales dialing and inbound call center operations. On the outbound side, the power dialer automates sequential calling from CRM contact lists with voicemail drop and disposition tagging. On the inbound side, customizable IVR menus, ring groups, and skills-based routing distribute incoming calls to the right agent without requiring dedicated contact center software. What separates Aircall from competitors like JustCall or Dialpad is its focus on the manager layer — live call monitoring with whisper coaching, barge-in capabilities, and team analytics dashboards give sales leaders real-time visibility into how reps perform on the phone rather than relying solely on post-call recordings. For organizations where the phone channel generates a meaningful share of pipeline, Aircall provides the infrastructure backbone that scales from an initial team of ten reps through a department of two hundred without requiring a platform migration along the way.

Key Features We Tested

✓ Power Dialer

Aircall's power dialer automates outbound calling by pulling contacts directly from connected CRM records and advancing through the list after each call disposition. Reps click once to launch a calling session rather than manually searching for and dialing the next number between conversations. Voicemail drop lets reps leave a pre-recorded message in one click when calls go unanswered, and post-call disposition tags sync back to the CRM automatically so managers can track call outcomes across the team without relying on manual logging.

✓ Call Routing & IVR

Inbound calls are distributed through a configurable routing engine that supports customizable IVR menus, ring groups organized by team or skill set, business hours scheduling across multiple time zones, and overflow rules for when primary agents are unavailable. The visual call flow editor lets admins build multi-level routing trees without engineering support or telephony expertise. Skills-based routing ensures that a high-value prospect calling about enterprise pricing reaches a senior closer rather than cycling through the general support queue first.

✓ Call Recording & Monitoring

Every inbound and outbound call is recorded automatically and stored alongside the associated CRM record for compliance documentation and coaching review. What elevates Aircall above basic recording tools is the live monitoring layer: managers can listen to active calls in real time, whisper coaching advice that only the rep hears during the conversation, or barge into the call directly when a deal requires senior involvement. These capabilities turn call recording from a passive post-call archive into an active, real-time coaching instrument for sales leaders.

✓ CRM Integrations

Native integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive go beyond basic call logging. Aircall displays a caller context card the moment an inbound call connects, showing the contact's deal stage, recent activity, and notes from previous conversations so reps never answer blind. Outbound calls logged to the CRM include duration, disposition, recording links, and timestamps without any manual data entry from reps. The Salesforce integration in particular supports custom object mapping, bi-directional field sync, and advanced workflow triggers that enterprise teams rely on.

✓ Analytics Dashboard

Real-time and historical dashboards surface the operational metrics that sales managers need to run phone-heavy teams effectively: call volume by rep and time period, average wait times for inbound queues, answer rates across the full team, missed call patterns, and talk time distribution. The analytics layer goes beyond vanity volume counts by correlating call activity with actual outcomes, letting managers identify which reps convert at higher rates and where the team loses prospects to extended hold times or missed callbacks.

✓ International Numbers

Aircall provisions local and toll-free numbers in over one hundred countries, which matters for sales teams selling into multiple geographies where local presence directly impacts answer rates. Local presence dialing displays a number matching the prospect's area code on outbound calls, and international toll-free numbers let inbound prospects call without incurring long-distance charges. Number management happens from a centralized admin dashboard where team leads can assign, reassign, and port numbers across reps without contacting a carrier or waiting days for provisioning.

What Aircall Gets Right (and Wrong)

After testing Aircall across multiple weeks of daily outbound dialing sessions, inbound call routing configurations, and CRM sync workflows with a mid-size sales team, here is an honest assessment of where the platform earns its reputation and where it leaves room for improvement. These findings come from actual prospect conversations, live call monitoring sessions, and verified CRM data — not from product demos or marketing claims. We evaluated Aircall against the criteria that matter most to scaling sales organizations: call audio quality, uptime reliability, CRM data fidelity, manager coaching tools, and whether the platform justifies its premium pricing compared to budget alternatives.

✓ What Works Well

  • Call quality and reliability are genuinely best-in-class: Audio clarity on both inbound and outbound calls was consistently excellent during testing, with minimal latency and zero dropped calls across hundreds of dials. This matters because poor call quality erodes prospect trust and wastes connect opportunities that took effort to generate
  • Manager coaching tools create real-time visibility: The combination of live monitoring, whisper coaching, and barge-in gives sales managers an active coaching channel that works during live calls rather than only after the fact. Being able to guide a junior rep through a pricing negotiation in real time changes the coaching dynamic compared to reviewing recordings hours later
  • Salesforce integration depth exceeds most competitors: The Salesforce connector supports custom object mapping, bi-directional field sync, and workflow triggers that go beyond basic activity logging. Teams that run their pipeline through Salesforce get call data embedded in their existing reporting without building custom integrations
  • Scales from ten reps to two hundred without platform migration: Aircall's architecture handles growing teams by adding users, numbers, and routing rules without the degradation that smaller VoIP tools exhibit at scale. Teams that outgrow budget dialers and switch to Aircall typically stay on the platform through their next stage of growth

✗ Key Limitations

  • Three-user minimum increases cost for very small teams: At thirty dollars per user per month for Essentials with a three-user minimum, Aircall's entry cost is ninety dollars per month before a single outbound call. Startups with one or two reps pay for seats they do not use, making JustCall or similar tools a more sensible starting point at lower headcount
  • No native SMS or WhatsApp messaging: Aircall is a phone-first platform that does not include built-in text messaging or WhatsApp integration. Teams that blend voice and text outreach in the same workflow will need a separate tool for messaging or choose a competitor like JustCall that bundles both channels
  • Power dialer locked behind Professional tier: The power dialer, Salesforce integration, and advanced analytics require the fifty-dollar-per-user Professional plan. Teams on Essentials get call recording and IVR but miss the features that drive outbound sales productivity, which means the realistic starting price for sales teams is fifty dollars per seat, not thirty
  • No parallel dialing or AI conversation intelligence: Aircall is a sequential power dialer without parallel dialing capabilities or built-in AI analytics for conversation patterns, sentiment tracking, or real-time coaching prompts. Teams that need maximum connect rates through parallel dialing or AI-powered call analysis will need dedicated tools like Orum or Gong alongside Aircall

When to Choose Aircall: Use Cases

✓ Right Fit For:

  • Mid-market sales teams of ten to two hundred reps where call quality and uptime reliability are non-negotiable because the phone channel generates a significant share of qualified pipeline
  • Sales managers and directors who need live call monitoring with whisper coaching and barge-in to actively develop reps during prospect conversations rather than relying exclusively on post-call review
  • Salesforce-centric organizations that require deep CRM integration with custom object mapping, bi-directional sync, and workflow automation beyond basic activity logging
  • Global sales teams that sell into multiple countries and need local presence numbers in one hundred or more markets managed from a single dashboard without dealing with regional carriers individually

✗ Not the Right Tool If:

  • You are a startup with fewer than five reps and need the lowest possible cost per seat — the three-user minimum and higher per-seat pricing make budget dialers like JustCall a more practical starting point
  • Your outbound workflow depends heavily on SMS, WhatsApp, or multi-channel text messaging alongside voice calls, since Aircall does not include native messaging capabilities
  • You need parallel dialing that connects multiple prospects simultaneously and routes live answers to available reps for maximum hourly connect rates across large SDR teams
  • You want AI-powered conversation intelligence with automatic call scoring, objection detection, and talk pattern analytics built into the phone platform rather than layered on through a separate tool

Final Verdict

Aircall is the cloud phone system that mid-market sales teams choose when call quality, reliability, and Salesforce integration depth matter more than the lowest price per seat.

Its carrier-grade voice infrastructure, live call monitoring with whisper coaching, and native Salesforce integration cover the complete workflow that phone-heavy sales organizations depend on daily: reliable outbound dialing, intelligent inbound routing, real-time manager visibility, and CRM data that stays accurate without manual logging. The trade-off is straightforward — Aircall costs more than budget alternatives and does not include SMS messaging or AI conversation analytics, but it delivers the call quality, uptime, and scaling headroom that growing teams need when the phone is their primary revenue channel. Teams under five reps or those that need multi-channel text messaging alongside voice will find better value starting with JustCall and migrating to Aircall as their phone operation scales.
