What Is Aircall?

Aircall is a cloud-based phone system designed for sales and support teams. It provides a VoIP phone system with built-in CRM integrations, call analytics, and power dialer functionality. Where Close CRM includes calling as part of the CRM, Aircall is a standalone phone system that integrates with your existing CRM — making it ideal for teams already invested in Salesforce or HubSpot who want to add cloud calling without switching their CRM.

Key Features

Cloud VoIP Phone System

Full-featured cloud phone with numbers in 100+ countries, call routing, IVR, voicemail, and call queues. No hardware required — works from desktop app or mobile.

CRM Integration

Native integrations with HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and Zendesk. Calls auto-logged to the right CRM record with duration, recording, and outcome.

Power Dialer

Automatically dial through contact lists with automatic call logging and next-number progression. Increases call volume without manual navigation between records.

Call Analytics

Team and individual call performance dashboards: call volume, talk time, first response time, missed calls, and conversion rates by rep.

Call Recording

Automatic call recording with searchable library. Review calls for coaching, dispute resolution, or training purposes.

Warm Transfer

Transfer calls with context — brief a colleague before transferring the call so the customer doesn't need to repeat themselves.

Pros & Cons

What's Great

  • Best-in-class HubSpot and Salesforce integrations
  • Clean, modern UI that reps actually use
  • Strong international calling coverage
  • Quick setup — live in under an hour

What Could Be Better

  • No built-in CRM — you'll need a separate CRM alongside Aircall
  • Power dialer locked to Professional plan
  • Call analytics less sophisticated than dedicated analytics platforms

Who It's For

Perfect For

  • Teams on HubSpot or Salesforce wanting clean cloud calling integration
  • International teams needing calls to multiple countries
  • Teams replacing on-premise phone systems

Skip If You

  • Teams wanting CRM + calling in one tool (use Close instead)
  • Very small teams where a basic VoIP app suffices

Limitations & Watch-Outs

No tool is perfect. Here are the downsides we found during testing.

  • Call quality dependent on internet connection stability
  • Advanced analytics require higher-tier plans
  • Limited AI capabilities compared to newer entrants
  • International calling rates can add up for global teams

When to Choose Aircall: Use Cases

Aircall works best in specific scenarios where its strengths align with your team's needs.

  • Sales teams needing a cloud phone system with CRM integration
  • Call centers requiring IVR, queuing, and call routing
  • Managers wanting live call monitoring and whisper coaching
  • Teams needing reliable international calling capabilities
