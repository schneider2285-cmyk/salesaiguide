What Is Clearbit?

Clearbit is a B2B data enrichment platform that attaches firmographic, technographic, and demographic data to leads and company records in real time. Founded in 2014, Clearbit built its reputation on a clean API-first approach to enrichment — feed it an email address or domain, and it returns structured data fields covering company size, industry, revenue range, technology stack, and individual role details. In early 2024, HubSpot acquired Clearbit and began integrating its enrichment engine directly into the HubSpot platform under the name Breeze Intelligence. This means that for new customers, Clearbit is no longer a standalone product — it is the data layer powering enrichment, visitor identification, and form optimization inside HubSpot.

The acquisition changed Clearbit's competitive position significantly. Before the HubSpot deal, Clearbit competed head-to-head with tools like ZoomInfo and Clay as a general-purpose enrichment provider that worked with any CRM. Now, Clearbit's deepest value is reserved for HubSpot users who get native enrichment that writes directly to CRM properties without middleware, syncs bidirectionally with workflows and lists, and powers lead scoring with first-party enriched data. Teams running Salesforce or other CRMs can still access Clearbit's API, but the product roadmap and tightest integration are clearly oriented toward the HubSpot ecosystem. For marketing and sales operations teams already invested in HubSpot, this native integration eliminates the integration tax that plagues bolt-on enrichment tools — no Zapier bridges, no CSV imports, no stale data from batch syncs.

Key Features We Tested

✓ Real-Time Data Enrichment

When a new lead enters your database through a form submission, list import, or API call, Clearbit instantly appends firmographic and demographic attributes without any manual lookup. The enrichment covers company size, industry classification, revenue range, technology stack, employee count, and individual role details including seniority level and department. For sales ops teams, this means every lead arrives pre-qualified with the data points needed for ICP scoring and routing decisions, eliminating the lag between capture and qualification that manual enrichment workflows create.

✓ Visitor Identification (Reveal)

Clearbit Reveal identifies anonymous website visitors by matching IP addresses to company records, surfacing the organization name, industry, employee count, and estimated revenue range of companies browsing your site. This powers account-based marketing workflows where the sales team receives alerts when target accounts visit high-intent pages like pricing or case studies. The identification works at the company level rather than individual level, which means you see that someone from a target account visited your site but not which specific person. Marketing teams use Reveal data to personalize website content and trigger outbound sequences when known accounts show buying signals.

✓ Form Shortening

Clearbit pre-fills form fields with data it already knows about the visitor, allowing marketing teams to remove fields like company name, company size, and industry from their lead capture forms. The visitor only sees questions that Clearbit cannot answer from its database, which typically means just name and email. This directly reduces form abandonment rates because shorter forms convert at higher rates. For demand generation teams running paid campaigns, the conversion lift from fewer form fields can meaningfully improve cost per lead without sacrificing the firmographic data that sales needs for qualification and routing.

✓ Lead Scoring and Routing

Enriched data attributes feed directly into lead scoring models that evaluate ICP fit based on company size, industry, revenue range, technology stack, and individual seniority level. Leads that match your ideal customer profile score higher and route to sales faster, while poor-fit leads get deprioritized or sent to nurture sequences automatically. Within HubSpot, this scoring uses native workflow logic rather than external scoring tools, which means the entire qualification and routing process runs inside a single platform. Teams report that automated ICP-based routing cuts average lead response time because reps receive pre-qualified leads matched to their territory or segment.

✓ HubSpot Native Integration

As Breeze Intelligence, Clearbit writes enriched data directly to HubSpot contact and company properties with no middleware layer. Enriched fields are immediately available in HubSpot lists, workflows, reports, and dashboards. This bidirectional sync means that when a contact record updates in HubSpot, the enrichment data stays current, and when Clearbit refreshes its data, those updates propagate into HubSpot automatically. For operations teams, the native integration eliminates the connector maintenance overhead that tools like Zapier or custom API scripts require. The enrichment credits are managed within HubSpot billing, so there is one vendor relationship and one invoice instead of managing a separate enrichment contract.

✓ API and Enrichment Platform

Beyond the HubSpot integration, Clearbit offers REST APIs for custom enrichment workflows that work with any stack. The Enrichment API accepts an email or domain and returns structured JSON with all available data attributes. The Company API and Person API can be called independently for different use cases. Developers use these endpoints for bulk enrichment jobs, real-time enrichment in custom applications, and integration with CRMs outside the HubSpot ecosystem. The API documentation is well-maintained and includes clear rate limit guidance and response schema details, making it accessible for engineering teams building custom data pipelines or enrichment microservices.

What Clearbit Gets Right (and Wrong)

After evaluating Clearbit across real enrichment workflows including inbound lead qualification, ABM visitor identification, and HubSpot CRM data hygiene, here is an honest assessment of where the platform delivers and where it falls short. These findings reflect actual operational usage with production lead volumes, not demo environments or marketing claims. We assessed Clearbit against the criteria that matter most to marketing and sales ops teams: data accuracy, enrichment coverage, integration depth, and whether the tool actually reduces manual work in daily CRM operations.

✓ What Works Well

  • HubSpot integration is genuinely native: Enriched data writes directly to CRM properties without connectors, middleware, or sync delays. Workflows, lists, and reports consume enriched fields as first-class data. This is not a bolt-on integration — it is part of the platform, which eliminates the maintenance burden that third-party enrichment connectors always carry
  • Real-time enrichment eliminates manual lookup: Leads arrive pre-enriched with firmographic data the moment they enter the database. Sales reps no longer spend the first minutes of follow-up researching company size and industry because that data is already populated on the contact record before the lead assignment notification fires
  • Form shortening measurably improves conversion: Removing fields that Clearbit can auto-fill results in shorter forms that convert better. Marketing teams running high-volume demand gen campaigns notice the difference in cost per lead when forms drop from eight fields to three without losing any data quality on the backend
  • Visitor identification enables account-based workflows: Knowing which target accounts are browsing your website — even before they fill out a form — lets marketing trigger alerts and personalized follow-up sequences. This closes the gap between anonymous website traffic and actionable sales intelligence

✗ Key Limitations

  • HubSpot lock-in is now unavoidable for new customers: With legacy standalone plans being sunset, new Clearbit customers must adopt HubSpot to access the full product. Teams running Salesforce, Pipedrive, or other CRMs lose access to the deepest integration features and are limited to API-only access, which narrows the value proposition significantly
  • Single-source enrichment limits data breadth: Unlike Clay, which waterfalls across dozens of providers, Clearbit relies on its own proprietary data. When Clearbit does not have data on a company or contact, there is no fallback — the field returns empty. This single-source model produces cleaner data but lower overall match rates compared to multi-provider orchestration tools
  • Credit-based pricing creates usage anxiety: Enrichment credits deplete with every record processed, and high-volume teams can exhaust their allocation before the billing cycle ends. The credit system encourages conservative usage, which conflicts with the operational goal of enriching every record automatically without manual gatekeeping
  • Visitor identification is company-level only: Reveal identifies the visiting organization but not the specific individual browsing your site. Sales teams still need to determine which person at the identified company to contact, adding a manual research step between identification and outreach that limits the workflow automation potential

When to Choose Clearbit: Use Cases

✓ Right Fit For:

  • HubSpot-centric marketing and sales organizations that want enrichment data flowing directly into CRM properties, workflows, and reporting without managing external connectors or sync schedules
  • Demand generation teams running paid campaigns where form shortening and automatic enrichment reduce cost per lead by improving conversion rates while maintaining firmographic data quality for qualification
  • Revenue operations teams building ICP-based lead scoring and routing models that need real-time enriched attributes to classify and distribute leads the moment they enter the database
  • Account-based marketing programs that need website visitor identification to trigger sales alerts and personalized sequences when target accounts show buying intent on high-value pages

✗ Not the Right Tool If:

  • You run a CRM other than HubSpot and need deep enrichment integration — Clearbit's API works standalone, but the native value lives inside HubSpot, and you would be paying for a fraction of the product's capability
  • Your enrichment strategy requires multi-source waterfall logic across dozens of providers. Clay connects to over 75 data sources and chains them with fallback sequences, which consistently produces higher overall match rates than any single-source enrichment tool
  • You need individual-level website visitor identification rather than company-level de-anonymization. Clearbit Reveal tells you which organization visited, not which person, which limits its usefulness for direct individual outreach
  • You are a small team with limited budget that does not already use HubSpot Marketing Hub Professional or Enterprise — the HubSpot platform cost is a prerequisite to accessing Breeze Intelligence enrichment

Final Verdict

★★★★☆
4.4/5 — SAG editorial score

Clearbit — now Breeze Intelligence — is the strongest choice for HubSpot-native teams that need real-time enrichment, visitor identification, and form optimization built directly into their CRM without external connectors.

The HubSpot acquisition transformed Clearbit from a general-purpose enrichment API into a deeply embedded data layer for HubSpot users. If your organization already runs HubSpot Marketing Hub Professional or Enterprise, Breeze Intelligence delivers enrichment that feels like a core CRM feature rather than a bolted-on integration. Leads arrive pre-enriched, forms shorten themselves, visitor identification triggers account-based workflows, and lead scoring consumes enriched attributes natively. The trade-off is equally clear: Clearbit is now a HubSpot product first and everything else second. Teams on Salesforce or other CRMs get API access but miss the workflow integration that makes the product compelling. And compared to multi-source orchestration platforms like Clay that waterfall across dozens of providers, Clearbit's single-source model will return empty fields more often when its proprietary database lacks coverage. For HubSpot teams that prioritize clean, real-time, zero-maintenance enrichment over maximum match rates across every provider, Clearbit remains the most operationally efficient option in the data enrichment category.
