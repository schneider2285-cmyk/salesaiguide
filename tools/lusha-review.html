What Is Lusha?

Lusha is a B2B contact data platform built around a Chrome extension that reveals verified email addresses and direct phone numbers while sales reps browse LinkedIn profiles and company websites. Founded in 2016, Lusha has grown into a prospecting tool used by over 100 million business professionals, according to Lusha's product page. The platform sits at the intersection of lead prospecting and data enrichment, giving individual reps and small teams fast access to contact information without requiring the budget or onboarding complexity of enterprise data providers like ZoomInfo.

Where Lusha differentiates from full-platform competitors like Apollo is in its deliberate simplicity. Apollo bundles a contact database with email sequences, a dialer, and enrichment workflows into one integrated platform. Lusha takes the opposite approach: it focuses on doing one thing well, which is surfacing accurate contact data quickly. The Chrome extension is the core experience. Reps install it, browse to a LinkedIn profile or company page, click the Lusha icon, and immediately see available emails and phone numbers. For teams that already have a sequencing tool and a dialer but lack reliable contact data, Lusha fills that specific gap without duplicating functionality they already own. The platform also offers a web-based prospecting dashboard for bulk searches and a data enrichment API for automated CRM workflows, but the extension remains the primary entry point for most users.

Key Features We Tested

✓ Chrome Extension

The Chrome extension is Lusha's flagship feature and the reason most reps adopt the tool. One click on a LinkedIn profile or company website reveals direct dial phone numbers and verified business email addresses. The extension overlays contact details directly on the page without requiring a tab switch or separate search. During testing, results appeared within two to three seconds on most profiles, and the data included mobile numbers that competing tools frequently miss on mid-market contacts.

✓ Prospecting Platform

Beyond the extension, Lusha offers a web-based prospecting dashboard with bulk search capabilities. Reps can filter by industry, company size, job title, seniority level, and geographic location to build targeted lead lists. Saved searches and ICP-based filters let teams standardize their targeting criteria across the organization. The platform exports lists as CSV or pushes them directly to connected CRM systems, making it practical for SDR teams that need to build outbound lists at volume rather than one profile at a time.

✓ Data Enrichment API

Lusha's enrichment API automates the process of filling missing contact fields in CRM records. Teams can set up automated workflows that enrich inbound leads in real time as they enter the system, ensuring sales reps always have complete contact information before making their first outreach attempt. The API also supports batch enrichment for cleaning existing databases, with real-time verification that flags outdated emails and disconnected phone numbers before reps waste time on dead contacts.

✓ Email Verification

Every email address Lusha surfaces goes through a verification layer that includes catch-all detection, deliverability scoring, and bounce prediction. The platform flags risky addresses before reps add them to outreach sequences, which helps protect sender reputation. During testing, verified emails from Lusha had a noticeably lower bounce rate compared to unverified data pulled from other sources. The verification runs automatically on both extension lookups and bulk prospecting results without requiring a separate tool or workflow step.

✓ CRM Integration

Lusha integrates natively with Salesforce and HubSpot, allowing reps to push contact data from the Chrome extension directly into CRM records with a single click. The integration maps fields automatically, creates new contact records when none exist, and updates existing records with fresh data. Auto-logging ensures that every contact lookup is recorded in the CRM activity timeline. The one-click export from the extension eliminates the manual copy-paste workflow that costs reps minutes per prospect across dozens of daily lookups.

✓ Compliance and Privacy

Lusha maintains GDPR and CCPA compliance across its data sourcing practices, with a publicly accessible opt-out management portal that lets individuals request removal from the database. The company holds ISO 27001 certification for information security management, which matters for organizations selling into regulated industries. Data provenance is transparent, and Lusha's community-sourced model means contact records are continuously updated by its user base rather than scraped from a single static source that decays over time.

What Lusha Gets Right (and Wrong)

After testing Lusha across several weeks of daily prospecting workflows including LinkedIn-based outreach, bulk list building, CRM enrichment, and cold calling campaigns, here is an honest assessment of where the platform delivers value and where it falls short. These findings come from real prospecting activity with actual sales targets, not from demo environments or marketing claims. We evaluated Lusha against the criteria that matter most to sales reps doing outbound work every day: data accuracy, speed of access, CRM workflow efficiency, and whether the tool actually helps reps connect with more prospects.

✓ What Works Well

  • Speed of contact discovery: The Chrome extension delivers contact data in seconds directly on LinkedIn profiles. No tab switching, no separate search interface, no waiting for batch processing. For reps who prospect on LinkedIn for hours daily, this speed advantage compounds into meaningful time savings across hundreds of lookups per week
  • Direct dial accuracy for mid-market contacts: Lusha consistently surfaced mobile and direct dial numbers for mid-market decision-makers where other tools returned only generic company switchboard numbers. This matters because reaching a VP directly instead of going through a receptionist dramatically improves cold calling connect rates
  • Low friction CRM integration: The one-click push from Chrome extension to Salesforce or HubSpot eliminates the copy-paste workflow that adds friction to every prospecting session. Reps can go from LinkedIn profile to CRM record creation in under five seconds, which keeps prospecting momentum intact
  • Accessible entry point for small teams: The free tier with five monthly credits lets individual reps test data quality before committing budget. Pro at $49 per month per user is significantly cheaper than enterprise data providers, making verified contact data accessible to teams that previously relied on manual research

✗ Key Limitations

  • Credit-based pricing constrains high-volume prospecting: Pro plan users receive 480 credits per year, which translates to roughly 40 lookups per month. SDR teams doing aggressive outbound campaigns that require hundreds of new contacts weekly will burn through credits quickly and need the Premium plan at $79 per month per user or higher for unlimited access
  • No sequencing or engagement features: Lusha provides contact data but not the tools to act on it. Unlike Apollo, which includes email sequences, a dialer, and task management, Lusha requires a separate sales engagement platform for actual outreach execution. Teams need to budget for both a data tool and a sequencing tool
  • Data coverage gaps outside North America and Western Europe: Contact accuracy and availability dropped noticeably when prospecting into APAC and Latin American markets during testing. Lusha's data is strongest in the US and UK, which limits its usefulness for teams with global territory assignments
  • Extension-dependent experience: The core value proposition requires the Chrome extension, which means Lusha is less useful for reps who primarily prospect from a CRM or work in non-Chrome browsers. The web platform offers bulk search but lacks the speed and integration that makes the extension compelling
