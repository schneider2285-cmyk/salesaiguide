What Is Mailshake?

Mailshake is a cold email and sales engagement platform with a reputation for simplicity and reliability. It provides email sequences, a LinkedIn automation add-on, and built-in phone dialer in one platform. While not as specialized as Instantly for deliverability optimization or Lemlist for personalization, Mailshake's strength is ease of use — most reps are up and running with their first campaign in under an hour.

Mailshake has been in the cold outreach space since 2017, which gives it a maturity advantage over newer tools. The platform sends emails through your existing email provider (Gmail or Outlook), which means messages go out from your real mailbox rather than through a third-party sending infrastructure. For teams that want a straightforward tool that connects their email account, loads a prospect list, and starts a sequence with minimal configuration, Mailshake remains one of the most approachable options available.

The typical Mailshake workflow starts with uploading a CSV of prospects, mapping your personalization fields (first name, company, custom variables), building a multi-step email sequence with follow-up timing, and clicking launch. The entire process from CSV to live campaign can be completed in under 30 minutes by a rep with no prior experience in cold outreach tooling. That speed to launch is Mailshake's primary competitive advantage — it trades advanced features for operational simplicity.

For agencies and consultancies running outreach on behalf of clients, Mailshake's simplicity also translates to faster client onboarding. Rather than training clients on a complex platform, agencies can set up campaigns quickly and show results without requiring the client to learn a new tool. This use case has made Mailshake a popular choice among smaller outbound agencies that manage outreach for multiple clients simultaneously.

One important distinction about Mailshake's sending model: because it sends through your actual Gmail or Outlook account, there is no third-party sending infrastructure involved. Your emails go out from your real mailbox with your real domain reputation, which means they land in the prospect's inbox the same way a manual email would. The trade-off is that you are limited by your email provider's daily sending limits — typically 500 emails per day for Google Workspace accounts. Teams needing to send beyond those limits need to connect multiple email accounts or consider a platform like Instantly that is built for higher-volume sending.

Mailshake also provides a data finder feature called SalesIntel integration that helps users find verified contact information for prospects. While this is not as robust as dedicated prospecting tools like Apollo or Clay, having some prospect data available within the same platform reduces the number of tools a rep needs to manage. For small teams that want a consolidated workflow — find the contact, write the email, run the sequence — this integration adds practical value even if the database is smaller than standalone prospecting platforms.

The Mailshake team has maintained a strong presence in the cold outreach community through educational content, webinars, and their Cold Email Masterclass. This ecosystem of support resources is worth mentioning because it means new users have access to best practices for deliverability, copy writing, and campaign strategy beyond just the software documentation. For teams new to cold outreach, these resources help bridge the gap between having a tool and knowing how to use it effectively.

Key Features

Email Sequences

Multi-step automated email sequences with personalization variables, A/B testing, and send schedule optimization. The sequence builder is clean and requires minimal training — most reps can build their first campaign from a CSV upload to live sending within 30 minutes without needing help from a manager or admin.

LinkedIn Automation

LinkedIn outreach automation for connection requests, messages, and profile views on the Sales Engagement plan. These LinkedIn steps integrate directly into your email sequences, so a prospect might receive an email on day one, a LinkedIn connection request on day three, and a follow-up email on day five — all managed from a single campaign view.

Phone Dialer

Built-in power dialer for following up with prospects via phone as part of multi-step cadences. Call recording and outcome logging are included, letting reps mark calls as connected, voicemail, or no answer and move to the next prospect without leaving the platform.

Lead Catcher

Lead Catcher monitors incoming replies and identifies positive responses — such as requests for more information or meeting requests — then routes them to a dedicated management view. Reps can respond directly from this view and mark leads as won, lost, or in progress.

Analytics

Per-campaign and per-step analytics showing open rates, click rates, and reply rates for every touchpoint in a sequence. Team-level dashboards give managers visibility into which campaigns and which reps are generating the most engagement.

Integrations

Native integrations with HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and Zapier for CRM sync and workflow automation. When a lead replies positively, Mailshake can automatically update the CRM record and trigger follow-up tasks so nothing falls through the cracks between prospecting and pipeline stages.

Pricing Breakdown

Mailshake uses per-seat pricing with two main plans. The Email Outreach plan covers core email sequencing and analytics, while the Sales Engagement plan adds LinkedIn automation, the built-in phone dialer, and Lead Catcher for managing replies. Both plans include CRM integrations and A/B testing. Annual billing discounts are available, but Mailshake does not currently offer a free trial — interested teams typically request a demo to see the platform before purchasing.

Pros & Cons

What's Great

  • Easiest onboarding of any cold email platform — most reps are live in under an hour
  • Clean, intuitive sequence builder with a minimal learning curve for new users
  • Multi-channel outreach in one platform combining email, LinkedIn, and phone dialer
  • Solid reputation built since 2017 with reliable sending deliverability through your own email provider
  • Strong educational resources and community support for teams new to cold outreach

What Could Be Better

  • More expensive than Instantly for email-only outreach — costs add up quickly with per-seat pricing on larger teams
  • Less deliverability optimization than Instantly/Smartlead — no built-in email warmup or inbox rotation tools
  • Fewer advanced features than Lemlist for personalization — no personalized images or landing pages
  • No unlimited mailboxes — per-seat model limits the volume scaling approach that tools like Instantly enable
  • LinkedIn automation requires a browser extension that can feel clunky compared to native integrations

Who It's For

Perfect For

  • Teams that want simple, reliable email + LinkedIn + phone in one tool
  • SDRs who need a quick setup without a steep learning curve
  • Agencies that want ease of use over maximum feature depth
  • Sales teams that value multi-channel cadences where email, LinkedIn, and phone steps run from a single workflow rather than separate tools

Skip If You

  • High-volume senders needing unlimited mailboxes and advanced deliverability (use Instantly)
  • Teams wanting personalized images and deep personalization (use Lemlist)
  • Budget-sensitive teams doing email-only outreach — Mailshake's per-seat pricing adds up fast compared to volume-based competitors

Limitations & Watch-Outs

No tool is perfect. Here are the downsides we found during testing.

  • Limited automation compared to newer outreach platforms
  • Phone dialer add-on costs extra on top of base subscription
  • Template library smaller than competitors
  • Reporting capabilities lack depth for advanced analytics needs

When to Choose Mailshake: Use Cases

Mailshake works best in specific scenarios where its strengths align with your team's needs.

  • Small sales teams starting with cold email outreach
  • Teams wanting a simple, no-frills email automation platform
  • Consultants needing basic phone plus email outreach
  • Organizations preferring ease of use over advanced features

Final Verdict

4.1 / 5
Good

Mailshake is the best choice for teams that prioritize simplicity and quick setup over maximum feature depth or deliverability optimization.

If getting a multi-channel outreach program running in under a day matters more than squeezing every last percentage point of deliverability, Mailshake delivers. Teams sending at scale or needing personalization should look at Instantly or Lemlist respectively.

The bottom line is that Mailshake occupies a specific niche: it is the tool for sales teams that want to combine email, LinkedIn, and phone outreach in a single platform without dealing with complex setup or steep learning curves. It is not the cheapest option, not the most powerful for pure email volume, and not the most advanced for personalization. But it does the multi-channel basics well, reliably, and with minimal onboarding friction — which for many teams is exactly the trade-off they want to make.

For teams evaluating Mailshake against the rest of the cold outreach market, the decision usually comes down to how many channels you genuinely use in your sales workflow. If your team only sends emails, more affordable and volume-focused tools will serve you better. If your reps actually work a multi-touch cadence across email, LinkedIn, and phone, Mailshake's integrated approach saves the complexity of stitching together separate tools for each channel.
