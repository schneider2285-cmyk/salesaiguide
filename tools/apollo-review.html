What Is Apollo.io?

Apollo.io is an all-in-one sales intelligence and engagement platform built for teams that want prospecting, outreach, and CRM functionality without stitching together multiple tools. The platform provides access to 275M+ B2B contacts and 73M+ companies, combined with email sequencing, a built-in dialer, and deal pipeline management.

What sets Apollo apart is the breadth of its feature set at an accessible price point. While competitors like ZoomInfo focus solely on data or Outreach focuses solely on engagement, Apollo bundles both into a single platform starting at $49/month. This makes it the default choice for startups and mid-market sales teams looking to do more with fewer tools.

Note: This profile is based on vendor documentation and G2 reviews, not firsthand testing. For user reviews, see Apollo.io on G2.

Key Features

275M+ Contact Database

Massive B2B database with email, phone, title, and company data. Regular updates keep records fresh.

Email Sequences with A/B Testing

Multi-step email sequences with automatic follow-ups, A/B testing on subject lines and copy, and performance analytics.

Built-in Dialer

Click-to-call dialer with call recording, voicemail drops, and local presence dialing to increase connection rates.

Chrome Extension for LinkedIn

Prospect directly from LinkedIn profiles. Pull contact data, add to sequences, and enrich records without leaving the browser.

CRM with Pipeline Management

Built-in deal tracking with pipeline stages, activity logging, and basic forecasting. Designed for teams that don't need Salesforce.

Intent Data Signals

Buyer intent signals help identify accounts actively researching relevant topics, enabling timely outreach to in-market prospects.

Pricing Breakdown

TL;DR: Free tier with 150 credits/mo. Basic at $49/mo. Professional at $79/mo. Organization at $119/mo per user.

⚠ Pricing Gotchas

  • Free tier is very limited — 150 credits/month is enough for testing, not production use
  • Annual billing required for best pricing; monthly billing is 20-30% more expensive
  • Per-seat pricing gets expensive for large teams — 50 reps at $119/mo = $71K/year
  • Email sending limits vary by plan — heavy senders may hit daily caps

What Apollo Is Great At

  • Largest B2B contact database (275M+): More contacts than most competitors at a fraction of the cost
  • All-in-one platform eliminates tool sprawl: Data, sequences, dialer, and CRM in one login saves time and money
  • Generous free tier for testing: Try the platform before committing any budget
  • Strong Chrome extension for LinkedIn prospecting: Pull verified emails and phones directly from LinkedIn profiles
  • Built-in CRM saves small teams from paying for Salesforce: Pipeline management without a $75/user/month CRM bill
  • Competitive pricing vs ZoomInfo: 80% of the data at 5% of the cost for SMB and mid-market teams

Where Apollo Breaks at Scale

  • Data accuracy degrades for niche verticals: Smaller industries, non-tech sectors, and international markets have lower match rates
  • Email deliverability concerns at high volume: Sending 500+ emails/day without proper warmup leads to spam folder issues
  • CRM features are basic compared to dedicated CRMs: No custom objects, limited reporting, and shallow workflow automation
  • Customer support is slow on lower tiers: Free and Basic users report multi-day response times
  • Sequence features are less robust than Outreach/Salesloft: Fewer channels, simpler branching logic, and limited analytics

Who Should NOT Buy Apollo

  • Enterprise teams needing premium data quality: If data accuracy is mission-critical, ZoomInfo's curated database is worth the premium
  • Teams already invested in Outreach/Salesloft for sequences: Apollo's sequencing is solid but not best-in-class — switching costs rarely justify it
  • Organizations requiring SOC 2 compliance at the platform level: Apollo's security certifications lag behind enterprise-grade competitors
  • Large teams where per-seat costs exceed the value of consolidated tooling: At 50+ seats, best-of-breed stacks can be more cost-effective

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Apollo's data accurate?

Apollo claims 275M+ contacts with regular updates. Data accuracy is generally strong for North American companies, but users report degraded quality for niche verticals, international markets, and smaller companies. Expect 85-90% accuracy for mainstream B2B contacts.

What are the free tier limits?

Apollo's free tier includes 150 email credits per month, 5 mobile credits, basic sequences (2 active), and limited filters. It's enough to test the platform but not sufficient for production prospecting.

How does Apollo compare to ZoomInfo?

Apollo is significantly cheaper ($49/mo vs $15K+/year) and includes built-in email sequences and a CRM. ZoomInfo offers better data quality and enterprise features (intent data, org charts) but at 10-30x the price. Apollo is the go-to for SMB and mid-market; ZoomInfo dominates enterprise.

Can Apollo replace my CRM?

Apollo includes a basic CRM with deal tracking and pipeline management. It works well for small teams (under 10 reps) but lacks the depth of Salesforce or HubSpot for reporting, custom objects, and workflow automation. Most teams outgrow Apollo's CRM by 15-20 reps.

How is Apollo's email deliverability?

Apollo provides built-in email sending but deliverability depends on your domain setup (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) and sending volume. High-volume senders (500+ emails/day) often pair Apollo's data with a dedicated sending tool like Instantly for better inbox placement rates.
