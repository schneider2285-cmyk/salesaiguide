What Is Apollo.io?

Apollo.io is an all-in-one sales intelligence and engagement platform with 275M+ B2B contacts, email sequencing, a built-in dialer, and CRM — bundled at $49/month, making it the default choice for startups and mid-market teams wanting prospecting + outreach in one tool.

Note: Based on vendor docs and G2 reviews, not firsthand testing. See Apollo.io on G2.

Key Features

275M+ Contact Database

B2B database with email, phone, title, and company data.

Email Sequences with A/B Testing

Multi-step sequences with auto follow-ups and A/B testing.

Built-in Dialer

Click-to-call with recording, voicemail drops, and local presence.

Chrome Extension for LinkedIn

Pull contact data from LinkedIn profiles into sequences.

CRM with Pipeline Management

Deal tracking with pipeline stages and basic forecasting.

Intent Data Signals

Identify accounts actively researching relevant topics.

Quick Verdict

✓ Best For

SDRs and AEs at startups or mid-market companies who need prospecting, email sequences, and a basic CRM in one affordable platform.

✗ Avoid If

You need enterprise-grade data quality for niche markets, or you already have a mature Outreach/Salesloft + ZoomInfo stack.

Bottom Line

Apollo is the best value all-in-one sales tool on the market — 80% of ZoomInfo's data at 5% of the price, plus built-in engagement. The tradeoff is data accuracy at the margins and enterprise features.

Pricing Breakdown

TL;DR: Free tier (150 credits/mo). Basic $49/mo. Professional $79/mo. Organization $119/mo per user.

⚠ Pricing Gotchas

  • Free tier (150 credits/mo) is testing only, not production
  • Annual billing required for best pricing; monthly is 20-30% more
  • 50 reps at $119/mo = $71K/year — adds up fast
  • Heavy senders may hit daily email caps

What Apollo Is Great At

  • 275M+ contact database: More contacts than most competitors at a fraction of the cost
  • All-in-one eliminates tool sprawl: Data, sequences, dialer, and CRM in one login
  • Generous free tier: Test the full platform before committing budget
  • Chrome extension for LinkedIn: Pull verified emails and phones from profiles
  • Built-in CRM: Pipeline management without a $75/user/mo CRM bill

Where Apollo Breaks at Scale

  • Data accuracy degrades for niche verticals: International and non-tech sectors have lower match rates
  • Deliverability concerns at high volume: 500+ emails/day without warmup risks spam folders
  • Basic CRM features: No custom objects, limited reporting
  • Slow support on lower tiers: Free/Basic users report multi-day response times
  • Sequences lag Outreach/Salesloft: Fewer channels, simpler branching

Who Should NOT Buy Apollo

  • Enterprise teams needing premium data: ZoomInfo's curated database is worth the premium for mission-critical accuracy
  • Teams invested in Outreach/Salesloft: Switching costs rarely justify it
  • Orgs requiring SOC 2 compliance: Security certifications lag enterprise-grade competitors
  • Large teams (50+ seats): Best-of-breed stacks can be more cost-effective at scale

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Apollo's data accurate?

Generally strong for North American companies, but quality degrades for niche verticals, international markets, and smaller companies. Expect 85-90% accuracy for mainstream B2B contacts.

What are the free tier limits?

Free tier: 150 email credits/month, 5 mobile credits, 2 active sequences, limited filters. Enough to test but not for production use.

How does Apollo compare to ZoomInfo?

Apollo is cheaper ($49/mo vs $15K+/year) with built-in sequences and CRM. ZoomInfo has better data and enterprise features at 10-30x the price. Apollo for SMB/mid-market; ZoomInfo for enterprise.
