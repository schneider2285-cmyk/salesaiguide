What Is Salesloft?

Salesloft is a sales engagement platform that combines cadence automation, conversation intelligence, and deal management for mid-market and enterprise sales teams. After acquiring Drift in 2023 and expanding into revenue intelligence, Salesloft now competes with Gong on analytics while maintaining its position as a leading sales engagement tool. It is most often compared to Outreach as the two dominant platforms in the category.

Key Features

Cadence Automation

Multi-step automated sequences combining email, phone calls, LinkedIn tasks, and custom steps. Branching logic, A/B testing, and conditional actions based on prospect behavior.

Conversation Intelligence

Call recording, transcription, and AI analysis of rep conversations. Talk ratio tracking, keyword alerts, and coaching feedback delivered directly in the platform.

Deal Management

Deal board with engagement scoring, risk alerts, and next-step recommendations. Pipeline inspection tools for managers reviewing forecast accuracy and deal health.

Dialer

Built-in dialer with click-to-call, voicemail drop, call recording, and local presence. Integrates call outcomes directly into cadence workflows and CRM.

Analytics & Forecasting

Rep activity dashboards, cadence performance analytics, and pipeline forecasting with AI-driven commit accuracy predictions.

CRM Integration

Bidirectional Salesforce and HubSpot sync. Activity auto-logging, lead and opportunity creation, and record updating without manual CRM data entry.

Pros & Cons

What's Great

  • Cleaner, more intuitive UI than Outreach — faster rep adoption
  • Strong cadence automation with solid branching logic
  • Conversation intelligence is solid without Gong's price tag
  • Deal management in the same platform as engagement tools
  • Excellent Salesforce sync and activity logging

What Could Be Better

  • Custom pricing lacks transparency — requires sales engagement before evaluation
  • Annual contracts required — meaningful financial commitment before proving value
  • Conversation intelligence less deep than Gong at enterprise scale
  • Expensive for smaller teams where simpler tools suffice

Who It's For

Perfect For

  • Mid-market sales teams (20-200 reps) needing structured cadences and CRM integration
  • Teams wanting conversation intelligence without Gong's price
  • Organizations running multi-channel outreach (email + phone + LinkedIn)
  • Teams that find Outreach's UI too complex or cluttered

Skip If You

  • Small teams under 15 reps (HubSpot Sales Hub is better value)
  • Purely inbound teams that don't need heavy outbound cadence tools
  • Teams that need best-in-class conversation intelligence (use Gong)
  • Anyone without budget for $75+/user/month minimum

Limitations & Watch-Outs

No tool is perfect. Here are the downsides we found during testing.

  • Premium pricing targets mid-market and enterprise only
  • Initial setup and CRM integration requires significant configuration
  • Cadence creation has a learning curve for new users
  • Reporting customization options could be more flexible

When to Choose Salesloft: Use Cases

Salesloft works best in specific scenarios where its strengths align with your team's needs.

  • Mid-market sales teams needing structured cadence execution
  • Revenue teams wanting conversation intelligence built into workflows
  • Sales managers coaching reps on email and call performance
  • Organizations needing forecasting tied to engagement activity
