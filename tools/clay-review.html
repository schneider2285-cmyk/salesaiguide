What Is Clay?

Clay is a data enrichment platform that aggregates 50+ providers (Apollo, ZoomInfo, Hunter, Clearbit, etc.) into a spreadsheet-like interface with a unified credit system, AI-powered research, and waterfall enrichment logic for RevOps and sales teams.

What Users Actually Say

Recurring themes synthesized from G2, Capterra, Reddit, and practitioner communities.

Waterfall enrichment finds data other tools miss

Users consistently report that Clay's multi-provider waterfall approach yields 20-40% higher match rates than single-source tools like Apollo or ZoomInfo. The ability to chain 50+ providers means if one source misses a contact, the next picks it up automatically.

Credit burn surprises teams mid-month

The most common complaint across all platforms: credit consumption is hard to predict. Multi-step workflows with AI enrichment can burn 5-10x more credits than expected. Teams frequently report running out of credits mid-cycle, forcing upgrades or workflow pauses.

Powerful but steep learning curve divides users

The spreadsheet-style UI is both Clay's strength and weakness. Technical users and RevOps teams praise the flexibility. Non-technical reps consistently report frustration in the first 1-2 weeks. The community templates and Claybooks help, but onboarding remains a real investment.

Replaces 3-5 data subscriptions for most teams

RevOps teams report consolidating separate subscriptions to ZoomInfo, Clearbit, Hunter, and others into Clay's single credit-based system. The net cost is often lower than maintaining multiple tools, though total spend depends heavily on workflow complexity.

Pricing Breakdown

TL;DR: $149/mo (2K credits). Most teams need Explorer at $349/mo (10K credits). Enterprise at $800/mo.

⚠ Pricing Gotchas

  • 5-step waterfalls can use 5+ credits per record
  • AI steps consume credits on top of data lookups
  • $149 → $349 tier jump is steep; no mid-tier option
  • Overage charges apply when you exceed monthly credits

What Clay Is Great At

  • Data consolidation: Replace 3-5 data subs with one credit system across 50+ providers
  • Waterfall enrichment: Fallback logic yields 20-40% higher match rates than single-source tools
  • AI-powered research: LLM integration turns raw data into personalized messaging
  • No-code workflow builder: Complex enrichment sequences without engineering
  • Active community: Templates and Slack community accelerate time-to-value

Where Clay Breaks at Scale

  • Credit cost unpredictability: Monthly costs hard to forecast until workflows run a full cycle
  • Large dataset performance: 10K+ row tables can become sluggish
  • Onboarding time: 2-4 weeks before teams are fully productive
  • No built-in sending: Data-only — need a separate outreach tool
  • Admin overhead: Workflows need ongoing RevOps attention

Who Should NOT Buy Clay

  • Solo SDRs on a budget: $149/mo minimum is steep for basic contact lookup
  • Teams wanting plug-and-play: Use Apollo or Lusha if you don't want to build workflows
  • Low-volume prospectors: Under 200 emails/week doesn't justify the overhead
  • Teams needing an all-in-one: No email sending, no CRM — data layer only

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Clay worth $149/month?

For teams subscribing to multiple data providers, Clay saves money by consolidating into one credit system. If you only need one source, a dedicated tool may be cheaper.

How fast do Clay credits get used up?

Simple lookups use 1 credit, but multi-step AI workflows burn credits fast. Most Explorer plan teams (10K credits) support 2-3 reps.

Can Clay replace ZoomInfo or Apollo?

Clay aggregates 50+ providers including ZoomInfo and Apollo, replacing direct subscriptions in many cases. However, it lacks a built-in CRM or email sending.
