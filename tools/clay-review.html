What Is Clay?

Clay is a data enrichment and prospecting platform that lets you access 50+ data providers (Apollo, ZoomInfo, Hunter, Clearbit, etc.) from one spreadsheet-like interface. Instead of paying for multiple data tools separately, Clay gives you credits that work across all providers.

The real magic is the AI layer on top — you can use ChatGPT/Claude to analyze prospect data, write personalized emails, and automate research at scale. After 8 months of building prospecting workflows in Clay, it's become the center of my entire outbound operation.

Key Features

✓ 50+ Data Providers in One

Access Apollo, ZoomInfo, Hunter, Clearbit, and 40+ other data providers from a single interface using unified credits. Instead of maintaining separate subscriptions and juggling multiple dashboards, Clay lets you query any provider from one table row. This matters for sales teams because different providers excel in different areas — Hunter is strong on email verification, Apollo on direct dials, ZoomInfo on org charts. Clay gives you all of them without separate contracts.

✓ AI-Powered Enrichment

Use ChatGPT or Claude directly inside Clay columns to research companies, write personalized opening lines, and score prospect fit against your ICP. You can build AI columns that read a company's website, pull recent news, and generate a custom value proposition — all automatically for every row in your table. For sales teams doing account-based outreach, this means personalization that used to take 30 minutes per prospect now happens in seconds across hundreds of rows.

✓ Waterfall Enrichment

Set up fallback sequences so that if one data provider doesn't have a result, Clay automatically queries the next provider in your chain. For example, you can try Hunter first for email, then fall back to Apollo, then Clearbit — stopping as soon as one returns a verified address. This is the feature that drives Clay's high match rates. In my testing, waterfall sequences consistently hit 90%+ email match rates compared to 60-70% from any single provider alone.

✓ No-Code Integrations

Clay connects to 200+ tools including HubSpot, Salesforce, Instantly, Lemlist, Slack, and Google Sheets through native integrations and webhooks. You can build end-to-end workflows where enriched prospect data flows directly into your CRM and outreach platform without manual CSV exports. For RevOps teams, this means building automated pipelines: trigger on new CRM leads, enrich in Clay, push qualified prospects to your sending tool — all without writing a line of code.

✓ Spreadsheet Interface

Clay uses a familiar spreadsheet layout that sales reps can start using on day one without coding knowledge. Each row is a prospect, each column is a data point or enrichment action, and you can sort, filter, and manipulate data just like in Excel or Google Sheets. The difference is that Clay columns can execute live API calls, run AI prompts, and trigger enrichment workflows. This design choice is smart — it lowers the barrier to adoption for non-technical team members who would struggle with API-based tools.

✓ Real-Time Collaboration

Clay's team workspace lets multiple users build and run enrichment workflows simultaneously with shared templates, saved table configurations, and live editing. Team members can create reusable workflow templates — for instance, a standard ICP qualification flow — that the entire sales org can apply to new prospect lists. This is especially valuable for growing teams where you want consistent data quality and enrichment logic across all reps rather than everyone building their own ad-hoc processes.

Limitations to Watch For

Clay is powerful, but it's not without trade-offs. After eight months of daily use, these are the workflow-level gotchas that caught me off guard — and that you should plan for before committing.

  • Building workflows from scratch takes time: Clay's flexibility is a double-edged sword. Creating your first multi-step enrichment table with conditional logic, waterfall sequences, and AI columns requires real investment. Expect to spend several hours on your first serious workflow before it runs smoothly. The Clay University docs help, but there's no shortcut to hands-on practice.
  • Credit-based pricing means heavy usage gets expensive fast: Every enrichment action costs credits, and complex workflows that chain multiple providers per row can burn through your monthly allotment quickly. A single table enriching 1,000 rows across three providers could consume 3,000 credits in one run. Monitor your usage closely, especially in the first few weeks.
  • Data enrichment quality varies by region: Clay's data providers deliver the strongest coverage for US and UK markets. If your target accounts are primarily in continental Europe, Asia-Pacific, or Latin America, expect lower match rates on emails and direct dials. Always test a small batch before running a full enrichment campaign in less-covered regions.
  • No built-in email sending: Clay is a data and enrichment platform, not an outreach tool. Once you've built and enriched your prospect list, you'll need to push it into a dedicated sending platform. If that's a dealbreaker, consider pairing Clay with Instantly.ai for cold email or Lemlist for multi-channel sequences.
