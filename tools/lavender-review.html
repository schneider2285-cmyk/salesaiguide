What Is Lavender?

Lavender is an AI email coaching tool that sits inside your email client as a Chrome extension, analyzing cold emails in real-time as you write them. It scores each email on effectiveness and provides specific, actionable suggestions to improve reply rates.

Unlike AI writing tools that generate emails for you, Lavender coaches you to become a better writer. It includes prospect personality analysis using the DISC framework, helping you tailor tone and messaging to each recipient. Team plans add coaching dashboards so managers can identify which reps need help.

Note: This profile is based on vendor documentation and G2 reviews, not firsthand testing. For user reviews, see Lavender on G2.

Pricing Breakdown

TL;DR: Free tier (5 emails/mo). Starter at $29/mo. Individual Pro at $49/mo. Team plans are custom pricing.

⚠ Pricing Gotchas

  • Free tier limited to 5 emails/month — enough to try but not for real daily use
  • Per-seat pricing on team plans can add up quickly for larger sales orgs
  • AI model quality varies by email type — cold email coaching is strongest, warm email coaching is less differentiated
  • Team analytics and coaching dashboards require Team plan (custom pricing, not listed publicly)
  • Most value comes from consistent daily use — occasional users see less ROI from the per-seat cost

What Lavender Is Great At

  • Permanent skill improvement: Real-time coaching improves rep writing skills over time — the benefits persist even without the tool
  • Personality-driven personalization: DISC-based prospect analysis helps reps adjust tone for each recipient automatically
  • Measurable reply rate impact: Users report 2-3x improvement in reply rates when consistently following coaching suggestions
  • Zero-friction workflow: Chrome extension works inside your existing email client — no context switching required
  • Manager visibility: Team coaching dashboards surface which reps need help and track improvement over time
  • Instant time-to-value: Install the extension and immediately get actionable coaching on your very first email

Where Lavender Breaks at Scale

  • Email-only coaching: Only helps with email — no coaching for calls, LinkedIn messages, or other outreach channels
  • Per-seat cost pressure: Per-seat model makes it expensive for large teams where incremental improvement may not justify the cost
  • Repetitive suggestions: AI suggestions can become repetitive over time for experienced users who've already internalized the coaching
  • Maturing team features: Team analytics are still developing compared to established coaching platforms like Gong or Outreach
  • Narrow integration scope: Beyond email clients and a few SEPs, integration depth with the broader sales stack is limited
  • Small review base: Relatively few G2 reviews (110) compared to more established competitors

Who Should NOT Buy Lavender

  • Inbound-only teams: If your team doesn't send cold email, Lavender provides no value — it's built specifically for outbound
  • Large teams on tight budgets: Per-seat costs outweigh incremental reply rate improvement when you're scaling past 20+ reps
  • Already-strong writers: Reps who already achieve high reply rates see diminishing returns from coaching suggestions
  • Teams needing a sending platform: Lavender only coaches email copy — it doesn't send, sequence, or automate outreach
  • Multichannel coaching needs: If you need coaching across calls, LinkedIn, and email, look at Gong or conversation intelligence tools instead

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Lavender actually improve reply rates?

According to Lavender's published data and G2 reviews, users report 2-3x improvement in reply rates after consistently using the coaching suggestions. Results vary by baseline skill level — reps who already write strong emails see smaller gains, while newer SDRs tend to see the most improvement.

How does Lavender compare to using ChatGPT for emails?

Lavender is purpose-built for sales emails with real-time scoring, prospect personality analysis (DISC), and data on what works based on millions of emails. ChatGPT is a general writing tool without sales-specific coaching, email scoring, or integration with your email client. Lavender coaches you to improve; ChatGPT writes for you.

How does Lavender handle email privacy and data?

Lavender processes email content to provide coaching suggestions. Per their documentation, they don't store email content permanently and comply with SOC 2 requirements. Check their current privacy policy for the latest data handling practices.

What's the difference between Team and Individual plans?

Individual plans ($29-49/mo) provide personal coaching and scoring. Team plans (custom pricing) add manager dashboards showing which reps need coaching, team-wide analytics, and admin controls. Team plans are typically worth it for teams of 5+ reps where coaching visibility matters.

Which email clients does Lavender work with?

Lavender works as a Chrome extension with Gmail and Outlook web. It also has native integrations with Outreach and Salesloft, coaching emails directly within those sales engagement platforms. It does not currently support desktop email clients like Apple Mail or Thunderbird.
