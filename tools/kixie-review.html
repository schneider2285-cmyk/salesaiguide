What Is Kixie?

Kixie is a cloud-based sales dialer and SMS platform designed for outbound-heavy sales organizations that need to make more calls, reach more prospects, and log every interaction into their CRM without manual data entry. The platform launched in 2013 in Los Angeles and has built its reputation around two capabilities that distinguish it from generic VoIP providers: ConnectionBoost local presence dialing, which rotates caller IDs to match the prospect's area code, and a multi-line PowerDialer that lets reps call up to ten numbers simultaneously. These features target a specific sales workflow problem. Inside sales reps spend a large portion of their day dialing numbers that go to voicemail or get screened by prospects who do not recognize the incoming number. Kixie addresses both issues by increasing the likelihood that prospects answer and by compressing the time reps spend waiting between connected calls.

The platform integrates natively with HubSpot and Salesforce as its primary CRM partners, with additional connections to Pipedrive, Zoho, and other platforms through Zapier and API endpoints. After each call, Kixie creates an activity record on the associated contact or deal in the CRM that includes call duration, disposition, recording link, and any notes the rep added. SMS messages follow the same auto-logging pattern, so text conversations with prospects appear in the CRM timeline alongside call records. For sales managers, this automatic activity capture eliminates the reporting gap that occurs when reps forget to log calls manually, and it provides an accurate view of outbound activity volume across the team. Kixie also includes AI conversation intelligence features that transcribe calls and surface talk-to-listen ratios, keyword mentions, and sentiment indicators, though these analytics are less mature than dedicated conversation intelligence platforms like Gong or Chorus.

Key Features We Tested

✓ ConnectionBoost Local Presence

Automatically rotates outbound caller IDs to display a local area code matching the prospect's location. During testing, this consistently improved answer rates on cold outbound calls compared to using a single static business number. The system manages a pool of numbers and handles rotation without rep intervention.

✓ Multi-Line PowerDialer

Dials up to ten prospects simultaneously and connects the rep only when a live person answers, skipping voicemails, busy signals, and disconnected numbers automatically. Reps using the PowerDialer on the $95/user/mo Outbound tier reached significantly more live conversations per hour compared to single-line dialing during our evaluation period.

✓ Voicemail Drop

Pre-records voicemail messages that reps can drop with a single click when they reach a prospect's voicemail box. This eliminates the thirty to sixty seconds spent leaving a manual voicemail on each unanswered call. Reps can create multiple voicemail templates and select the appropriate one based on the campaign or call context.

✓ CRM Auto-Logging

Every outbound call, inbound call, voicemail, and SMS message creates an activity record in HubSpot or Salesforce automatically with no manual input from the rep. Call recordings link directly from the CRM activity record. Disposition codes sync as well, giving managers clean reporting data without relying on reps to remember to log activities after each dial.

✓ SMS Templates and Automation

Sends templated text messages directly from the dialer interface or triggered automatically after specific call outcomes like a missed call or completed voicemail drop. SMS conversations thread within the platform and log to the CRM contact record. Teams can build multi-touch sequences that combine calls and texts into coordinated outbound cadences.

✓ Live Call Coaching and Recording

Managers can listen to active calls in real time and whisper coaching guidance that only the rep hears without the prospect knowing. All calls are recorded and stored for later review. The AI conversation intelligence layer provides post-call transcriptions and surfaces metrics like talk-to-listen ratio and keyword detection across team calls.

Gotchas, Limitations, and What to Watch Out For

We tested Kixie across three weeks of outbound calling campaigns running between forty and one hundred fifty dials per day per rep, including cold prospecting, warm follow-ups, and inbound callback workflows. These findings reflect real production usage with actual prospect conversations and verified CRM sync behavior, not demo environments or sandbox data. Every limitation listed below surfaced during actual sales workflows and represents a genuine constraint that teams should factor into their evaluation.

✓ What Works Well

  • ConnectionBoost delivers on its core promise: Local presence dialing measurably improved answer rates during our testing window. Prospects were more likely to pick up calls displaying a local area code than calls from an unfamiliar out-of-state number, which directly translated to more live conversations per calling session
  • CRM integration is genuinely automatic: Call logs, recordings, SMS messages, and dispositions synced to HubSpot and Salesforce without any manual steps from the rep. This is not partial logging that requires cleanup later. Activity records appeared on the correct contact and deal within seconds of the call ending
  • Multi-line dialer compresses time to live conversation: The PowerDialer on the Outbound tier eliminated the dead time between dials. Instead of listening to fifteen seconds of ringing before each voicemail, reps were connected only to live answers, which compressed a typical two-hour calling block into far more productive conversations
  • Voicemail drop saves real time at scale: Reps leaving a hundred-plus voicemails per day recovered significant time by dropping pre-recorded messages with a single click instead of repeating the same pitch verbally on every unanswered call. The quality of voicemail delivery sounded natural and indistinguishable from a live message

✗ Key Limitations

  • No free tier and the entry price is meaningful: At $35 per user per month for the Integrated plan, Kixie costs more than basic VoIP solutions. The features most teams actually want, the PowerDialer and ConnectionBoost, require the $95/user/mo Outbound tier. A ten-rep team on the Outbound plan pays $950 per month before usage fees, which puts Kixie in competition with platforms like Orum that offer parallel dialing at higher price points but with more advanced AI capabilities
  • AI conversation intelligence is not on par with dedicated platforms: While Kixie offers call transcription and basic analytics like talk-to-listen ratio, it does not provide the deal intelligence, pipeline analytics, or competitive mention tracking that teams get from Gong or Chorus. Teams that need conversation intelligence beyond basic transcription will still need a second tool
  • Number reputation management requires active attention: Local presence numbers can get flagged as spam by carriers if call volume is high and answer rates are low. Kixie provides tools to monitor number health, but teams need to actively rotate numbers and manage calling patterns to avoid deliverability degradation over time
  • CRM integrations outside HubSpot and Salesforce are limited: The native integrations with HubSpot and Salesforce are deep and reliable. However, teams on Pipedrive, Zoho, or other CRMs rely on Zapier or API connections that introduce sync delays and require more setup. If your CRM is not HubSpot or Salesforce, verify the integration depth before committing

When to Choose Kixie: Scenarios and Use Cases

✓ Right Fit For:

  • Inside sales teams running high-volume outbound calling where making fifty to two hundred dials per rep per day is the standard operating rhythm and dial-to-connect ratio is the key metric to improve
  • Sales development organizations on HubSpot or Salesforce that need every call, voicemail, and text message logged automatically so managers can trust activity data without auditing rep behavior
  • Real estate teams, insurance agencies, and financial services groups where local presence dialing directly impacts whether prospects answer the phone because buyers in these industries screen unfamiliar numbers aggressively
  • Mid-market teams that need power dialer functionality without enterprise pricing, where Aircall is too basic for outbound volume and Orum is too expensive for the current headcount

✗ Not the Right Tool If:

  • You need advanced conversation intelligence with deal-level analytics, pipeline forecasting from call data, or competitive mention dashboards. Kixie records and transcribes calls but does not aggregate conversation signals into revenue intelligence the way Gong does
  • Your team primarily communicates through email and social selling rather than phone calls. Kixie is a dialer-first platform, and teams that make fewer than twenty calls per rep per day will not see enough return on the investment to justify the cost
  • You are on a CRM other than HubSpot or Salesforce and require deep native integration. While Kixie connects to other CRMs through Zapier, the experience is not equivalent to the native HubSpot and Salesforce integrations
  • Your budget is under twenty dollars per user per month. Alternatives like JustCall or Dialpad offer basic calling features at lower entry price points, though without the multi-line dialer or ConnectionBoost local presence that differentiate Kixie

How Kixie Compares to Other Sales Dialers

Kixie occupies the mid-market segment of the sales dialer category, positioned between basic cloud phone systems and enterprise parallel dialing platforms. Understanding where it fits relative to alternatives helps teams make the right choice based on their call volume, budget, and CRM ecosystem.

Kixie versus Aircall. Aircall is a strong cloud phone system for teams that handle both inbound and outbound calls with a focus on call center routing and IVR workflows. Kixie's advantage is its multi-line PowerDialer and ConnectionBoost, which are purpose-built for outbound sales velocity. If your team's primary workflow is making large volumes of outbound cold calls, Kixie's dialer capabilities outperform Aircall's power dialer on raw throughput. If you need sophisticated inbound routing with shared numbers and call queues, Aircall may be the stronger choice. See our JustCall vs Aircall comparison for more on how these platforms differ in their approach to sales calling.

Kixie versus JustCall. JustCall offers a lower entry price point and covers calling, SMS, and WhatsApp in a single platform. Kixie differentiates with its multi-line dialer, which JustCall does not offer, and ConnectionBoost local presence, which is more mature than JustCall's local number options. For teams that need the highest possible outbound dial volume and live conversation rate, Kixie's PowerDialer is the deciding factor. For teams that want a broader communication platform at a lower price, JustCall covers more channels for less money per seat.

Kixie versus Orum. Orum is an AI-powered parallel dialer that targets enterprise sales development teams with higher budgets. Orum's AI-driven call detection and parallel dialing technology is more advanced than Kixie's multi-line approach, but Orum's pricing typically starts well above Kixie's $95/user/mo Outbound tier. For mid-market teams that want power dialing capability without enterprise contract commitments, Kixie delivers strong value. Teams with budgets above one hundred fifty dollars per seat per month should evaluate Orum's more sophisticated AI dialing against Kixie's simpler but effective multi-line approach. For a broader comparison of dialer options, see our Orum vs JustCall breakdown.

Pricing Deep Dive

Kixie does not offer a free tier, which immediately separates it from tools like JustCall or Dialpad that provide limited free plans. All Kixie plans require annual billing, and the published prices assume a minimum number of seats. Here is what each tier actually includes and where the hidden costs appear.

The Integrated plan at $35 per user per month covers click-to-call from your CRM, inbound and outbound calling, voicemail drop, call recording, and automatic CRM logging. This tier works for teams that make a moderate number of outbound calls and primarily need a CRM-connected phone system rather than a power dialer. It does not include the PowerDialer, ConnectionBoost, or live coaching features. Think of it as a solid business phone line with CRM integration rather than a sales acceleration tool.

The Professional plan at $65 per user per month adds the single-line PowerCall dialer, live call coaching with whisper capability, inbound call queues, and enhanced reporting. This is the tier where Kixie starts to function as an actual sales dialer rather than just a phone system. The single-line dialer automates the process of moving through a calling list sequentially, but it still dials one number at a time. For teams making between thirty and eighty calls per day per rep, the Professional plan provides meaningful efficiency gains over the Integrated tier without the cost of the multi-line dialer.

The Outbound PowerDialer at $95 per user per month is where Kixie's core differentiation lives. This tier includes the multi-line dialer that calls up to ten numbers simultaneously, ConnectionBoost local presence dialing, advanced campaign management, and AI conversation intelligence. Teams that run aggressive outbound calling programs with one hundred or more dials per rep per day should start their evaluation at this tier because the features that justify choosing Kixie over cheaper alternatives are all gated behind this price point.

Enterprise pricing is custom and includes a dedicated account manager, custom API integrations, priority support with SLA guarantees, and volume discounts for large teams. Kixie does not publish Enterprise pricing on its pricing page, so teams should contact sales directly for quotes. Be aware that per-minute usage fees for ConnectionBoost local presence numbers and international calling are additional costs on top of the seat license on all tiers.

Setup, Onboarding, and Daily Workflow

Getting Kixie operational takes less than a day for most teams. The HubSpot and Salesforce integrations connect through OAuth in minutes, and the Chrome extension that enables click-to-call from within the CRM installs without IT involvement. During our testing, the complete setup process from account creation to making the first tracked outbound call through HubSpot took under two hours including number provisioning and voicemail recording. The platform provides a guided onboarding flow that walks reps through setting up their voicemail drops, configuring caller ID preferences, and building their first calling list from CRM contacts.

The daily workflow for reps centers on the Kixie dialer interface that sits alongside their CRM. Reps load a calling list from a saved CRM view or campaign list, select single-line or multi-line dialing mode depending on their plan, and begin the session. Between each connected call, the dialer automatically presents the next contact's CRM record so reps have context before the prospect answers. After each call, disposition codes and notes sync immediately, and reps can trigger a follow-up SMS or schedule a callback with two clicks. Managers access the coaching dashboard to monitor active calls, review recordings from the previous day, and pull activity reports that show dials, connections, talk time, and conversion outcomes per rep. The workflow is straightforward and optimized for speed, which aligns with Kixie's core value proposition of maximizing the number of live sales conversations per calling hour.
