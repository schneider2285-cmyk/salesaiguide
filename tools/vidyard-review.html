What Is Vidyard?

Vidyard is a video messaging and hosting platform designed specifically for B2B sales and marketing teams. Founded in 2011 in Kitchener, Ontario, the company has grown to serve over 250,000 businesses that use video to engage prospects, accelerate deals, and measure the impact of video content across the buyer journey. The platform combines video recording, personalized sharing, viewer-level analytics, and CRM integration into a single tool that fits directly into existing sales workflows. Unlike general-purpose screen recording tools such as Loom, Vidyard was built from the ground up for revenue teams that need to track who watched a video, how much they watched, and what they did afterward, then push that data into Salesforce or HubSpot for follow-up prioritization and lead scoring.

The core value proposition is straightforward: sales reps record short, personalized video messages and embed them in emails, LinkedIn messages, or sales sequences. When a prospect clicks play, Vidyard tracks the entire viewing session and attributes it to a specific contact record in the CRM. This gives reps a signal that a prospect is actively engaged, which is more actionable than knowing an email was opened. In 2025, Vidyard expanded significantly into AI-powered features including AI Avatars that generate realistic video messages from text scripts and Video Agent, an automation tool that creates and sends personalized videos triggered by prospect actions like downloading content or requesting a demo. These additions moved Vidyard beyond basic screen recording into a platform that can scale personalized video outreach without requiring reps to manually record every message, which addresses one of the biggest bottlenecks in video-based selling: the time it takes to produce individual videos for each prospect.

Key Features We Tested

✓ Personalized Video Recording

Record webcam-only, screen-only, or combined webcam-plus-screen videos directly from the browser extension, desktop app, or mobile device. Videos can be recorded inside Gmail, LinkedIn, Outreach, and Salesloft without switching between applications. The recording interface is minimal and loads in under two seconds, which matters when reps need to produce dozens of personalized messages per day without significant workflow interruption. Videos can include custom thumbnails and animated preview GIFs that display in email to increase click-through rates.

✓ Screen and Webcam Capture

The combined screen-and-webcam mode places a small webcam bubble overlay on screen recordings, which is the most common format for sales prospecting videos where reps walk through a prospect's website, LinkedIn profile, or a proposal document while speaking directly to the viewer. Screen capture quality was sharp during testing across multiple monitor resolutions. The tool supports basic in-app trimming and editing after recording, though advanced editing features like cuts, annotations, or multi-clip stitching require upgrading or using a separate editor before uploading.

✓ Viewer Analytics Dashboard

Vidyard tracks every video view at the individual viewer level, showing who watched, what percentage of the video they completed, which segments they replayed, and whether they clicked any embedded CTAs. The analytics dashboard surfaces attention drop-off points using heatmap-style visualizations, so reps can identify where prospects lost interest and refine their messaging accordingly. View notifications arrive in real time, enabling reps to follow up within minutes of a prospect engaging with a video, which several users report improves response rates substantially compared to delayed outreach.

✓ Video Playlists and Hosting

Vidyard doubles as an ad-free video hosting platform with SEO-optimized, branded video pages. Sales teams can organize videos into playlists and curated collections called Vidyard Rooms, which centralize resources for multi-stakeholder deals where different buyers need different content. Hosted videos load reliably and include customizable player branding, interactive CTAs such as meeting booking prompts and email capture forms, and password protection for sensitive content. The hosting side differentiates Vidyard from lightweight recording tools that only support one-off video links.

✓ CRM and Email Integration

Bidirectional integration with Salesforce and HubSpot pushes video view data directly onto contact and lead records, enabling workflow triggers based on viewing behavior. For example, teams can configure HubSpot to automatically enroll a contact in a follow-up sequence when they watch most of a product demo video. The integration also feeds video engagement data into lead scoring models, so prospects who watch multiple videos get prioritized in the pipeline. Salesforce integration works with Sales Engagement (formerly High Velocity Sales) for embedding videos directly in cadences.

✓ AI Avatars and Video Scripts

Vidyard's AI Avatars let reps create a digital likeness by recording a 90-second training video, then generate unlimited personalized videos from text scripts without ever appearing on camera again. The AI script generator produces first-draft scripts based on prospect details pulled from CRM data, and keyword-based personalization swaps specific script segments per recipient. Avatars support 29 languages for global teams. The technology produces natural-looking facial movements, though trained users will notice subtle differences from live recordings. Video Agent extends this further by automating video creation and delivery based on prospect actions through Salesforce, HubSpot, or Marketo triggers.

What Vidyard Gets Right (and Wrong)

After testing Vidyard for outbound video prospecting, internal communications, and multi-stakeholder deal support, the platform shows clear strengths in sales-specific video workflows alongside notable limitations. The viewer analytics and CRM integration capabilities are genuinely differentiated from simpler screen recording tools, but the pricing structure and feature gating create real barriers for teams that cannot justify enterprise-level video spending. Here is what stood out during hands-on evaluation across multiple sales use cases and team configurations.

✓ What Works Well

  • Viewer analytics built for sales follow-up: Knowing exactly who watched your video, how much they watched, and which segments they replayed gives reps actionable intelligence for timing and tailoring follow-up outreach
  • CRM integration pushes engagement data where reps work: The Salesforce and HubSpot integrations log video views directly on contact records, enabling workflow triggers and lead scoring adjustments based on viewing behavior
  • AI Avatars scale personalized outreach: For teams that need high-volume video prospecting without recording each message individually, the avatar feature produces passable personalized videos from text scripts in seconds
  • Video Rooms for multi-stakeholder deals: Curated video collections shared via a single link let buying committees consume content asynchronously while giving the AE visibility into which stakeholders engaged with which materials

✗ Where It Falls Short

  • Steep pricing jump from free to paid: The free tier is limited to five videos per month, and the first paid plan at $59 per user per month is significantly more expensive than competing tools like Loom
  • CRM integration locked behind the Teams plan: The most valuable sales feature, automatic CRM data sync, requires the $99 per user per month Teams tier, putting it out of reach for budget-conscious teams
  • Limited native video editing: Only basic trimming is available natively; anything beyond cutting the beginning or end requires a separate editing tool, which adds friction for reps wanting polished output
  • AI Avatar quality depends on training input: Poor lighting or audio in the initial training recording produces an avatar that looks noticeably artificial, and even well-trained avatars show occasional lip-sync issues

Vidyard Scenarios and Use Cases

✓ Right Fit For:

  • Account executives who need to send asynchronous product walkthroughs, proposal explanations, or pricing breakdowns to buying committees where scheduling a live call with every stakeholder is impractical
  • Sales enablement managers who want visibility into which reps are using video, how often, and what engagement their videos generate through the team performance dashboard

  • Your budget is tight and the jump from free to $59 per user per month for the Starter plan is difficult to justify, especially for teams under five reps where per-seat costs add up quickly
  • You need advanced video editing capabilities like multi-clip assembly, text overlays, transitions, or detailed annotation tools, which Vidyard does not provide natively
  • Your primary video use case is marketing content hosting for public-facing campaigns where YouTube's reach and discovery features may deliver more value than Vidyard's sales-focused analytics

Adding a 60-second Vidyard video to the first touch in their Outreach sequence more than doubled reply rates in the team's first quarter using video. The viewer analytics let SDR managers identify which reps' videos generated the most engagement and coach lower performers on messaging, pacing, and thumbnail selection. When the CFO watches the pricing walkthrough three times, the AE follows up specifically on ROI and cost justification, armed with data about exactly which pricing segments held the CFO's attention longest.

Gotchas and Limitations to Watch For

Pricing Jumps Significantly from Free to Paid. The free tier limits users to 5 videos per month with a 30-minute recording cap and basic analytics. The first paid tier, Starter, costs $59 per user per month billed annually, which is a steep increase compared to competitors like Loom (from $15 per user per month) or Sendspark. For a team of five reps, that is $3,540 per year on the Starter plan before reaching the Teams tier required for CRM integration. Sales leaders need to confirm that the viewer analytics and CRM data justify this premium over simpler recording tools, because the basic act of recording and sending a video is something every competitor handles adequately at a lower price.

CRM Integration Is Locked Behind the Teams Plan. Salesforce and HubSpot integration, which is arguably Vidyard's strongest differentiator for sales teams, requires the Teams plan at $99 per user per month. Teams on the Starter plan can see viewer analytics in the Vidyard dashboard but cannot push that data into their CRM automatically. This means the highest-value feature for revenue operations, using video engagement to score leads and trigger follow-up workflows, is only accessible at the most expensive standard tier. Budget-conscious teams may need to build workarounds with Zapier or manual data entry to bridge the gap.

Limited Native Video Editing. Vidyard supports basic trimming after recording, but anything beyond cutting the beginning or end of a clip requires a separate editing tool. There are no native text overlays, transitions, background music options, or multi-clip assembly features. For reps who want polished videos with annotations pointing to specific parts of a screen share, or who need to stitch together multiple takes, the lack of editing depth means maintaining a parallel workflow in a tool like Canva or a dedicated video editor. Multiple G2 reviewers cite limited editing as a consistent frustration with the platform (G2 Reviews, Mar 2026).

AI Avatars Have a Learning Curve and Quality Variance. While AI Avatars are impressive in concept, the quality of the generated video depends heavily on the initial 90-second training recording. Poor lighting, inconsistent framing, or background noise during the training session produces an avatar that looks noticeably artificial. Even well-trained avatars occasionally produce lip-sync mismatches or unnatural eye movement that attentive viewers will detect. For high-stakes prospecting to C-suite buyers, manually recorded videos still outperform AI-generated ones in perceived authenticity, so teams should treat avatars as a scaling tool for volume outreach rather than a replacement for genuine human connection in top-of-funnel messaging.

Mobile App Stability Issues. Several reviewers on G2 and Capterra report that the Vidyard mobile app is less stable than the desktop and browser extension experiences. Recording on mobile occasionally produces lower-quality output, and the app interface lacks some features available on desktop. For reps who frequently record videos from their phone between meetings or while traveling, this gap matters. The browser extension and desktop app perform well, but mobile should be tested before committing if mobile recording is a core part of your workflow (Capterra Reviews, 2025).

How Vidyard Compares to Alternatives

Vidyard vs. Loom. Loom is the most frequently cited alternative to Vidyard, and the comparison comes down to use case. Loom is optimized for internal team communication, quick screen recordings, and asynchronous collaboration at $15 per user per month for the Business tier. Vidyard is built for external sales prospecting, with viewer-level analytics, CRM integration, and AI Avatars that Loom does not offer. If your primary need is sharing quick recordings with colleagues, Loom is the better choice. If you need to track which prospects watched your video, push that data into Salesforce, and scale personalized outreach with AI-generated content, Vidyard is purpose-built for that workflow. The price difference reflects this: Vidyard's Starter plan at $59 per user per month is four times Loom's Business tier, but delivers sales-specific features Loom does not attempt.

Vidyard vs. BombBomb. BombBomb focuses on video email for relationship-building, particularly in real estate, financial services, and customer success. BombBomb's strength is simplicity: record a video, embed it in an email, and see who opened it. Vidyard offers deeper analytics (segment-level viewing data, heatmaps, CRM sync), more advanced hosting capabilities (playlists, Rooms, branded pages), and AI features (Avatars, Video Agent) that BombBomb lacks. BombBomb may be simpler for teams that want basic video email without the complexity of a full video selling platform, but sales teams running multi-touch outbound sequences will find Vidyard's analytics and integrations more valuable. For a broader look at tools that complement video prospecting, see our Outreach review and Salesloft review.

Vidyard vs. Hippo Video and Sendspark. Hippo Video and Sendspark are lower-cost alternatives that target similar video selling use cases. Hippo Video offers video editing features that Vidyard lacks and positions itself as a more affordable option for teams that need both recording and production capabilities. Sendspark emphasizes simplicity and personalization-at-scale with dynamic video personalization. Both are viable for teams where Vidyard's pricing is prohibitive, but neither matches Vidyard's depth of CRM integration, the maturity of its AI Avatar technology, or the breadth of its analytics dashboard. Teams evaluating these alternatives should weigh whether the CRM data pipeline that Vidyard provides is worth the price premium over simpler recording and sharing tools. For email-focused outreach tool comparisons, see our Instantly vs Smartlead breakdown.

How Vidyard Fits in a Sales Tech Stack. Vidyard works best when paired with a sales engagement platform like Outreach or Salesloft that handles sequence management, and a CRM like Salesforce or HubSpot that captures the viewer analytics data. For teams comparing engagement platforms, our Outreach vs Salesloft comparison covers the sequence management side. For teams also using AI-powered email writing tools like Lavender, Vidyard adds the video layer that text-only tools cannot provide. The combination of Lavender for email copy optimization and Vidyard for video embeds covers both the written and visual components of modern outbound prospecting. Teams using Gong for conversation intelligence can also leverage Vidyard's Gong integration, which pushes video viewing data into Gong's deal intelligence view so managers see both live call performance and async video engagement in one place.

Vidyard Pricing Deep Dive

Understanding the Full Cost Structure. Vidyard's pricing has four tiers, and the real cost depends on which features your team actually needs. The free tier is useful for individual reps experimenting with video prospecting but is too limited for team adoption at 5 videos per month. The progression from Free to Starter to Teams represents meaningful feature unlocks at each level, but also meaningful cost increases that compound quickly across a sales team. All prices below are per user per month, billed annually, based on Vidyard's published pricing as of March 2026.

  • Free ($0): 5 videos per month, 30-minute recording limit, basic view tracking, AI script generator, browser extension, mobile and desktop apps, LinkedIn and Gmail integrations, 15 AI Avatar videos included
  • Starter ($59/user/mo): Unlimited video recording and hosting, full viewer analytics with heatmaps, custom branding on video pages, interactive CTAs (meeting bookers, email capture, banners), custom thumbnails, video playlists, team performance tracking
  • Teams ($99/user/mo): Everything in Starter plus Salesforce and HubSpot CRM integration, team performance dashboard with manager reporting, priority customer support, advanced security controls, Vidyard Rooms for deal acceleration
  • Enterprise (Custom): SSO and SAML authentication, dedicated customer success manager, advanced admin controls, custom integrations, volume discounts typically 15-22% below list price for multi-year contracts according to vendor negotiation benchmarks (Vendr, 2025)
  • Video Agent Add-On (Custom): Available as an add-on to any paid plan, unlocks unlimited AI Avatar videos and automated video creation and delivery triggered by CRM workflow events. Pricing is not publicly listed and requires contacting Vidyard sales (vidyard.com/pricing)

Cost Comparison Context. For a team of 10 SDRs on the Teams plan, the annual cost is $11,880 per year. The same team on Loom Business would pay approximately $1,800 per year, but would lack viewer-level analytics, CRM integration, AI Avatars, and sales-specific features. BombBomb's pricing falls between the two. The question is whether Vidyard's CRM data pipeline and AI capabilities generate enough additional meetings and pipeline to justify the 5-6x price premium over Loom. For teams that actively use the viewer analytics to prioritize follow-up and the CRM integration to score leads, the answer is typically yes. For teams that primarily need to record and share quick videos without deep analytics, the premium is harder to justify.

Scoring Criteria. Our 4.3/5 SAG editorial score reflects strong performance in sales-specific features (viewer analytics, CRM integration, AI Avatars) tempered by high pricing relative to competitors, limited native video editing, and the CRM integration paywall at the Teams tier. Vidyard scores highly on functionality depth and sales workflow fit, but loses points on value-for-money for smaller teams and on editing capabilities compared to platforms like Hippo Video. The G2 community rating of 4.5/5 across 800+ reviews aligns with our assessment that users who commit to the platform and integrate it with their CRM see strong results, while users on the free or lower tiers often feel constrained by feature limitations.

Who Benefits Most from Vidyard. The ideal Vidyard customer is a sales team of 10 or more reps running structured outbound sequences through a sales engagement platform, with a CRM (Salesforce or HubSpot) at the center of their workflow, and a commitment to video as a core channel rather than an occasional experiment. These teams extract the most value from the viewer analytics to CRM pipeline, the team performance dashboard for coaching, and the AI Avatar capability for scaling output. Individual reps or very small teams will find Vidyard's per-seat pricing difficult to justify when Loom covers basic video sharing at a fraction of the cost. The platform rewards teams that invest in building video into their standard sales process rather than treating it as an optional add-on.
