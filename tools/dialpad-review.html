What Is Dialpad?

Dialpad is an AI-powered business communications platform that unifies voice calling, video conferencing, team messaging, and contact center operations under a single interface with artificial intelligence embedded into every layer of the product. Founded in 2012 by the team behind Google Voice, Dialpad was built from the ground up around AI rather than retrofitting intelligence onto a legacy telephony system. The platform differentiates itself from traditional cloud phone providers like Aircall or RingCentral by treating real-time transcription, live coaching prompts, and sentiment analysis as core platform features available on every call rather than premium add-ons restricted to enterprise tiers. Where most business phone systems focus on call routing reliability and integration breadth, Dialpad leads with the premise that every conversation generates intelligence that should be captured and acted on immediately.

For sales teams specifically, this AI-first architecture means that every outbound call, inbound inquiry, and video meeting produces a searchable transcript with automatically extracted action items, detected topics, and sentiment scores within seconds of the conversation ending. The AI Sales Center add-on extends this further with real-time assist cards that surface relevant battlecards, objection handling scripts, and competitive intelligence during live calls based on what the prospect is actually saying. Managers gain visibility through coaching scorecards and sentiment trend data across the entire team's call activity without needing to listen to recordings. The unified communications aspect eliminates the common enterprise problem of managing separate vendors for phone, video, and messaging, which reduces both licensing costs and the operational friction of switching between disconnected tools throughout the workday. Dialpad positions itself as the AI layer that sits on top of all business conversations, turning voice data from an ephemeral event into a structured, actionable asset.

Key Features We Tested

✓ AI-Powered Voice Intelligence

Every call produces a real-time transcript with speaker labels and timestamps as the conversation happens, not after it ends. The AI automatically extracts action items, detects keywords and topics discussed, and generates a structured post-call summary that captures decisions made and next steps identified. No additional recording software or manual configuration is required because transcription runs by default on every call placed through the platform. The transcript becomes searchable immediately, so reps can reference specific prospect statements when composing follow-up emails minutes after hanging up.

✓ Real-Time Coaching (AI Assist)

During active calls, AI Assist displays live coaching cards on the rep's screen based on what the prospect is saying in the moment. When a competitor name is mentioned, relevant battlecard content surfaces automatically. When a pricing objection arises, suggested responses appear without the rep needing to search a knowledge base mid-conversation. Managers configure the triggers, keyword detection rules, and content libraries that power the system, and the AI handles real-time delivery to the correct rep based on conversation context detected through natural language processing of the live transcript.

✓ Unified Communications

Dialpad replaces separate tools for voice calls, video meetings, team messaging, and business SMS with a single platform that shares the same contact directory, conversation history, and AI transcription capabilities across all channels. Switching from a phone call to a video meeting with the same contact preserves context and full transcript history in one timeline. Sales teams that previously juggled a phone system, a Zoom license, and a Slack workspace can consolidate into one vendor with consistent AI features running identically across every communication channel rather than managing fragmented tool-specific workflows.

✓ Contact Center

The built-in contact center module handles inbound call routing through configurable IVR menus, queue management with estimated wait times, and workforce management tools for scheduling agents across shifts and time zones. Real-time monitoring dashboards let supervisors track active calls, queue depth, and individual agent availability simultaneously from a single view. For sales teams that handle both outbound prospecting and inbound lead qualification, having the contact center on the same platform as the sales dialer eliminates the data gap between marketing-generated inbound calls and outbound sales activity that typically fragments reporting across disconnected systems.

✓ CRM Integration

Native integrations with Salesforce and HubSpot provide automatic call logging with AI-generated summaries pushed directly to deal and contact records after every conversation ends. Click-to-dial functionality lets reps initiate calls from within the CRM interface without switching applications or copying phone numbers manually. Call disposition, duration, sentiment score, and full transcript links appear on the contact activity timeline automatically, keeping pipeline records accurate without requiring reps to manually log call notes or update deal stages between conversations throughout the selling day.

✓ Analytics & Sentiment

AI-driven sentiment analysis tracks the emotional trajectory of every call in real time, flagging specific moments where prospect sentiment shifts negative so managers can intervene during the call or provide targeted coaching afterward using the exact transcript segment. Beyond individual call scoring, the analytics dashboard aggregates call volume, average duration, outcome distribution, and coaching scorecard data at both the team and individual rep level. Managers use sentiment trend reports to identify which reps consistently maintain positive prospect engagement and which conversation topics or objection types most frequently correlate with negative sentiment shifts across the team.

What Dialpad Gets Right (and Wrong)

After testing Dialpad across several weeks of daily sales calls including outbound prospecting, discovery meetings, product demos, and internal team syncs, here is an honest assessment of where the platform delivers meaningful value and where it falls short of its ambitious AI-native positioning. These findings come from real sales workflows with actual prospect conversations, CRM sync verification, and coaching feature evaluation rather than from marketing demos or sandbox environments. We evaluated Dialpad against the criteria that matter most to sales teams considering an AI-first phone system: transcription reliability, coaching card usefulness during live calls, unified communications quality, and whether the AI capabilities justify the price premium over simpler alternatives.

✓ What Works Well

  • AI transcription on every call by default: Unlike platforms where transcription is a premium add-on or requires manual activation, Dialpad transcribes every call automatically from the Standard plan onward. Transcripts are available within seconds of the call ending with speaker labels, timestamps, and extracted action items that reps can reference immediately for follow-up emails
  • Real-time coaching that actually arrives in time: The AI Assist cards surface during the conversation when they are relevant, not after the call when the moment has passed. During testing, competitor mention triggers delivered battlecard content within seconds of the keyword appearing in conversation, which gave reps useful information while the prospect was still on the line
  • Genuine unified communications: Having voice, video, messaging, and SMS in one platform with shared AI capabilities across all channels eliminates the tool fragmentation that plagues most sales tech stacks. The experience of moving from a phone call to a video meeting with the same prospect felt seamless rather than requiring a platform switch
  • Sentiment analysis adds coaching depth: The real-time sentiment tracking gives managers a dimension of call quality visibility that pure transcription tools lack. Being able to identify calls where sentiment dropped and correlate that with specific conversation moments creates coaching opportunities that raw call metrics cannot surface

✗ Key Limitations

  • AI Sales Center is an expensive add-on: The most compelling sales features like real-time coaching cards, AI scorecards, and advanced analytics are not included in the base plans. The AI Sales Center requires additional per-seat licensing on top of the Pro or Enterprise plan, which can push the total cost well above simpler alternatives for teams that want the full AI coaching experience
  • CRM integrations require Pro plan or above: Salesforce and HubSpot native sync is locked behind the twenty-five dollar per seat Pro tier. Teams on the Standard plan at fifteen dollars per seat get Google and Microsoft 365 integration but need to manually bridge the gap to their CRM or build a Zapier workflow for call logging
  • Coaching card quality depends on configuration investment: The real-time assist system only delivers useful content if someone has built out the battlecard library, objection response database, and keyword triggers thoroughly. Out of the box, the coaching feature lacks pre-built content and requires meaningful upfront setup before it becomes valuable during live calls
  • Transcription accuracy degrades with poor audio: While transcription performed well on clean audio from desktop apps and quality headsets, accuracy dropped noticeably during mobile calls with background noise, speakerphone usage, or connections with significant latency. Teams with field reps calling from variable environments should expect inconsistent transcript quality

When to Choose Dialpad: Use Cases

✓ Right Fit For:

  • Sales teams that want AI transcription and intelligence built into every call by default rather than bolted on as a separate recording tool or conversation intelligence subscription
  • Organizations looking to consolidate their phone system, video conferencing, team messaging, and contact center onto a single platform to reduce vendor sprawl and licensing complexity
  • Sales managers who need real-time visibility into call quality through sentiment analysis and coaching scorecards without listening to full recordings or sitting in on live conversations
  • Mid-market teams of five to fifty reps where the value of live coaching prompts and automated call intelligence justifies the premium over basic cloud phone systems that handle voice but not AI

✗ Not the Right Tool If:

  • Your primary need is a budget-friendly power dialer with SMS and you do not need AI transcription, video conferencing, or unified messaging capabilities bundled into your phone system
  • You need parallel dialing that connects to multiple prospects simultaneously and routes live pickups to available reps for maximum outbound call volume per hour
  • Your team operates primarily through WhatsApp or international SMS channels where dedicated messaging platforms like JustCall offer deeper functionality and lower per-message costs
  • You want enterprise-grade conversation intelligence with deal-level analytics, pipeline forecasting from call signals, and multi-call journey tracking that platforms like Gong provide

Final Verdict

Dialpad is the strongest AI-native phone system for sales teams that want real-time transcription, live coaching, and unified communications without stitching together multiple vendors.

Its core value proposition delivers: AI transcription runs on every call by default, real-time coaching cards surface relevant content during live conversations when it matters, and the unified platform genuinely consolidates voice, video, and messaging into one coherent experience. For teams where AI-powered call intelligence and unified communications justify a premium over simpler dialers, Dialpad represents the most complete AI-first phone platform available. Teams that primarily need a budget dialer with SMS should evaluate JustCall first, while organizations requiring enterprise deal intelligence across pipeline stages will still need Gong or a dedicated conversation intelligence platform alongside their phone system.
