What Is Gong?

Gong is a revenue intelligence platform that captures and analyzes customer interactions across calls, emails, and web conferences. The platform uses AI to transcribe conversations, identify key topics and risks, and surface actionable insights for sales leaders, managers, and individual reps.

Beyond basic call recording, Gong provides deal intelligence (which deals are at risk and why), coaching tools (scorecards, call snippets, and libraries), and revenue forecasting that goes far deeper than CRM-based pipeline reports. It answers the question every sales leader asks: what's actually happening in our deals?

Note: This profile is based on vendor documentation and G2 reviews, not firsthand testing. For user reviews, see Gong on G2.

Key Features

AI Call Recording & Transcription

Automatically records and transcribes calls from Zoom, Teams, Meet, and dialers with speaker identification and topic tagging.

Deal Intelligence & Risk Scoring

AI analyzes deal health by tracking stakeholder engagement, competitor mentions, next steps, and sentiment to flag at-risk opportunities.

Sales Coaching with Scorecards

Manager coaching tools with call scorecards, snippet sharing, and performance benchmarking across the team.

Revenue Forecasting

AI-driven forecasting based on actual deal engagement data, not just rep self-reporting in the CRM.

Market Intelligence

Aggregated insights from call patterns reveal competitor trends, common objections, and messaging effectiveness.

CRM Integration (Auto-Logs Calls)

Bi-directional sync with Salesforce and HubSpot automatically logs activities and surfaces Gong insights inside CRM records.

Pricing Breakdown

TL;DR: No public pricing. Expect $100-150/user/month with annual contracts and minimum seat commitments. Enterprise deals start in the $200K+ range.

⚠ Pricing Gotchas

  • No public pricing at all — you must talk to sales to get a quote
  • Typical enterprise contracts start at $100-150/user/month based on industry reports
  • Annual contracts with minimum seat commitments are standard
  • Implementation fees are often not disclosed upfront and can add $10K-30K
  • Per-user model means costs scale linearly with team size — 100 reps at $150/mo = $180K/year

What Gong Is Great At

  • Best-in-class call analysis and AI insights: No competitor matches Gong's depth of conversation understanding and pattern recognition
  • Deal risk scoring catches at-risk deals before they stall: AI flags deals based on engagement patterns, not just rep sentiment
  • Coaching tools with call snippets and scorecards: Managers can share specific call moments and benchmark rep performance objectively
  • Revenue forecasting accuracy that CRMs can't match: Predictions based on actual buyer engagement data, not self-reported pipeline stages
  • Market intelligence from aggregated call patterns: Understand competitor trends, pricing objections, and messaging effectiveness across all calls
  • Massive G2 review base (5,800+) with 4.8 rating: Broad enterprise adoption and consistently high satisfaction scores

Where Gong Breaks at Scale

  • Extremely expensive: Easily $200K+/year for mid-size teams, making it one of the most expensive tools in the sales stack
  • Opaque pricing creates budget unpredictability: No way to plan costs without going through a sales process first
  • Reps may resist being recorded: Cultural adoption is a real challenge — some reps feel surveilled rather than coached
  • Only valuable if your team is on calls: Email-first or async selling motions get zero value from Gong
  • Implementation requires RevOps to configure deal boards and coaching workflows: Out-of-the-box setup captures calls, but real ROI needs custom configuration

Who Should NOT Buy Gong

  • Email-first sales teams: Gong adds no value if your reps aren't on calls — the entire platform depends on recorded conversations
  • Small teams under 10 reps: The cost doesn't justify the insights at this scale — managers can listen to calls directly
  • Companies without a coaching culture: Gong provides the data, but if managers don't use it for coaching, you're paying for an expensive call recorder
  • Budget-conscious startups: There are 80% solutions at 20% of the cost — Fireflies.ai or Avoma cover basic needs
  • Teams that only need call recording: If transcription and search is enough, Fireflies.ai or Otter.ai are dramatically cheaper

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Gong cost for small teams?

Gong does not publish pricing. Based on G2 reviews and industry reports, typical costs range from $100-150 per user per month with annual contracts and minimum seat commitments. A 10-person team can expect to pay $15K-25K per year. Implementation fees are additional and often not disclosed upfront.

How does Gong compare to Chorus?

Gong and Chorus (now part of ZoomInfo) are the two leading conversation intelligence platforms. Gong generally leads in AI depth, user experience, and deal intelligence. Chorus offers tighter ZoomInfo data integration. Gong has a higher G2 rating (4.8 vs 4.5) and significantly more reviews, suggesting broader adoption.

Are there privacy concerns with recording calls?

Gong records and transcribes all sales calls, which raises privacy considerations. The platform supports consent notifications and complies with major regulations (GDPR, CCPA). Two-party consent states require informing all participants. Most organizations handle this through standard call disclaimers. Rep resistance to being recorded is a common cultural adoption challenge.

How do you justify the ROI on Gong?

Gong's ROI typically comes from three areas: increased win rates (5-15% improvement from coaching insights), faster ramp time for new reps (30-50% reduction using call libraries), and improved forecast accuracy (reducing pipeline surprises). Teams with 20+ reps and a coaching culture see the strongest returns.

How does Gong integrate with my CRM?

Gong offers bi-directional integration with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Microsoft Dynamics. It automatically logs call activities, surfaces deal insights in CRM records, and syncs deal stage data. The Salesforce integration is the deepest, with custom object support and embedded Gong widgets in Salesforce views.
