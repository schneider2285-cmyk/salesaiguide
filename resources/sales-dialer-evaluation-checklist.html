Sales Dialer Evaluation Checklist

Updated: Feb 2026  ·  Last verified: Feb 2026

Our Methodology   |   Editorial Policy   |   How We Make Money

This checklist helps sales teams evaluate dialer platforms systematically. It covers the criteria that separate dialers built for real calling workflows from those that look good in a demo but create friction once deployed across a team.

Why trust this checklist: Every criterion below comes from hands-on testing of dialer platforms in live calling workflows. Our evaluation process is documented in our methodology, and our editorial standards are published in our editorial policy. No vendor influenced the criteria or weighting in this checklist.

How to Use This Checklist

  1. Identify your dialer type. Read the "Dialer Types" section below to determine whether your team needs a power dialer, parallel dialer, or progressive dialer based on daily call volume and team size.
  2. Score each vendor. Walk through the evaluation criteria and mark which requirements each platform meets. Use the pilot scorecard at the bottom to track your scores side by side.
  3. Run a 14-day pilot. Before committing, test your top pick against the pilot validation checklist with real call volume and CRM sync active.

Dialer Types and What They Are For

  • Power dialer: Calls numbers sequentially from a list, connecting the rep to each call one at a time. Best for teams where reps handle both inbound and outbound, or where call preparation between dials matters.
  • Parallel dialer: Calls multiple numbers simultaneously and routes the first live answer to the next available rep. Built for SDR teams doing high-volume cold calling where maximizing live conversations per hour is the primary goal.
  • Progressive dialer: Paces outbound calls based on agent availability, automatically dialing the next number only when a rep finishes their current call. Useful for teams that need consistent calling throughput without overwhelming individual reps.

Evaluation Criteria

Sales Workflow Fit

  • Dialing mode: Confirm the platform supports the dialing mode your team needs — power, parallel, or progressive. Some platforms offer multiple modes; verify that the mode you need is included in your pricing tier, not gated behind an upgrade.
  • Local presence and number management: Displaying a local area code to the prospect increases answer rates. Check whether local presence is included in the base plan or requires per-number add-on fees, and how number provisioning works across area codes.
  • Voicemail drop: Pre-recorded voicemails that deploy with a single click let reps move to the next call immediately instead of recording a live message each time. Verify that the platform supports multiple voicemail recordings for different campaigns or personas.
  • SMS capability and compliance: Some dialers include SMS for pre-call or post-call text touches. If SMS is part of your workflow, check for opt-in/opt-out management and message logging for compliance.

CRM and Data

  • Salesforce and HubSpot sync depth: Verify that calls, dispositions, notes, and recordings sync automatically to the correct contact and activity record. Some integrations only log basic call metadata without notes or outcomes.
  • Disposition and activity logging quality: Custom disposition fields, required disposition on call completion, and automatic activity creation in the CRM prevent data gaps that distort pipeline reporting.

Coaching and QA

  • Call recording and storage: Confirm that recordings are stored with sufficient retention, accessible by managers, and searchable by rep, date, or disposition. Check whether storage limits apply and what happens when limits are reached.
  • Coaching annotations: The ability for managers to bookmark, comment on, or tag specific moments in a call recording makes coaching scalable. Without this, managers must listen to full recordings to find teachable moments.
  • Analytics visibility: Rep-level and team-level dashboards showing calls made, connections, talk time, and conversion rates let managers identify coaching opportunities and track performance trends.

Ops and Compliance

  • Consent and opt-out controls: Call recording consent management that adapts to single-party and two-party consent jurisdictions, plus a do-not-call list integration that prevents dialing numbers on federal or state registries.
  • Role permissions: Admin, manager, and rep roles with appropriate access levels so reps see their own data, managers see their team, and admins manage platform configuration.

Common Failure Modes

  • Buying a parallel dialer for a small team: Parallel dialers need enough reps online simultaneously to handle multiple live connections. A team of two to three reps will pay for infrastructure designed for larger operations and underutilize it. Start with a power dialer until the team scales to a size where parallel capacity adds measurable value.
  • Ignoring consent management across jurisdictions: Call recording consent requirements vary by state and country. Selecting a dialer without consent tracking creates legal exposure that costs more to fix retroactively than to address during the buying process.
  • Choosing by call volume alone without checking CRM sync: A dialer that places hundreds of calls per day but fails to push dispositions and notes into your CRM creates a data gap that slows follow-up and distorts pipeline reporting.
  • Skipping the voicemail drop evaluation: Voicemail consumes a significant portion of outbound calling time. A dialer without automated voicemail drop wastes minutes per rep per hour that compound into hours of lost productivity per week.
  • Not testing call quality on actual networks: Audio quality, latency, and dropped call rates vary by provider and network conditions. A demo call on a conference room Wi-Fi connection does not replicate production calling conditions.

What to Validate in a 14-Day Pilot

  • Set up a live calling list of at least two hundred contacts and run calls for a full two-week cycle to account for callback patterns and follow-up timing.
  • Test CRM sync by verifying that call logs, dispositions, notes, and recordings appear on the correct contact record in Salesforce or HubSpot within the expected timeframe.
  • Have reps test voicemail drop on at least twenty calls to confirm audio quality and delivery timing.
  • If using local presence, verify that the correct area code is displayed for calls to different regions and that callback numbers route correctly.
  • Run a coaching workflow by having a manager review call recordings, add annotations, and confirm that the coaching features work with your team's existing review cadence.
  • Test role permissions by logging in as admin, manager, and rep to verify that each role sees only the data and controls appropriate to their access level.
  • Pull a performance report at the end of the pilot and verify that the data is complete, accurate, and exportable in a format compatible with your reporting workflow.
