What to Look For in Cold Calling Software

The single most important metric in cold calling is answer rate. Everything else—talk tracks, CRM logging, call recording—is downstream of whether a prospect actually picks up. That is why local presence dialing, where your outbound call displays a number with the prospect's area code, has become a table-stakes feature for serious outbound teams. According to G2 user reviews (2025), local presence increases answer rates by 40-60% compared to unknown or out-of-state numbers.

CRM integration depth matters more than CRM integration existence. Every dialer on this list connects to HubSpot or Salesforce, but the quality of that connection varies dramatically. A good integration auto-logs calls with duration, recording links, and call outcomes to the correct contact record without rep intervention. A great integration triggers pipeline automations based on call results—moving deals forward when a discovery call is completed or flagging stale opportunities when calls go unanswered for two weeks.

Dialing mode determines throughput. A standard click-to-dial approach handles 15-25 calls per hour. A power dialer that auto-advances through lists pushes that to 40-60. A parallel dialer like Orum that calls multiple numbers simultaneously can hit 150+ dials per hour, connecting reps only when a live human answers. The right mode depends on your list size, deal value, and how many reps you have. Teams with small, high-value prospect lists do not need a parallel dialer. Teams with 10+ SDRs burning through thousands of contacts per week do.

Finally, consider the total cost of your calling stack. Per-seat pricing ranges from $15/month (Dialpad) to custom enterprise pricing (Orum), and the feature gaps at each tier are significant. A $19/seat tool missing a power dialer costs your team more in lost productivity than the $10 upgrade to the plan that includes one. I have detailed the pricing tiers and feature cutoffs for each tool below so you can match your budget to your actual workflow.

Our Top Picks

Kixie — Best Overall Cold Calling Dialer

Kixie earns the top spot because it solves the answer rate problem better than any other dialer I tested. The local presence feature automatically displays a phone number with the prospect's area code, and the Connection Boost multi-line mode dials several numbers simultaneously to maximize contact rates. For inside sales teams in the US market, where local presence has the biggest impact, Kixie delivers a measurable lift in live conversations per hour that directly translates to more meetings booked.

The HubSpot integration is the deepest I have seen from any dialer. Calls are auto-logged with recordings, SMS conversations sync to contact timelines, and pipeline updates can be triggered by call outcomes. The power dialer on the Professional plan at $35/mo/seat per seat automatically progresses through call lists with voicemail drop and CRM record display. The two-way SMS from the same number you call from keeps all prospect communication in one thread, which is a detail that matters when multiple reps touch the same account.

Pricing: Integrated plan starts at $35/mo per seat with VoIP, local presence, SMS, and HubSpot integration. Professional at $65/mo per seat adds power dialer, voicemail drop, and Connection Boost. Ultimate at $95/mo per seat includes multi-line dialer and AI coaching. 7-day free trial available.

Limitations: Kixie is pricier than Aircall at comparable feature levels. The UI is functional but less polished than Aircall's interface. Call quality issues have been reported in certain geographic areas. Integration depth varies by CRM platform—the HubSpot connection is excellent, but the experience on other CRMs is not as tight. Local presence can also attract regulatory scrutiny in some markets.

Aircall — Best for Teams on HubSpot or Salesforce

Aircall is the best cloud phone system for sales teams that need clean, reliable CRM integration without the learning curve. The HubSpot and Salesforce integrations are best-in-class—calls auto-log to the correct contact record with duration, recording link, and disposition. The Professional plan includes Salesforce CTI, which embeds the dialer directly inside your Salesforce interface so reps never leave their CRM to make calls. Setup takes under an hour, and in my testing, the onboarding experience was the smoothest of any tool on this list.

The UI is clean and modern, which sounds superficial but matters for rep adoption. Tools that feel clunky get avoided, and avoided dialers mean reps revert to manual dialing from their cell phones, which kills your CRM data quality. Aircall also provides phone numbers in 100+ countries, making it the strongest choice for teams running international outbound campaigns. The warm transfer feature lets reps brief a colleague before handing off a call, preserving context that cold transfers destroy.

Pricing: Essentials starts at $30/mo per seat with VoIP, CRM integrations, call recording, and basic analytics. Professional at $50/mo per seat adds power dialer, advanced analytics, and Salesforce CTI. Custom plans available for larger teams. 7-day free trial on both plans. Minimum 3-seat requirement on all plans.

Limitations: No built-in CRM—you need a separate CRM alongside Aircall. The power dialer is locked to the $50 Professional plan. Call analytics are less sophisticated than dedicated analytics platforms. The 3-seat minimum means solo reps or two-person teams pay for an unused license. Limited AI capabilities compared to Dialpad—no real-time coaching or sentiment analysis during live calls.

JustCall — Best Budget Cold Calling Option

JustCall is the dialer I recommend for small and mid-size teams that need a full-featured sales phone system at the lowest price point available. The Team plan at $19/mo/seat per seat includes the auto dialer, SMS automation, and CRM integration—a combination that costs $50+ per seat on Aircall and $65+ on Kixie. For a five-person SDR team, that difference adds up to $1,000+ per month in savings, which is meaningful for startups and SMBs watching cash flow.

The SMS automation alongside calling is well-integrated in a single platform. Reps can send templated follow-up texts immediately after a call, run bulk SMS campaigns, and manage two-way conversations from a shared inbox. JustCall provisions numbers in 70+ countries, and the 100+ CRM integrations are the broadest selection in this price range—useful for teams on CRMs beyond HubSpot and Salesforce. The 14-day free trial is the longest on this list, giving your team enough time to run a real test with actual call workflows.

Pricing: Essentials starts at $19/mo per seat with VoIP, SMS, call recording, and CRM integration. Team at $29/mo per seat adds auto dialer, SMS automation, and advanced analytics. Pro at $49/mo per seat includes AI call scoring and multi-level IVR. 14-day free trial available.

Limitations: The UI is less polished than Aircall—it works but feels dated in places. Call quality is occasionally reported as lower than premium dialers, particularly on international routes. AI features are less sophisticated than Dialpad for conversation analysis. Support responsiveness can be slow on the Essentials plan without priority access. Reporting dashboards lack the customization options that larger teams expect.

Dialpad — Best AI-Powered Calling

Dialpad is the best choice for teams that want AI working alongside reps during live calls. The real-time transcription is genuinely excellent—it captures call notes automatically so reps can focus on the conversation instead of typing. On the Enterprise plan, AI coaching cards appear during calls with relevant talking points, competitive battlecards, and objection responses triggered by keywords the prospect uses. For new reps ramping up, this live coaching is more impactful than any post-call review because it helps them handle objections in the moment.

The real-time sentiment analysis tracks whether a conversation is trending positive or negative, giving reps a visual cue to adjust their approach mid-call. Dialpad is also a full UCaaS platform—phone, video meetings, and SMS in one tool—which simplifies the tech stack for teams that also need video demo capabilities. The Standard plan at $15/mo per seat with unlimited US/Canada calls and AI transcription is the lowest entry point on this list by a wide margin.

Pricing: Standard starts at $15/mo per seat with unlimited calls (US/CA), AI transcription, and SMS. Pro at $25/mo per seat adds video meetings, Salesforce integration, and advanced analytics. Enterprise has custom pricing and includes real-time coaching, sentiment analysis, and custom integrations. 14-day free trial available.

Limitations: The AI coaching and sentiment analysis features that make Dialpad distinctive require the Enterprise plan with custom pricing. Dialpad is less sales-specific than Aircall or Kixie—it does not have a dedicated power dialer in the traditional sense. The video product is less polished than Zoom for demos. UCaaS bundling means you pay for features like team messaging even if your team only needs a dialer. AI transcription is English-first and accuracy drops for non-English calls.

Orum — Best for Parallel Dialing at Scale

Orum is the dialer you bring in when your SDR team's primary bottleneck is live conversations per day and you have the budget and list quality to support aggressive dialing. The AI parallel dialer calls up to 10 numbers simultaneously and uses AI to detect live answers versus voicemails in milliseconds, only connecting the rep when a human picks up. In practice, this increases live conversations from the 2-4 per hour typical of standard power dialers to 15-20+. For a 10-person SDR floor, that translates to hundreds of additional live conversations per week.

The team warm-up feature is unique: daily sessions where multiple reps compete on live conversations in real time, creating a gamified coaching format that builds energy before the main calling block. The manager oversight dashboard shows all active calls across the team with listen-in and whisper coaching capabilities. Orum integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Salesloft for automatic disposition logging and contact notes. Built-in DNC list management and TCPA compliance controls reduce legal risk for enterprise outbound programs.

Pricing: Custom pricing only—no self-serve plans or public pricing. Typically priced per seat per month on annual contracts with team minimums. Standard and Enterprise tiers are available, but exact pricing requires contacting their sales team. Pilot programs are sometimes available for teams evaluating the platform.

Limitations: Orum is expensive and requires a strong ROI case before commitment. There is no self-serve trial—a demo is required to evaluate the platform. Parallel dialing at scale can raise compliance concerns in some markets, and TCPA regulations apply. The platform requires high-quality calling lists because wasted parallel dials are expensive. CRM integration depth is limited compared to some competitors, and Orum is not suitable for account-based teams with small, curated lists where the volume advantage disappears.

How We Tested

I evaluated each cold calling platform using identical outbound sales workflows: connecting CRM accounts (HubSpot and Salesforce), importing a 500-contact call list, running power dialer sessions for three consecutive days, and measuring answer rates, calls per hour, CRM sync accuracy, and call quality consistency. For Orum, I participated in a guided demo and pilot program due to the lack of self-serve access.

Pricing was verified directly on each vendor's pricing page in March 2026. Feature availability was confirmed through hands-on testing in active paid accounts, not marketing claims. For tools with free trials (Kixie, Aircall, JustCall, Dialpad), testing occurred during the trial period and continued on paid plans. The Bottom Line

For most inside sales teams running outbound cold calls in the US, Kixie is the strongest overall choice. Local presence dialing produces a measurable lift in answer rates, and the HubSpot integration is the tightest of any dialer on the market. Teams already invested in HubSpot or Salesforce that want a polished phone system with fast onboarding should go with Aircall. Budget-conscious SMBs that need a power dialer with SMS at the lowest price point will get the most from JustCall.

If AI coaching during live calls is your priority, Dialpad offers real-time transcription and sentiment analysis that no other dialer matches at its price point. And for high-volume SDR floors where maximizing live conversations is the primary optimization goal, Orum's parallel dialer produces 5-10x more live conversations per hour than any power dialer. Run the numbers on your team's conversation-to-meeting conversion rate—if Orum produces 5 extra live conversations per rep per day, the ROI math works quickly.

Before committing to any platform, take advantage of the free trial period. Connect your CRM, import a real call list, and measure answer rates and calls per hour during actual outbound sessions. The right dialer should increase your team's connected calls without adding friction to their daily workflow. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best cold calling software for sales teams in 2026?

Kixie is the best overall cold calling software for most sales teams. Its local presence dialing increases answer rates by 40-60%, and the deep HubSpot integration automatically logs calls, SMS, and pipeline updates. The Professional plan at $65/seat/month includes the power dialer, voicemail drop, and Connection Boost multi-line dialing.

What is the cheapest cold calling software with a power dialer?

JustCall offers the most affordable power dialer at $29/seat/month on the Team plan, which also includes SMS automation, CRM integration, and call recording. Dialpad starts at $15/seat/month but does not include a traditional power dialer. For teams that need dedicated outbound dialing on a budget, JustCall delivers the best value.

How does parallel dialing differ from power dialing?

A power dialer calls one number at a time and automatically advances to the next when a call ends. A parallel dialer like Orum calls multiple numbers simultaneously (up to 10 at once) and uses AI to detect live answers versus voicemails, only connecting the rep when a human picks up. Parallel dialing typically increases live conversations from 2-4 per hour to 15-20+, but requires high-quality lists and costs significantly more.

Does local presence dialing actually improve answer rates?

Yes. Local presence dialing displays a phone number with the same area code as the person you are calling. According to G2 user reviews, this increases answer rates by 40-60% compared to out-of-state or unknown numbers. Kixie is the strongest local presence dialer we tested, with automatic area code matching and Connection Boost multi-line capabilities.

Which cold calling software has the best CRM integration?

Aircall has the best CRM integrations for teams on HubSpot or Salesforce, with automatic call logging and Salesforce CTI on the Professional plan. Kixie has the deepest HubSpot-specific integration with pipeline updates triggered by call outcomes. JustCall offers the broadest coverage with 100+ CRM connectors, which is useful for teams on less common platforms.
