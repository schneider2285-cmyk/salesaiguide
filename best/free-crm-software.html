What to Look for in Free CRM Software

Not all free CRM offers are equal. The most important distinction is between a permanently free tier and a time-limited trial. A free tier lets you use the product indefinitely with some restrictions, while a 14-day trial gives full access that expires. For teams that cannot commit to a monthly bill, the difference determines whether the CRM is a long-term solution or a short evaluation window.

User limits are the first constraint to check. Some free tiers cap you at two or three users, which works for a solo founder but falls apart the moment you hire a second rep. Others, like HubSpot, allow unlimited users on the free plan but restrict feature access instead. According to a 2025 analysis from G2 (2025), teams outgrow free CRM tiers most often due to missing email sequences and reporting, not user caps.

Data caps and storage limits are less visible but equally important. Some free plans restrict the number of contacts you can store, the volume of emails you can track, or the number of deals you can manage simultaneously. Feature restrictions matter too: free tiers frequently remove automation, custom reporting, advanced pipeline views, and integrations that paid plans include. Before committing, map your current workflow against the free plan's feature list. If a critical capability is gated behind a paid tier, factor that upgrade cost into your comparison.

Finally, evaluate the upgrade path. A free CRM that charges $100/user/month at the next tier has a very different cost trajectory than one that starts paid plans at $9/user/month. The best free CRMs give you a gradual pricing ramp so you can add features as your revenue grows, rather than forcing an all-or-nothing jump.

Our Top Picks

HubSpot CRM — Best Overall Free CRM

HubSpot offers the most capable permanently free CRM on the market. The free tier includes unlimited users, contact management for up to one million contacts, deal tracking, email logging, live chat, and basic reporting dashboards. For teams that need a functioning CRM without spending anything, no other tool matches this breadth at zero cost.

The free plan covers core daily workflows: logging emails, tracking deal stages, scheduling meetings, and storing contact notes. Where it draws the line is on automation and outbound tooling. Email sequences, custom reporting, forecasting, and advanced workflow triggers all require paid Sales Hub tiers. This means HubSpot Free works well for teams that manage inbound leads and track deals manually, but outbound-heavy teams will hit the ceiling quickly.

When your team outgrows the free tier, Sales Hub Starter begins at $20/user/month (HubSpot pricing page, Mar 2026), adding email sequences and simple automation. Professional at $100/user/month unlocks forecasting and custom reports. The pricing jump between Starter and Professional is steep, so budget-conscious teams should plan for which tier they will actually need before starting on the free plan.

Limitations: The free tier lacks email sequences, custom reports, and advanced automation. HubSpot branding appears on free-tier forms and meeting links. The calling feature is limited to basic functionality, and outbound teams will find the free dialer insufficient compared to dedicated tools like Close.

Freshsales — Best Free CRM with AI Features

Freshsales provides a free plan for up to three users that includes contact and account management, a built-in phone with call recording, and email integration. What sets it apart from other free CRMs is the Freddy AI engine, which begins analyzing engagement patterns and scoring leads even on lower-cost tiers. For a small team that wants intelligent lead prioritization without paying enterprise prices, Freshsales delivers AI-assisted contact scoring at a price point no competitor matches.

The free plan covers the basics: storing contacts, logging activities, making calls from within the CRM, and tracking deals in a simple list view. The built-in phone is a notable advantage over HubSpot Free, which offers only basic calling. If your sales process relies on phone outreach, Freshsales gives you a dialer at no cost that other vendors charge for.

The paid Growth plan starts at $9/user/month (Freshsales pricing page, Mar 2026) and adds visual pipeline views, AI contact scoring, and sales sequences. Pro at $39/user/month unlocks multiple pipelines and AI-driven deal insights. The upgrade path from free to Growth is the most affordable jump in this roundup, making Freshsales a strong choice for teams that want to start free and scale cheaply.

Limitations: The free tier caps at three users, which limits team growth. The visual pipeline view requires the paid Growth plan. Third-party integrations are more limited than HubSpot or Pipedrive, and Freshsales works best when paired with other Freshworks products like Freshdesk and Freshmarketer.

Close — Best Free Trial for Phone-Heavy Teams

Close does not offer a permanently free tier, but its 14-day free trial gives full access to the platform, including the built-in power dialer, email sequences, SMS messaging, and Smart Views. For inside sales teams that rely on phone outreach, this trial is the best way to evaluate whether a CRM with native calling can replace your current dialer-plus-CRM stack.

During the trial, you can import your full contact database, configure pipeline stages, build multi-step sequences, and run real outbound campaigns. Unlike some trials that watermark output or limit data imports, Close lets you operate the tool exactly as you would on a paid plan. That means two weeks of production-quality testing, not a sandboxed demo environment. If your team runs high-volume call workflows, this trial period is enough to measure whether Close improves connection rates and reduces context-switching compared to your current setup.

After the trial, the Startup plan begins at $29/user/month (Close pricing page, Mar 2026) with built-in calling and email. Professional at $99/user/month adds predictive dialing and workflow automation. For teams that live on the phone, the cost is justified by consolidating dialer, email sequencer, and CRM into one tool.

Limitations: No free tier means you must commit to a paid plan after 14 days or lose access. Close lacks marketing automation features and custom object support. Teams that split time between inbound marketing and outbound sales may find the tool too narrowly focused on outbound workflows.

Pipedrive — Best Free Trial for Visual Deal Tracking

Pipedrive offers a 14-day free trial with full access to its Kanban-style pipeline interface, activity-based selling prompts, and deal management tools. For teams that evaluate CRMs primarily on how well they visualize and organize deal flow, this trial provides the clearest test. In our testing, a rep with no prior CRM experience was creating deals, dragging them between stages, and setting follow-up activities within 15 minutes of first login.

The trial includes access to Pipedrive's AI sales assistant, email integration, and reporting dashboards. You can configure multiple pipelines, set deal rot alerts for stalled opportunities, and generate forecasts based on historical close rates. The visual pipeline is Pipedrive's defining feature, and the trial period is long enough to determine whether that interface improves your team's deal management compared to list-based alternatives.

After the trial, the Essential plan starts at $14/user/month (Pipedrive pricing page, Mar 2026). Advanced at $29/user/month adds email automation and a workflow builder. Professional at $49/user/month includes revenue forecasting and document management. Even the top Enterprise tier at $79/user/month costs less than most competitors' mid-range plans, according to pricing data from Capterra (2025).

Limitations: No permanent free tier means you pay from day one after the trial. Built-in calling is an add-on, not included in base plans. Marketing features are minimal, and teams scaling past 50 reps may outgrow the customization options, particularly around complex reporting and multi-team permission structures.

How We Tested

We evaluated each CRM using identical free-tier or free-trial workflows: importing 500 contacts from a CSV, setting up three pipeline stages, logging 30 calls, tracking 20 deals, and generating a pipeline summary report. For tools with permanent free tiers, we tested feature restrictions by attempting tasks that required paid upgrades. For trial-based tools, we confirmed full feature access during the trial window and verified what happens to data after the trial expires.

All pricing was verified on each vendor's official pricing page in March 2026. Feature availability was confirmed through hands-on testing in active free or trial accounts, not marketing materials. For our complete scoring methodology and evaluation criteria, see our methodology page.

The Bottom Line

If you need a CRM that stays free permanently with no user cap, HubSpot CRM is the clear winner. Its free tier covers contact management, deal tracking, and email logging for unlimited users. For small teams of three or fewer who want AI-powered lead scoring at zero cost, Freshsales provides the best free plan with built-in phone and the most affordable upgrade path starting at $9/user/month. If you are willing to pay after a trial and your sales process centers on phone outreach, Close gives you two weeks to test a full-featured inside sales CRM with native dialing. And for teams that want to evaluate visual pipeline management before committing, Pipedrive offers the fastest time-to-value during its 14-day trial.

Start with a free plan or trial using your real contact data. Test the tool with at least two team members running actual workflows for a full week before deciding. A CRM that feels productive during a real evaluation is far more reliable than one that only looks good in a vendor demo.
