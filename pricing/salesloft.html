Salesloft Pricing 2026: Plans, Costs & Which Tier to Pick

Updated March 2026 • Pricing verified on official site • Last verified: March 2026
Our Methodology Editorial Policy How We Make Money Corrections

Salesloft runs $75-125 per user per month with annual contracts. Like most enterprise sales tools, there's no public pricing page and no free trial—you'll need to talk to their sales team.

But compared to Outreach, Salesloft is more approachable for mid-market teams. The entry point is lower, the implementation is faster, and the feature set has expanded significantly since the Vista Equity acquisition. Conversation intelligence, deal management, and forecasting are now baked into the higher tiers—features that used to require separate tools like Gong.

Budget for $75-125/user/month minimum. Negotiate multi-year deals for meaningful discounts. Here's exactly what each plan includes and which one fits your team.

Which Salesloft Plan Should You Pick?

Your plan choice depends on team size and whether you need conversation intelligence baked in or can get it elsewhere.

Solo rep or freelancer

Skip Salesloft. At $75+/user/month with annual commitment, it's overkill for one person. HubSpot's free CRM includes basic sequences that handle what a solo rep needs. If your motion is cold email, Instantly at $37/month is a fraction of the cost.

Small team (under 15 reps)

Still a stretch. HubSpot Sales Hub Starter gives you sequences, email tracking, and a CRM for much less. Salesloft's value kicks in when you need governance, team-level analytics, and structured cadence management—things that matter less with a small team where everyone can see what everyone else is doing.

Growth team (20-200 reps)

Essentials ($75/user/month) or Advanced ($125/user/month). This is Salesloft's sweet spot. Essentials gets you the core cadence engine, dialer, and Salesforce sync. Upgrade to Advanced if you want conversation intelligence built in instead of paying separately for Gong ($100-150/user/month). The math works: Advanced at $125 is cheaper than Essentials ($75) plus Gong ($100+).

Enterprise (200+ reps)

Premier. At this scale you need the dedicated CSM, custom integrations, and enterprise security controls. Negotiate hard—at 200+ seats you should be getting significant per-seat discounts. Push for multi-year pricing if your team is stable.

Hidden Costs & Watch-Outs

Salesloft's sticker price is real, but there are additional costs and friction points you should plan for.

Annual contracts with auto-renewal

Every Salesloft contract is annual. If you're scaling down or switching tools, you're still paying for unused seats until the contract ends. Watch for auto-renewal clauses—most require 60-90 days notice to cancel. Miss that window and you're locked in for another year.

CRM sync requires careful field mapping

The Salesforce integration is strong, but getting field mapping right takes real effort. Bad mapping means duplicate records, missing data, and reps who don't trust the tool. Budget 1-2 weeks of sales ops time for proper CRM configuration. HubSpot integration is available but less mature than the Salesforce connector.

Conversation intelligence has limits

The Advanced plan includes conversation intelligence, but it's not as deep as a dedicated tool like Gong. If call recording and analysis is a primary use case for your team—not just a nice-to-have—test Salesloft's conversation features against Gong before committing. For most teams, Salesloft's built-in version is good enough.

Admin training is non-trivial

Salesloft has a lot of configuration options: cadence rules, governance settings, analytics dashboards, integration mappings. Your sales ops person needs dedicated time to learn the admin side. Underinvest here and your reps will work around the tool instead of through it.

How Salesloft Compares on Price

Salesloft sits in the mid-to-upper range of the sales engagement market. Here's where it lands against direct competitors.

Salesloft vs Outreach

Outreach runs ~$100-150/user/month, making it 25-35% more expensive than Salesloft. Both require annual contracts and sales-led purchasing. The feature gap between the two has narrowed considerably—Salesloft's conversation intelligence and deal management close most of the distance. Unless you specifically need Outreach's advanced AI models or account-based plays, Salesloft is the better value. Read our full Outreach vs Salesloft comparison.

Salesloft vs Instantly

Instantly costs $37-97/month total (not per user), which makes it dramatically cheaper for pure cold email. But Instantly is a single-channel tool—no dialer, no conversation intelligence, no deal management. If your outbound motion is email-only, Instantly wins on price. If you need multi-channel sequences with phone, email, and LinkedIn, Salesloft is the right tool. See our Salesloft vs Instantly breakdown.

Salesloft vs HubSpot

HubSpot Sales Hub ranges from free to ~$450/month for teams. The free tier includes basic sequences and a CRM. For small teams, HubSpot is significantly cheaper and easier to set up. But HubSpot's sequences are basic compared to Salesloft's cadence engine, and HubSpot lacks a built-in dialer and conversation intelligence. At 20+ reps, Salesloft's structure and governance features start justifying the premium.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Salesloft cost?

Salesloft pricing is not publicly listed. Based on verified customer data, the Essentials plan costs approximately $75/user/month and the Advanced plan approximately $125/user/month. All contracts are annual. Premier pricing is custom and depends on team size, feature requirements, and contract length.

Does Salesloft have a free trial?

No. Salesloft does not offer a free trial or self-serve signup. You need to request a demo through their website, go through a sales process, and sign an annual contract. The process is faster than Outreach—typically 1-3 weeks from first contact to access.

Is Salesloft worth $75-125 per month?

For mid-market and enterprise teams with 20+ reps, yes. The cadence engine, built-in dialer, and Salesforce integration create measurable efficiency gains. The Advanced plan's conversation intelligence can replace a separate Gong subscription, saving money overall. For smaller teams, HubSpot or Instantly are better values. Read our full Salesloft review.

How does Salesloft pricing compare to Outreach?

Salesloft is roughly 25-35% cheaper. Essentials starts at ~$75/user/month vs Outreach Standard at ~$100/user/month. Both require annual contracts with no free trial. The feature gap has narrowed, making Salesloft the better value for most mid-market teams. See our full comparison.

What's included in Salesloft Essentials?

Salesloft Essentials (~$75/user/month) includes core cadences, email sequences, a built-in dialer, Salesforce integration, activity tracking, and basic analytics. It does not include conversation intelligence, deal management, advanced analytics, or pipeline forecasting—those require the Advanced plan at ~$125/user/month.
