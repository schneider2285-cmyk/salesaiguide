Outreach Pricing 2026: Plans, Costs & What You'll Actually Pay

Updated March 2026 • Pricing verified on official site • Last verified: March 2026
Our Methodology Editorial Policy How We Make Money Corrections

Outreach doesn't publish prices. You won't find a pricing page on their site—just a "Request a Demo" button. But after talking to dozens of teams that use it and verifying with current customers, here's what you'll actually pay: $100-150 per user per month on annual contracts.

There's no free trial, no self-serve signup, and no monthly billing option. You're going through their sales team, negotiating a contract, and committing to a 4-8 week implementation before a single rep sends an email. This is enterprise tooling priced accordingly.

Outreach commands premium pricing vs. Salesloft. That premium is only justified for organizations with complex enterprise outbound motions—think 30+ reps running multi-channel sequences across multiple buyer personas. If that's not you, save your money.

Which Outreach Plan Should You Pick?

Here's my honest take on who should and shouldn't be using Outreach at all, based on team size and use case.

Solo rep or freelancer

Skip Outreach entirely. At $100+/user/month with annual commitment and multi-week implementation, it makes zero sense for individuals. Use Instantly ($37/month) for cold email or HubSpot's free CRM with sequences if you need multi-channel.

Small team (under 15 reps)

Skip Outreach. The implementation overhead alone will cost you weeks of selling time. Salesloft at ~$75/user/month or Instantly at $37-97/month gives you 80% of the functionality at half the cost. Outreach's enterprise features won't benefit a team this size.

Growth team (30+ reps)

Standard plan (~$100/user/month). This is where Outreach starts making sense. At 30+ reps, the sequence governance, built-in dialer, and Salesforce integration pay for themselves through consistency and reduced admin overhead. You get real multi-channel orchestration that's hard to replicate with cheaper tools.

Enterprise team (100+ reps)

Professional or Enterprise plan. At this scale, the AI-powered insights and account-based plays actually produce measurable ROI. The advanced sequence branching lets you build complex, persona-specific outbound motions that smaller teams don't need. Negotiate hard on price—at 100+ seats, you have use.

Hidden Costs & Watch-Outs

The per-seat price is just the beginning. Here are the costs that blindside teams after they sign.

Implementation and professional services

Outreach implementations take 4-8 weeks and often require professional services, which can run $10,000-25,000+ depending on complexity. CRM field mapping, sequence migration, and admin training all take time. Budget for at least one full-time person managing the rollout for a month.

Sequence governance can block reps

Outreach's governance features are a double-edged sword. Admins can lock down sequences, templates, and sending limits. That's great for compliance, but I've seen it paralyze sales teams when the admin is slow to approve new sequences or the rules are too restrictive. Plan for ongoing admin overhead.

Annual lock-in with no easy exit

Annual contracts mean you're paying for 12 months whether you use it or not. If half your team churns six months in, you're still paying for those seats. Some contracts include auto-renewal clauses that extend the term if you don't cancel 60-90 days before expiration. Read the fine print.

Reporting requires admin access

The reporting in Outreach is powerful but gated behind admin roles. Individual reps get basic activity metrics, but pipeline attribution, sequence performance analysis, and team-level dashboards require admin permissions. If your sales ops team is stretched thin, reports won't get built.

Add-on costs for advanced features

Some features that feel like they should be included—like advanced AI modeling, custom triggers, and certain integrations—may require an upgraded plan or add-on licensing. Ask specifically what's included in your quote before signing.

How Outreach Compares on Price

Outreach sits at the top of the sales engagement market on pricing. Here's how it stacks up.

Outreach vs Salesloft

Salesloft runs ~$75-125/user/month, making it roughly 25-35% cheaper than Outreach. Both require annual contracts and sales-led purchasing. Salesloft is more approachable for mid-market teams and has a slightly faster implementation timeline. The feature gap has narrowed significantly—Salesloft's conversation intelligence and deal management close much of the distance. Read our full Outreach vs Salesloft comparison.

Outreach vs Instantly

Instantly costs $37-97/month total (not per user). If your motion is primarily cold email, Instantly is 10-20x cheaper and you can be sending within hours instead of weeks. Outreach's advantage is multi-channel orchestration, built-in calling, and enterprise-grade governance. But for pure outbound email, Instantly wins on value by a mile.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Outreach cost?

Outreach pricing is not published publicly. Based on verified customer data, the Standard plan runs approximately $100/user/month and the Professional plan approximately $150/user/month. All contracts are annual. Enterprise pricing is fully custom and negotiable based on seat count and contract length.

Does Outreach have a free trial?

No. Outreach does not offer a free trial or self-serve signup. You must request a demo, go through their sales process, and sign an annual contract before accessing the platform. From first contact to live access, expect 2-4 weeks minimum.

Is Outreach worth the price?

For enterprise teams with 30+ reps running complex multi-channel outbound, yes. The governance, analytics, and Salesforce integration justify the premium. For smaller teams or teams focused mainly on cold email, cheaper tools like Instantly or Salesloft deliver better ROI. Read our full Outreach review for details.

How does Outreach pricing compare to Salesloft?

Outreach is approximately 25-35% more expensive than Salesloft. Outreach starts around $100/user/month vs Salesloft at ~$75/user/month. Both require annual contracts. Salesloft has closed much of the feature gap, so the Outreach premium is harder to justify unless you specifically need its advanced AI models or account-based plays. See our full comparison.

What's the minimum team size for Outreach?

Outreach typically requires a minimum of 10-15 seats, though this can vary. They are built for mid-market and enterprise sales organizations. If you have fewer than 10 reps, you'll get faster onboarding and better per-dollar value from Salesloft, HubSpot Sales Hub, or Instantly.
