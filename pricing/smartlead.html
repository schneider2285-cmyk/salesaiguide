Hidden Costs and Gotchas

Smartlead's pricing looks straightforward, but a few things trip people up.

Active Lead Limits on Basic

The 2,000 active lead cap on Basic is the real constraint, not the email limit. "Active leads" means contacts currently in a running campaign. Once a campaign ends, those leads free up. But if you're running multiple overlapping campaigns, you hit that ceiling fast. I've seen teams upgrade to Pro within 30 days purely because of this limit.

White-Label Locked to Pro

If you're an agency, don't bother with Basic. White-label workspaces — the feature that lets you present Smartlead as your own platform to clients — is only available on Pro. There's no add-on option. You either pay $94/month or you're sending clients screenshots of a tool with someone else's branding.

UI Has a Learning Curve

This isn't a direct cost, but it's a time cost. Smartlead's interface isn't as polished as Instantly. Setting up your first campaign takes longer than it should, and the analytics dashboard requires some digging to find the metrics that matter. Budget an extra day of setup time compared to competing tools.

Instantly and Smartlead are nearly identical on price at the entry level. The difference is in what you get at the mid-tier. Instantly's Hypergrowth plan ($97/mo) focuses on raw sending volume. Smartlead's Pro ($94/mo) focuses on agency infrastructure. If you're running campaigns for clients, Smartlead Pro is the better deal. If you're sending for yourself and want the cleanest UX, Instantly wins.

Lemlist charges per seat ($39–99/seat), which gets expensive with team growth. Smartlead's flat-rate pricing is more predictable for agencies managing multiple campaigns.

Is Smartlead Worth It?

At $39/month for unlimited mailboxes and solid deliverability infrastructure, Smartlead is one of the better values in cold outreach tooling. The Basic plan works for anyone doing focused, low-volume outreach. The real question is whether the Pro plan at $94/month is worth the jump.

For agencies: yes, without question. White-label workspaces and dedicated IP warmup are table-stakes features for managing client campaigns professionally. For solo senders who just need to send cold email, the Basic plan is fine — and if the UI bothers you, Instantly is two dollars cheaper with a better interface.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Smartlead cost?

Smartlead has two fixed plans: Basic at $39/month (2,000 active leads, 6,000 emails, unlimited mailboxes) and Pro at $94/month (30,000 active leads, 150,000 emails, white-label workspaces, dedicated IP warmup). Custom plans are available for enterprise teams that need unlimited volume and SLA guarantees.

Does Smartlead have a free trial?

Yes, Smartlead offers a 14-day free trial. You get full access to test the platform, set up mailboxes, and run initial campaigns. I'd recommend using the trial to test deliverability with your specific domain setup before committing.

Is Smartlead worth it for agencies?

The $94/month Pro plan is built specifically for agencies. White-label workspaces let you manage client campaigns under your own branding, and dedicated IP warmup protects deliverability across all your accounts. If you're managing 3+ client campaigns, the Pro plan pays for itself by replacing multiple single-user subscriptions.

How does Smartlead compare to Instantly on price?

They're nearly identical at the entry level: Smartlead Basic is $39/month vs. Instantly Growth at $37/month. Both offer unlimited mailboxes. At the mid-tier, Smartlead Pro ($94/mo) adds agency features while Instantly Hypergrowth ($97/mo) adds more sending volume. Choose Smartlead for agency work, Instantly for simpler use cases.

What's included in the Smartlead Pro plan?

Smartlead Pro ($94/month) includes everything in Basic plus: 30,000 active leads (up from 2,000), 150,000 emails per month (up from 6,000), white-label workspaces for agency branding, dedicated IP warmup, and advanced analytics. It's the plan Smartlead clearly designed for cold outreach agencies.
