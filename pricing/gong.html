What Does Gong Actually Cost?

Gong doesn't publish pricing. That's intentional — they want you talking to their sales team before you see a number. Based on conversations with current customers and data from multiple sources, expect to pay $100 to $150 per user per month with mandatory annual contracts.

There's no free trial. There's no self-serve signup. You're booking a demo, sitting through a discovery call, and negotiating a contract before you ever touch the product. Plan on 4-6 weeks from first demo to deployment.

For a team of 30 reps (close to Gong's practical minimum), that's $3,000-4,500/month or $36,000-54,000/year. That's real budget. If you're a smaller team or an early-stage startup, Gong is not built for you — and honestly, they don't want your business at that price point.

Gong Plans & Pricing Breakdown

Mid-Market — ~$100-150/user/month

This is what most Gong customers pay. You get the core conversation intelligence platform: call recording, transcription, deal intelligence, pipeline forecasting, and standard coaching analytics. Salesforce and HubSpot integrations are included.

The deal intelligence features are where Gong earns its price. Automatic deal risk scoring, next-step recommendations, and pipeline analytics give sales managers visibility they can't get from CRM data alone. If your reps are running 20+ calls per week, the ROI math starts to work.

  • Conversation recording and AI transcription
  • Deal intelligence and risk scoring
  • Pipeline forecasting
  • Standard coaching analytics
  • Salesforce and HubSpot integration
  • Email and web conferencing capture
  • Team performance dashboards

Enterprise — Custom Pricing

Enterprise pricing is fully custom and negotiated based on seat count, modules, and contract length. You get everything in the Mid-Market package plus advanced competitive intelligence, custom analytics dashboards, a dedicated customer success manager, and SLA guarantees.

At the Enterprise tier, Gong also unlocks advanced API access, custom integrations, and more granular admin controls. If you're running 100+ reps across multiple teams, this is the tier where Gong assigns real resources to your account.

  • Everything in Mid-Market
  • Advanced competitive intelligence
  • Custom analytics and reporting dashboards
  • Dedicated customer success manager
  • SLA guarantees and priority support
  • Advanced API access and custom integrations
  • Enterprise-grade security and compliance

Which Gong Plan Should You Pick?

Solo rep or tiny team (1-10 reps)

Skip Gong entirely. Use Fireflies.ai ($10-19/user/month) for meeting recording and transcription. You'll get 80% of Gong's core functionality at a fraction of the cost. Gong's minimum seat requirements and annual contracts don't make sense at this scale.

Small team (10-30 reps)

Probably still skip Gong. At 15-30 seats, you're looking at $1,500-4,500/month. Unless your average deal size justifies that spend, consider Chorus (lighter, cheaper) or Fireflies.ai + your CRM's built-in forecasting. The ROI math only works if you're closing deals worth $20K+ and losing winnable deals due to poor coaching.

Growth team (30-50 reps)

Mid-Market plan. This is where Gong starts to make financial sense. At 30+ reps doing high-volume calls, the coaching analytics and deal intelligence features pay for themselves if they help you close even 2-3 additional deals per quarter. Budget $100-150/user/month and negotiate hard on the annual contract.

Enterprise (50+ reps)

Enterprise plan, custom-negotiated. At this scale, push for volume discounts, extended payment terms, and included add-on modules. Gong's list pricing has significant room for negotiation at 50+ seats. I've heard of organizations getting 25-35% below initial quotes by involving procurement early.

Hidden Costs & What Gong Doesn't Tell You

Minimum seat requirements

Gong typically requires 15+ seats minimum. Some packages start at 20-25 seats. You can't buy 5 licenses and test it with a small team — they want full department commitments. This means your minimum annual spend is roughly $18,000-27,000 even at the low end.

Add-on modules for forecasting and deal boards

Revenue intelligence features — the forecasting dashboards, deal boards, and pipeline analytics that Gong heavily markets — are often add-on modules priced separately from the base conversation intelligence platform. Ask explicitly what's included in your quoted price. I've talked to customers who assumed forecasting was included and discovered it cost an additional 20-30% on top of their per-user price.

Implementation takes 4-6 weeks

Gong isn't a tool you sign up for on Monday and use on Tuesday. Expect 4-6 weeks for full deployment, including CRM integration, call platform setup, team onboarding, and analytics configuration. During that time, you're paying but not getting full value. Factor implementation costs — both hard dollars and lost productivity — into your ROI calculations.

Annual lock-in with no early exit

All Gong contracts are annual. There's no monthly billing option and, from what I've heard, no early termination clause in standard contracts. If Gong doesn't deliver the value you expected, you're paying for the full year regardless. Negotiate a 90-day out clause or a pilot period if possible — though Gong's sales team will push back hard on this.

Two-party consent requirements

Gong records calls, which means you need to comply with two-party consent laws in states like California, Illinois, and Florida (plus various international regulations). Your legal team needs to review consent workflows before deployment. This isn't a hidden cost in dollars, but it's a hidden cost in time and compliance effort that most teams underestimate.

Gong Pricing vs. Competitors

Gong vs. Fireflies.ai

Fireflies.ai charges $10-19/user/month — roughly 85-90% cheaper than Gong. Fireflies handles meeting recording, transcription, and basic analytics well. It doesn't have Gong's deal intelligence, pipeline forecasting, or advanced coaching features. For teams that primarily need "record my calls and make them searchable," Fireflies is the right choice. For teams that need to analyze patterns across thousands of calls and coach reps based on data, Gong is in a different class.

Gong vs. Chorus

Chorus (owned by ZoomInfo) runs approximately $70-100/user/month — 20-40% cheaper than Gong. If you already pay for ZoomInfo, Chorus may be bundled into your contract at a steep discount or included entirely. Chorus handles conversation intelligence well but lacks Gong's depth in deal analytics and competitive intelligence. For teams already in the ZoomInfo ecosystem, Chorus is the pragmatic choice.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Gong cost?

Gong costs approximately $100-150 per user per month based on data from current customers. Gong doesn't publish pricing publicly. You'll need to book a demo and go through their sales process to get an exact quote for your team. Annual contracts are required, and there are typically minimum seat requirements of 15+ users.

Does Gong have a free trial?

No. Gong does not offer a free trial, free tier, or self-serve signup. The only way to access the product is through their sales team. Expect 4-6 weeks from initial demo request to product access. If you want to test conversation intelligence before committing to Gong's price point, try Fireflies.ai — they have a free tier.

Is Gong worth the price?

Gong is worth it for mid-market and enterprise teams with 30+ sales reps running high-volume calls on deals worth $20K+. The deal intelligence and coaching analytics provide value you can't replicate with cheaper tools. For smaller teams, startups, or teams primarily needing meeting transcription, Gong is overpriced — Fireflies.ai or Chorus will serve you better at a fraction of the cost.

What's the minimum team size for Gong?
